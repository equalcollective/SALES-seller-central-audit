# ASIN Selection: Sapo All Natural

**Data window:** Jan 2026 - Mar 2026 (Mar partial through 3/22)
**Ad data available from:** Jan 2, 2026 (88 days)
**Note:** No ad data exists before Jan 2026. The seller was running purely organic prior to that.

## Catalog Overview

8 parent ASINs, all single-child (no variations). Natural skincare brand: cleansers, creams, serums, and one body lotion. Account-level 3-month sales: ~$7,102 on ~$644 ad spend.

**Important: Cleanser Merge.** Cucumber Face Cleanser (B071QXBDWR) and Calendula Face Cleanser (B06ZXSL6DY) are the same product in different scents. They are treated as one product for prioritization purposes.

Calendula Cleanser annual context: Steady at $350-580/mo over the year. CVR historically 12-23%, dropped to 8-12% since ads started in Jan 2026. Sessions spiked to 542 in Jan (from ~300 avg) when ads launched, then settled back to ~345.

- **Cucumber is in a clear decline.** Sales down 55% from April 2025 peak ($2,498) to March 2026 ($1,119). Sessions fell from 751 to 386 over the same period. This is a traffic problem: fewer people are finding the product.
- **CVR dropped when ads started** but remains strong. Pre-ads CVR was consistently 31-38%. Since Jan 2026 it's 25-34%. This is expected: paid traffic converts lower than organic. The product still converts well when people land on it.
- **The brand was 100% organic until Jan 2026.** Every dollar of revenue before that was organic. Ads are new, and the current ad setup (ROAS 1.77 combined across cleansers) is not yet profitable enough to offset the organic traffic decline.

## Insights

- **Capital misallocation on Calendula Cleanser.** Calendula is getting $96/mo in ad spend but generating only $30-$120/mo in ad sales (ROAS 0.34-1.25). Meanwhile, Cucumber Cleanser has better ROAS (3.44-3.53 in Jan-Feb) on less spend. The Calendula's 10% CVR vs. Cucumber's 29% CVR suggests the ad budget would produce better returns if shifted toward Cucumber.
- **Milk Tea Body Lotion is a money pit.** $56 in ad spend over 3 months with 0.36 ROAS and sub-2% CVR. This product is not converting from either organic or paid traffic. Ad spend here should be paused entirely.
- **Bakuchiol Face Serum is the one growth signal.** Revenue tripled from $40 (Jan) to $120 (Mar), though from a small base. It's heavily ad-dependent (59% ad sales) but ROAS is strong at 5.42. Worth monitoring as a potential scale candidate.

## Things to Investigate Further

- **Why is Cucumber Cleanser traffic declining?** Sessions dropped from 751 (Apr 2025) to 386 (Mar 2026). Need to check SQP data to see if this is a market-wide search volume decline or a loss of ranking/impression share on key queries.
- **Calendula Cleanser CVR gap.** If these are truly the same product in different scents, why does Calendula convert at 10% vs. Cucumber's 29%? Could be listing quality, reviews/ratings, price, or the ad traffic being sent to irrelevant queries. Product Understanding and SQP will help clarify.
- **Hydrating Night Cream buy box at 93%.** This is the lowest buy box in the catalog. For a private label brand with likely no competing sellers, this could indicate price change issues or inventory gaps.

## Questions for the Seller

- **What prompted starting ads in January 2026?** The brand was fully organic for at least 18 months prior. Was this a response to declining organic traffic, or a proactive growth decision?
- **Is the Milk Tea Body Lotion a product they want to continue selling?** It gets decent traffic (143 sessions/mo) but almost nobody buys it. Is there a known issue with the product, pricing, or listing that explains the sub-2% CVR?
