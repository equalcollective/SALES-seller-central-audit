# Product Understanding - P0: Mole Scissor Trap (YARDDOG)

## Product Profile

**Product:**
- Scissor-style mole trap with a step-to-set mechanism. No digging required. Place over a mole tunnel, step on the foot pedal to arm, and the scissor jaws close when triggered by mole activity underground.
- Commercial-grade galvanized/coated steel construction. Compact at 6"L x 6"W x 13"H. Reusable across multiple seasons.
- Solves the problem of mole damage to lawns and gardens without chemicals, professional services, or complex setup. The step-to-set design is the primary differentiator, making it accessible to homeowners who aren't experienced trappers.
- Core purchase motivation: frustration with mole damage + desire for a DIY solution that works without requiring skill or effort.

**Customer:**
- Homeowners with lawns or gardens experiencing active mole damage. Skews toward suburban/rural property owners who maintain their own yards.
- The purchase is problem-driven and usually urgent ("moles are destroying my lawn right now"). This explains the strong seasonal pattern (moles are most active and most visible in spring/summer).

**Brand:**
- YARDDOG is a legitimate registered trademark brand, not white-label. Founded around 2021, inspired by the founder's dog Daisy.
- Established multi-channel presence: Amazon, Home Depot, Tractor Supply, Walmart, Menards. This is notably broad distribution for a brand at this revenue level and is a strong credibility signal.
- Website: yarddogproducts.com. Professional but basic. Social media presence is limited.
- Products are backed by a lifetime guarantee, which is a competitive differentiator most Chinese-manufactured competitors don't offer.
- **Brand vibe:** Rugged, practical, working-class Americana. The brand story (named after a farm dog, "here to protect your yard") targets a homeowner who values no-nonsense, durable tools. Not premium or polished, more "reliable neighbor" energy.

**Opportunities:**

Competitors show this in their listings.
