- The brand operated with zero ad spend from at least April 2025 through December 2025, generating $7-10K/month purely organically. This is a strong organic baseline.
- Sessions spiked in Q4 (Oct: 6,221, likely holiday/travel season traffic), then remained elevated into January 2026 (5,800). February and March dropped back to ~4,200 level, below even the pre-Q4 baseline of ~4,000.
- The seller started advertising in January 2026, spent ~$1,100, then $921 in February, then dramatically pulled back to $217 in March before stopping entirely around March 21. The ads had poor ROAS (~2.0) and high ACoS (~47-50%). This looks like a failed first attempt at advertising.

## Insights

- This is a brand with proven organic demand (~$8K/mo for over a year with zero ads). The product sells itself, meaning there is a solid product-market fit. The opportunity is not "can we make this product sell" but "can we scale a product that already sells."
- The seller's first attempt at advertising (Jan-Mar 2026) produced poor returns (2.0 ROAS, ~47% ACoS) and was abandoned. This suggests the campaigns were not well-structured or targeted, not that the product doesn't respond to ads.

## Things to Investigate Further

- Why does the Women's Plus variant (B0BRBX5NM8) have 3.3% CVR vs 6.3% for the standard 12-pack despite getting significant sessions (2,330 in 3 months)? Check in SQP/Product Understanding whether this is a listing quality issue or a mismatch with the traffic it's receiving.
- The seller pulled ad spend from $1,100/mo to near zero within 2 months. Check the ad campaigns to understand what went wrong, specifically whether the spend was allocated to the right keywords.

## Questions for the Seller

- You started running ads in January 2026 and stopped by mid-March. Was this pulled due to poor ROAS, budget constraints, or a deliberate strategy shift? Understanding your ad experience will help us design a better campaign structure.
