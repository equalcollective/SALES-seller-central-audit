- P0 shows a clear seasonal pattern: summer trough (Jun-Jul), fall/winter peak (Oct-Dec), January dip, then recovery. This aligns with radon testing behavior, as homeowners test when windows are closed during heating season. Need to verify with SQP search volume in Step 3.

- CVR fluctuates between 4.6% and 9.5% across the year. The swings are significant for a product at this price point and likely tied to traffic quality shifts (organic vs. ad-driven, seasonal intent strength).

## Insights

- **TACoS is exceptionally efficient at 3.5%.** The brand is overwhelmingly organic (80%+ of sales), with PPC acting as a small incremental driver. There is significant room to scale ad spend if the unit economics hold.
- **P1 (Continuous Radon Monitor) has zero ad spend and zero sales in two of the last six months** (Oct 2025, Jan 2026) despite receiving sessions. At an ~$895 price point with 3.4% CVR, this product has structural challenges. It is not a priority for investment.

## Things to Investigate Further

- P0's March spike (sessions +143% WoW in the week of 3/22) is unusually sharp even for seasonal demand. Need to check if this was driven by an external event (media coverage, EPA advisory, viral content) or a sustained organic trend.
- P1 recorded $0 sales in Oct 2025 and Jan 2026 despite 148 and 52 sessions respectively. Buy box was 97% and 94%. Worth checking if there were stockouts or listing issues.

## Questions for the Seller

- P1 (Continuous Radon Monitor) has no ad spend and very sporadic sales (0-5 units/month). Is this product being phased out, or is there a reason it's not being marketed? At $895, it may serve a different customer segment (professional vs. consumer).
