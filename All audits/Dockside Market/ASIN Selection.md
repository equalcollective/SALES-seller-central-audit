## The Defining Pattern: This Is a Christmas-Gift Brand

| Month | Total Account Sales |
|-------|--------------------|
| Typical month (2025-26) | $600-900 |
| **December 2025** | **$6,996** |

December alone is roughly **40% of the ~$16.5k annual revenue**. The December engine is the **Tropical Tower gift (P2)**, which did **$3,380 (52 units)** that month and then collapses to ~1 unit/month the rest of the year. Bundt, Cookies, and Mini Sampler each also roughly tripled in December. This is a holiday gifting business with a long quiet tail.

- **Most consistent line in the catalog.** Bundt sells $180-510/month year-round with a December lift, so it is less holiday-dependent than the gift bundles. This is why it is the hero: it is the product you can actually build a year-round growth plan around.
- **Sessions eroded over the year (234 → 140) while CVR held or improved.** This is a traffic problem, not a conversion problem. Organic visibility is shrinking with zero ad support to backfill it.
- **Sharp post-holiday collapse** (Dec $1,410 → Jan $120). Normal demand drop after the gifting season, consistent with the brand-wide pattern.

## Insights

- **The brand is overwhelmingly Christmas-seasonal.** ~40% of annual revenue lands in December, led by P2 (Tropical Tower gift) at $3,380 in that month. The off-season run-rate is ~$600-900/month across the whole catalog.
- **Zero advertising across the entire catalog and the full history window.** Revenue is 100% organic. For a gift brand with a hard Q4 peak, this is the largest single untapped lever, especially in the Oct-Dec ramp.
- **P3 (Mini Sampler Cakes) has a severe traffic-to-conversion breakdown.** March 2026: 2,308 sessions → 6 units (0.26% CVR). It absorbed ~88% of all account sessions that month while generating $204. Either the sessions are inflated/irrelevant or the listing is badly broken. This must be understood before any spend goes near it.

## Things to Investigate

- **P0 (Bundt Cake) session erosion** (234 → 140 over 12 months). Is organic rank slipping on its core keywords? Verify in Step 3 (SQP share trend).
- **Parent buy box ~60-67% on P0 (Bundt Cake) for most of the year.** At parent level this is diluted across 9 children, so confirm at child level. The account snapshot already flags one child, P3 (Mini Sampler "Tropical pack of 6"), at 46.8% buy box. For a private-label brand with no competing sellers, sub-70% buy box usually points to price/MAP swings, not competition.
- **P3 (Mini Sampler) session anomaly** - confirm whether the 2,000+ session months are real demand or a data/traffic artifact.

## Questions for the Seller

- **Is Dockside intended to be a year-round brand or primarily a Q4 holiday-gift play?** December is ~40% of revenue and the rest of the year is thin. This single answer reshapes the entire strategy: a year-round build (grow Bundt/Cookies as everyday gourmet gifts) versus a seasonal maximization play (pour everything into the Sep-Dec gifting ramp around the Tropical Tower).
