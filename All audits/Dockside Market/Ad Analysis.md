# Step 4: Ad Analysis → Ad-Launch Strategy - Dockside Market

## The Account Has Never Advertised

Verified three ways: Seller Analytics shows `ads_day_count = 0` (no advertised-product data ever), and the full multi-year Metabase ad history (Q698 monthly report and Q699 campaign report, 2023 to 2026) returns **zero rows**. Dockside Market's revenue is **100% organic**.

There is no campaign structure, auto/manual split, profitability, or targeting analysis to run. Instead, this step lays out the launch strategy, because the entire paid channel is greenfield. That is the headline opportunity: a credentialed heritage brand (NYT "best key lime cake") that has never spent a dollar telling Amazon which customers to put it in front of.

This strategy flows directly from the Step 3 blockers:
- **Primary blocker: impression share.** The brand shows on a fraction of a percent of relevant searches. With no ads, there has been no lever to fix this. Launching ads is the single most direct fix.
- **Secondary blocker: PDP conversion (ATC/CVR).** Outside the `key lime cake` term, the barebones listing converts at less than half industry. This is a pay-per-click model: paying to send traffic to a weak PDP burns money. So the listing fix from Step 2 sequences **before** broad PPC scaling.

## Sequencing Principle

Because CTR is healthy (Step 3) but PDP conversion is weak outside the signature term, the launch is staged:

1. **Start where the brand already converts** (key lime + branded). Low risk, immediate.
2. **Fix the listing** (bullets, A+, video, from Step 2) in parallel.
3. **Then scale** into the broader flavored-cake and bundt-cake terms once the PDP can close them.
4. **Overlay seasonality:** ramp hard into the Sep-Dec gifting peak (Step 1 + Step 3 both confirm December demand roughly doubles).

## Recommended Launch Architecture

| Priority | Campaign | Type | Target terms | Why now / gate |
|----------|----------|------|--------------|----------------|
| 1 | **Branded defense** | SP Exact, small budget (2-5%) | dockside market, dockside cakes, dockside key lime cake | Protective. Competitors can bid on the NYT-credentialed brand name. Cheap to own. Do **not** scale beyond defense. |
| 2 | **Key Lime hero** | SP Exact + Top-of-Search modifier | key lime cake, key lime bundt cake | Launch immediately. Dockside already holds ~4% impression share here and converts **above** industry (Dec 16% vs 5.7%). Pure scale of a proven winner. |
| 3 | **Bundt cake capture** | SP Exact/Phrase | bundt cake, mini bundt cake | Launch immediately for visibility. ~3-5k searches/mo where the brand is invisible (0.65% share) despite selling exactly this. Healthy CTR. Monitor CVR; pair with adding "bundt cake" to the title/backend. |
| 4 | **Flavored cakes scale** | SP Exact + Auto discovery | coconut cake, lemon cake, orange cake, rum cake | **Gate behind the listing fix.** Big market (~$124k/mo) but the brand converts below industry here today. Turn on after A+/bullets ship, or CVR-weak traffic will just burn budget. |
| 5 | **Gift-occasion (seasonal)** | SP Phrase + SD | florida gift basket, florida food gifts, florida citrus gift baskets | Small volume, high relevance, converts well. Ramp Sep-Dec around the Tropical Tower gift bundle. On-brand and seasonal. |
| 6 | **Conquest (optional)** | SP Product Targeting / Exact | nothing bundt cakes, competitor ASINs | Adjacent bundt demand. Low intrinsic conversion (navigational), so keep small and watch ACoS. |

**Branded keyword note:** branded defense is protective, not a growth lever. Keep it under ~5% of budget and never scale it. New customers come from the non-branded terms above.

## Seasonal Overlay

December demand roughly doubles (Tier 1 search volume ~48k vs ~25k typical; the gift terms peak Dec-Jan). The launch calendar should treat **September-December as the budget peak**, front-loading the gift-occasion and Tropical Tower campaigns ahead of the holiday, not reacting once December arrives. The rest of the year is a lean "always-on" presence on the hero (key lime) and bundt-cake terms.

## Expectations & Guardrails

- **Start small and prove it.** The base is tiny (~$16.5k/yr, ~$30 AOV). This is a controlled launch, not an aggressive scale. A modest starting budget on campaigns 1-3, expanding into 4-6 as the listing fix lands and the hero/bundt campaigns prove out.
- **Break-even floor:** without COGS data, use the standard 1.5x ROAS floor to judge profitability. Perishable food with real shipping cost means margin confirmation from the seller matters before scaling spend (see Questions).
- **The listing gate is real.** Do not turn on campaign 4 (the big flavored-cake market) until the PDP can convert it. Otherwise the impression-share fix just funds a leaky funnel.

## Insights

- **The paid channel is 100% untapped.** Every visibility blocker in Step 3 exists partly because the brand has never bid on a single keyword. Even a small, well-structured launch on terms the brand already converts on (key lime, branded) should produce immediate incremental sales with low risk.
- **The brand has earned the right to bid on `key lime cake`.** Above-industry conversion on its signature term means PPC there is scaling a winner, not subsidizing a weak one. This is the safest first dollar.

## Things to Investigate (post-launch)

- Once campaigns run, harvest converting search terms from the auto/discovery campaign into dedicated exact campaigns (standard harvest-and-scale loop the account has never done).

## Questions for the Seller

- **What is the contribution margin per cake after COGS and shipping?** This sets the break-even ROAS and the ceiling on launch spend. A perishable gift cake at ~$30 with cold/expedited shipping may have thin margins, which would argue for a conservative, hero-term-only launch rather than broad scaling.
- **Is there appetite to fund a Q4 push?** The single highest-ROI window is the Sep-Dec gifting ramp. A pre-holiday budget commitment is where launch dollars work hardest.
