# Seller Central Audit - Dockside Market

**Brand:** Dockside Market ("Dockside Cakes - A Taste of the Florida Keys") | **Marketplace:** US | **Catalog:** 4 products, 21 child ASINs
**Snapshot:** Gourmet food gift brand from Key Largo, FL (est. 1978). ~$16.5k annual revenue, **~40% of it in December**. **Has never run a single ad.** NYT-voted best Key Lime Pie Cake in the US.

**The one-line story:** A credentialed heritage brand with a real product and a proven holiday demand spike is leaving its two biggest levers completely untouched: it has never advertised, and its flagship listing has no bullets, no A+ content, and no video. The growth path is to build the listing so it converts, then turn on paid traffic, and pour both into the September-December gifting peak.

## Section 2: Qualitative Product Understanding (P0 - Key Lime Bundt Cake)

**Product:**
- A 24 oz tropical-flavored bundt cake, made from scratch in small batches with 100% natural key lime juice, GMO-free, no preservatives.
- Sold as a gourmet gift. Key Lime is the original recipe and best seller; the line extends to Coconut, Lemon, Orange, Rum, Banana Nut, and chocolate variants.
- **Standout asset:** the New York Times voted Dockside the best Key Lime Pie Cake in the US. A real, citable credential.

**Customer:**
- Gift buyers first (holidays, birthdays, gift baskets), self-treat and Florida-Keys nostalgia buyers second.

**Brand:**
- **Registered heritage brand, not white-label.** Baking since 1978 in Key Largo. Multi-channel: own Shopify store (docksidemarket.com), Goldbelly (curated gourmet marketplace, a credibility signal), and Florida gift-fruit resellers.
- **Vibe:** nostalgic, tropical, nautical Florida Keys postcard aesthetic. Authentic but visually dated. Amazon is clearly its least-tended channel.

**Competitive Landscape:**
- **Price positioning:** Gourmet key-lime-bundt-by-mail runs ~$30 (Dockside) to ~$46 (We Take The Cake). Dockside sits ~35% below the premium anchor despite arguably stronger credentials. It is priced like a value option while carrying premium proof.

| Competitor | Key product | Approx price | Notes |
|-----------|-------------|-------------|-------|
| We Take The Cake (Miami) | Famous Key Lime Bundt Cake | ~$46 shipped | Oprah's "Favorite Things." The premium benchmark. |
| Goldbelly bakeries | Assorted bundt cakes | $40-70 | Marketplace, sets the gourmet ceiling. |

On Amazon specifically, branded "key lime bundt cake" competition is thin. Dockside has the credential and the niche to itself but isn't converting the visit into the sale.

**Listing Quality:**

*Strengths:*
- **Title:** strong. Brand, USP ("100% Natural Key Lime juice"), sweet/tart hook, gift framing, size. Readable, no stuffing.
- **Food photography:** 8 appetizing images plus a packaging shot.
- **Heritage credential** (NYT, since 1978) exists as an asset.

*Opportunities:*
- **Bullet points: there are none.** Zero of five filled. The single biggest gap, no selling points, no buyer-concern handling, no keyword capture.
- **A+ Content: absent.** The brand's story (NYT, 1978, Florida Keys, all-natural) is tailor-made for image-based A+. None of it is on the page.
- **Video: absent.** No way to show texture/moistness or the gift unboxing.
- **Brand Store: absent.** No storefront to cross-sell 9 flavors and gift bundles (critical in Q4).
- **Main image:** add a "100% Natural Key Lime Juice" / "NYT Best Key Lime Cake" badge and clean the background to lift CTR.
- **Rating 4.0 (57 reviews):** polarized (32 five-star, 7 one-star). A 1-star cluster on a perishable gift usually means arrival condition.

## Section 5: Ad Opportunity (Greenfield - Never Advertised)

Verified three ways (Seller Analytics `ads_day_count = 0`; Metabase Q698 and Q699 return zero rows 2023-2026): **Dockside has never run a single ad.** Revenue is 100% organic. The entire paid channel is untapped, which is why every visibility blocker above exists.

**Launch architecture (staged by risk):**

| Priority | Campaign | Target terms | Gate |
|----------|----------|--------------|------|
| 1 | Branded defense (small, 2-5%) | dockside market, dockside cakes | Protective only, never scale |
| 2 | Key Lime hero | key lime cake, key lime bundt cake | Launch now - already converts above industry |
| 3 | Bundt cake capture | bundt cake, mini bundt cake | Launch now - relevant, invisible, healthy CTR |
| 4 | Flavored cakes scale | coconut / lemon / orange / rum cake | **Gate behind listing fix** - big market, weak CVR today |
| 5 | Gift-occasion (seasonal) | florida gift basket, florida food gifts | Ramp Sep-Dec around the Tropical Tower |
| 6 | Conquest (optional) | nothing bundt cakes, competitor ASINs | Keep small, watch ACoS |

**Guardrail:** this is a pay-per-click model. Sending paid traffic to a PDP with no bullets/A+ wastes spend, so the big flavored-cake market (campaign 4) waits until the listing converts. Start where the brand already wins (key lime + branded), fix the listing in parallel, then scale.

## Section 6: Action Plan (8 Weeks)

The primary blocker is impression share, made worse by a PDP that can't convert traffic outside the signature term. Because the brand already converts on `key lime` and has healthy CTR everywhere, we can launch low-risk PPC immediately while fixing the listing in parallel, then scale paid into the broad market once the PDP can close it. PPC and listing run together here rather than PPC-first, because the listing has a critical gap (zero bullets) that would waste any broad ad spend.

### Weeks 1-2: Launch the safe paid traffic + quick listing wins
- Launch campaigns 1-3: branded defense, key lime hero (with Top-of-Search modifier), bundt cake capture. These convert or have healthy CTR today.
- Quick SEO win: add "bundt cake" and core flavor terms to the title/backend so the brand can rank organically on Tier 2.
- Refresh the main image with a "100% Natural Key Lime Juice / NYT Best Key Lime Cake" badge to lift CTR.
- Begin writing the missing bullet points (currently zero) for the hero and top flavors.

### Weeks 2-4: Publish core listing content + scale what works
- Publish 5 benefit-led bullets on P0, leading with freshness/shipping reassurance and the NYT credential.
- Start the A+ content build (heritage story, Florida Keys, all-natural, gift framing) and a short product/unboxing video.
- Launch an auto discovery campaign; scale key lime and bundt campaigns on early data.

### Weeks 4-6: Publish the conversion assets + open the big market
- Publish A+ content, video, and a Brand Store (cross-selling all 9 flavors and gift bundles).
- Turn on campaign 4 (coconut/lemon/orange/rum cake) now that the PDP can convert; monitor CVR lift vs the Step 3 baseline.

### Weeks 6-8: Scale + prep the Q4 engine
- Scale PPC on the improved listing across Tier 1 and Tier 2.
- Build the September-December gifting plan: gift-occasion campaigns + the Tropical Tower bundle, front-loaded ahead of the peak.
- Evaluate P1 (Cookies, strongest CVR) as the next product to extend the playbook to.

## Section 7: Insights & Questions for the Seller

**Insights:**
- **The brand is overwhelmingly a Christmas-gift business.** ~40% of revenue lands in December, led by P2 (Tropical Tower) at $3,380 that month. The off-season run-rate is ~$600-900/mo. Seasonality is market-driven and confirmed in SQP.
- **Two huge levers are completely untouched: ads and the listing.** P0 (Key Lime Bundt) has never been advertised, and its listing has zero bullets, no A+, no video, and no brand store, despite an NYT credential. Building the listing then launching paid is the core growth path.
- **The brand converts on reputation, not merchandising.** On `key lime cake` it holds ~4% impression share and converts above industry; across the broader flavored-cake market its add-to-cart rate is less than half industry. Fixing the PDP is the highest-leverage conversion move.
- **Visibility is eroding with nothing to stop it.** P0 (Bundt Cake) Tier 1 impression share fell from 0.44% to 0.21% over 12 months, tracking a session decline from 234 to 140/mo. No ad spend is holding it up.
- **`bundt cake` is a free, relevant, winnable term the brand ignores** (~3-5k searches/mo, near-zero impressions). Title SEO plus a launch campaign captures it directly.

**Questions for the Seller:**
- **Is Dockside meant to be a year-round brand or primarily a Q4 holiday-gift play?** December is ~40% of revenue. This reshapes the strategy: a year-round build (grow Bundt/Cookies as everyday gourmet gifts) versus a seasonal maximization play (pour everything into the Sep-Dec ramp around the Tropical Tower).
- **What is the contribution margin per cake after COGS and shipping?** A perishable ~$30 gift cake with cold/expedited shipping may have thin margins. This sets the break-even ROAS and the ceiling on launch spend.
- **Are the 1-star reviews (7 of 57 on P0) arrival-condition complaints?** Hypothesis: cakes occasionally arrive stale or damaged in transit, holding the rating at 4.0. If so, a packaging/shipping fix is a direct lever on rating, conversion, and repeat purchase.
