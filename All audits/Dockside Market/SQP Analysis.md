# Step 3: SQP Analysis - P0: Bundt Cake (Key Lime flagship)

Brand-level SQP from Brand Analytics. Queries tagged into three tiers around the Bundt Cake line. Market sizing on the trailing 12 months (May 2025 to Apr 2026); share and blocker rates on the last 3 months (Feb to Apr 2026), volume-weighted.

## Tagging Rationale

- **Tier 1 (Hero, flavored gourmet cakes):** `key lime cake`, `coconut cake`, `lemon cake`, `orange cake`, `rum cake`. The Dockside bundt cake IS exactly this: a flavored gourmet cake. These are the direct-match terms for the product's flavors.
- **Tier 2 (Core bundt market):** `bundt cake`, `mini bundt cake`, `nothing bundt cakes`. The cake form Dockside sells, plus the dominant competitor brand term (Nothing Bundt Cakes, a national chain) that represents adjacent bundt-cake demand.
- **Tier 3 (Broad / gift occasion):** `birthday cake`, `key lime pie`, `florida gift basket`, `florida food gifts`. Occasion and adjacency terms. `birthday cake` is huge but generic; the Florida gift terms are small but tightly on-brand for the gift positioning.

Excluded as intent mismatches: `key lime cake mix` (baking mix, not a baked cake), `key lime cookies` / `orange cookies` (the Cookies line, P1, not P0), `coconut pie` / `fruit pies` (pies).

**Catalog overlap (Phase 5):** On Tier 1 flavor terms, the bundt cake is the primary ranker but the Mini Sampler and Tropical Tower also contain these flavors, so 1 to 2 products may rank (cap ~8 to 16%). On Tier 2 `bundt cake`, the 9 bundt flavors plus mini bundt can all rank under the brand (cap ~16%+). Either way, Dockside's actual share is so low that the impression-share blocker holds regardless of the cap used.

**Reading the funnel:** CTR is healthy or above industry on every tier where Dockside appears, so the search-results tile and main image are doing their job. The two real constraints are **(1) being seen** (impression share is a fraction of a percent, and there are no ads to fix it) and **(2) closing the sale on the PDP** (ATC is less than half the industry rate on Tier 1, consistent with the zero-bullets/no-A+ listing from Step 2). The brand gets the click and then loses the shopper, except on `key lime cake` where the NYT reputation carries the conversion.

## Insights

- **P0 (Bundt Cake) visibility is eroding on a $124k/mo market it barely touches.** Tier 1 impression share fell from ~0.44% to ~0.21% over 12 months. The brand is slowly disappearing from its core flavored-cake market, with no ad spend to arrest the slide.
- **The listing converts on reputation, not on merchandising.** On `key lime cake` Dockside converts above industry (NYT credential doing the work). Across the broader Tier 1 its add-to-cart rate is 15.5% vs 37.4% industry. The barebones PDP (no bullets, no A+, no video) loses the shopper who is not already sold on the brand. Fixing the listing is the highest-leverage conversion move.
- **`bundt cake` is a free, relevant, winnable term the brand ignores.** ~3-5k searches/mo, Dockside shows on fewer than 20 impressions. It sells bundt cakes and does not rank for "bundt cake." Title/backend SEO plus a launch ad campaign captures this directly.
- **CTR is a strength, not a problem.** Above-industry click-through everywhere the brand appears means the fix is visibility + PDP conversion, not the image/price/title tile.

## Things to Investigate (carried into Step 4 / launch plan)

- There are no ads, so impression share cannot be bought today. Step 4 becomes an ad-launch plan: the priority targets are `key lime cake` (defend the term it owns), the broader flavored-cake Tier 1 terms (scale once the listing converts), and `bundt cake` (capture the obvious visibility gap).
