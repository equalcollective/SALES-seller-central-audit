## Key Observations from Step 1

**Insights:**
- The brand has an unmistakable **winter/cold-month seasonality** pattern: Dec–Feb is 4-7x larger than spring/summer months. Dec 2024 ($4.5K), Dec 2025 ($3.9K) and Jan 2026 ($3.8K) are all peaks. This is consistent with chili miso oil being a hot/winter-cooking ingredient (ramen, hotpot, stir fry). To be verified via SQP search volume in Step 3.
- The brand is **extremely small on Amazon** at ~$15K trailing 12 months. Even peak months are sub-$5K. Combined with the absence of meaningful ad spend (only $175 over the entire 12-month period), this looks like a brand that has done minimal Amazon optimization to date.
- The P0 (Mild) parent had a **buy box collapse from late March through mid-April 2026** (dropped to 26-47% for 4 consecutive weeks before recovering to 87-100%). Sales dropped during the same window. As a presumed private label brand, this is most likely a MAP/price-related buy box suppression event.

**Things to Investigate:**
- Sales were **zero from July–October 2025 (4 months)** and tailed off from May 2025 onward. This is not consistent with normal seasonality alone (which would still produce some baseline sales). Most likely explanation: stockout or listing suspension. Cross-check Keepa price/sales-rank history for the Mild parent in Step 2 to see whether the listing went dormant or whether the product was unavailable.
- P3 (Variety Pack) and the P1 Spicy 2-Pack only appear in revenue data from Nov 2025 onward. These are recent SKU launches. Verify launch dates and listing maturity in Step 2.

**Questions for the Seller:**
- "Sales went to zero from July through October 2025 and have been recovering since November. Was this a stockout, a deliberate pause, or a listing-level issue? We want to understand whether the 2024 holiday peak is a true ceiling or whether availability gaps in 2025 capped the year."
- "P0 (OKAZU Mild) lost buy box for about a month in late March–April 2026, dropping below 30% on a private-label listing. Were there recent price changes that could have triggered MAP-related buy box suppression?"
