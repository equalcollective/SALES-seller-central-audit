**Parent ASIN:** B0F57PYS5L (contains Mild + Curry children, plus parent shell)
**Primary child (P0 focus):** B07JWCDC4D - OKAZU Mild Chili Miso Oil 230mL @ $19.49

Note: The Mild and Curry children share a parent but are arguably different products (different flavor profiles, different keyword universes). They share A+ content and bullet structure (likely templated across the catalog). Both relevant to the P0 analysis below.

## Product

- **What it is:** OKAZU is a Japanese-style "chili miso oil" condiment - a glass jar (230mL / 7.78 fl oz) of fermented miso, chili, toasted sesame, and garlic suspended in oil. The Mild variant is the gentler, more savory/umami expression of the line.
- **Format:** Glass jar with metal lid, ~$19.49 typical retail price.
- **Ingredients & claims:** Vegan, gluten-free, non-GMO, preservative-free. Built around fermented miso as the umami driver, with garlic and toasted sesame for aroma.
- **Use cases (positioned for):** Ramen topping, stir fry, dipping, cooking, drizzling over rice/eggs/noodles.
- **Value prop:** A "Japanese alternative to chili crisp" - umami-forward (miso base) rather than purely numbing/spicy (Sichuan base). Clean label, premium quality.
- **Purchase motivation:** Adventurous home cooks seeking a Japanese-specific flavor finisher; ramen enthusiasts; clean-label condiment shoppers.

## Customer

- **Target buyer:** Home cooks aged 25-45 interested in Japanese cuisine. Foodie/clean-label shoppers who would also buy Fly By Jing, Momofuku, or specialty miso. Likely skews urban, educated, female lean.
- **Purchase driver:** "Authentic Japanese flavor" + clean ingredients. Reviewers cite versatility (eggs, rice, ramen, dumplings, salad dressings). The miso-base differentiation from Chinese chili crisps is part of the appeal.

## Brand

- **Identity:** Abokichi is a registered brand (Brand Registry confirmed - has Brand Store URL and A+ Premium content). "Abokichi" roughly translates to "avocado boy" in Japanese. Based in Toronto, Canada, husband-and-wife founded brand selling Japanese-inspired modern condiments.
- **Maturity:** Established on Amazon US since 2017 (B07JGJXTK8 Spicy listing first listed 2017-09-22; Mild listed 2019-11-02). 8+ years on platform.
- **Brand depth signals:** Listings consistently use the OKAZU sub-brand name. Brand Store is built out. A+ Premium with 7 modules, 9 images, ~437 words of copy.
- **Notable signals:** Existence of a brand store and brand-registry features suggests a real DTC/retail operation outside Amazon (likely sold in specialty grocers, Asian-American food media coverage).
- **Brand vibe:** Premium-but-approachable, founder-driven, Japanese-Canadian indie. Not a mass-market brand.

**Opportunities:**
- **No video on the primary Mild listing (B07JWCDC4D).** The Curry sibling (B07JGJXYHP) has a video; the Mild does not. Video is one of the highest-leverage CVR improvements and is missing from the highest-volume SKU. **Add a 20-30 second video to B07JWCDC4D.** Note the existing Curry video is under 30 seconds, so re-using/repurposing would be straightforward.
- **A+ content has 437 words of standalone text.** Per current best practice, A+ should be image-first with text designed into the images themselves. 437 words across 7 modules suggests there are still text-heavy modules. Recommended: rebuild A+ so each module is image-with-overlay-text, no standalone text blocks.
- **Title readability score is 35/100** (very low). Title is heavily keyword-stuffed which hurts mobile scannability. Top-3 keyword priority + clean reading order would improve CTR on mobile.
- **Review velocity has stagnated:** 0.19 reviews per 180 days at the Mild flagship. No new reviews in 30 or 90 days. For a private-label product with 4.6 stars, this is leaving social proof on the table. Vine + a structured review-request flow are levers.
- **No active coupon.** Listing had coupons historically but none currently. A standing 5-10% coupon is a known CTR boost on a chili oil at this price point.
