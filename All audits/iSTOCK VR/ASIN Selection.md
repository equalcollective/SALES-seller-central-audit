- Product launched Aug 2025, scaled quickly through Q4. December was a 2x holiday peak ($3,599). Post-holiday sales stabilized at a healthy ~$1,700-1,860/mo baseline, which is strong for a niche VR accessory.
- **Buy box collapsed to 66.5% in March.** This is the most critical issue on the account. Sessions are actually growing month-over-month (699 to 841 to 989), meaning demand is there, but CVR is dropping because shoppers cannot buy from iSTOCK VR when the buy box is lost. Organic sales dropped from $1,300 (Feb) to $660 (Mar) as a direct result.
- The seller started running ads in January 2026. Before that, all sales were organic. Ad spend has been ramping ($15 to $126 to $208), but organic sales are declining faster than ad sales are growing, likely driven by the buy box loss rather than a traffic shift.

## Insights

- **Buy box crisis on P0 and P1:** Both primary products dropped from ~100% to ~66% buy box in March. For P0, this turned $1,300/mo in organic sales into $660. For P1, sessions declined 35% (1,005 to 652 over 3 months) alongside the buy box crash. This is the single biggest constraint on the account. Since iSTOCK VR appears to be a private label brand (no competing sellers expected), this likely points to a pricing event or inventory issue triggering buy box suppression.
- **PSVR2 Gunstock (B0CVJ53357) is burning cash:** $66 in ad spend over 3 months with zero sales in Feb and Mar. Sessions exist (91 in Mar) but no one is buying. This spend should be paused immediately.

## Things to Investigate Further

- P2 (iSTOCK PRO VR Gunstock) had a sharp sales drop from $1,065 in January to ~$280-315 in Feb/Mar, with sessions falling from 829 to ~247. Need to check in Product Understanding whether this was a stockout, suppression, or seasonal drop.
- P3 (B0F4ZJFQMW) is unnamed in the data, growing from $0 to $270, with no ad spend. Need to identify what product this is and whether it warrants ad investment.

## Questions for the Seller

- Buy box dropped from ~100% to ~66% on both P0 (SMARTstock VR Gunstock) and P1 (iSTOCK VR Gunstock) in March. Have there been recent price changes, inventory issues, or new third-party sellers on these listings?
- iSTOCK PRO VR Gunstock sales fell from $1,065 in January to ~$280 in February. Was this a stockout, intentional discontinuation, or something else?
- You started running ads in January 2026 after months of purely organic sales. What prompted this, and are you managing ads in-house or through an agency?
