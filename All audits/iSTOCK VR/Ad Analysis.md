### CVR Blocker: Placement Distribution

| Placement | Spend | Sales | ROAS | CTR | CVR | Orders |
|-----------|-------|-------|------|-----|-----|--------|
| Top of Search | $254.94 | $1,164.70 | 4.57 | 8.86% | 6.19% | 31 |
| Rest of Search | $655.58 | $2,175.41 | 3.32 | 1.57% | 3.05% | 57 |
| Product Pages | $329.18 | $900.76 | 2.74 | 0.70% | 2.31% | 22 |

**Finding: Top of Search is the highest-converting placement but gets only 21% of spend**

**Problem:**
- Top of Search delivers the best ROAS (4.57), highest CTR (8.86%), and highest CVR (6.19%)
- But it receives only $254.94 of $1,239.70 total spend (20.6%)
- Product Pages get $329.18 (26.6%) at 2.74 ROAS, nearly half the conversion rate

**Solution:** Increase Top of Search bid modifier on the manual campaigns to shift more spend toward this high-converting placement. Reduce Product Pages exposure where possible.

**Impact:** If $100 of Product Pages spend shifts to Top of Search (maintaining similar performance ratios), the expected sales increase would be ~$183 (at TOS ROAS of 4.57 vs PP ROAS of 2.74 on the same $100). That is ~$183 in additional sales from the same ad budget.

### PSVR2 Gunstock: Wasted Cross-Product Spend

**Finding: iStock Gunstock for PlayStation VR 2 is burning money across every campaign**

**Problem:**
- Total PSVR2 spend across all campaigns: ~$66 with only $20.99 in sales (0.32 ROAS)
- This product had $0 sales in Feb and March from Seller Analytics
- It appears in 6 different campaigns (ALL AUTO, Catch All Available PT, VR GunStock Phrase, VR GunStock PT, Exact KW'S, New Phrase), eating budget that could go to profitable products

**Solution:** Negate the PSVR2 ASIN from all campaigns, or pause it as an advertised product entirely.

**Impact:** $66 in wasted spend recovered. At the account's 4.45 ROAS on manual campaigns, redirecting this to P0 or P1 would generate ~$294 in additional sales.

## Insights

- The ad account is fundamentally well-managed. Campaign structure is clean (Exact + Phrase separation), ROAS is healthy (4.32-4.69 on manual campaigns), and CPCs are low ($0.35-0.49). The seller knows how to structure campaigns. The issue is not ad management, it is product-level conversion.
- The branded vs non-branded CVR gap (12.5-19% branded vs 2.2% non-branded) is the single most telling data point in this audit. The product works. People who seek it out love it. But new shoppers who discover it through generic keywords don't have enough confidence to buy. This is a solvable problem through reviews, A+ content, and video.
