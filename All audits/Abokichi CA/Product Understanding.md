# Product Understanding (P0) - Abokichi Inc. (Canada)

P0 selected in Step 1 is **OKAZU Premium Japanese Spicy Chili Miso Oil, 230mL Single (B07JGJXTK8)**, the largest-revenue child ASIN in the Abokichi CA catalog. The product is physically identical to the US Spicy listing; the qualitative profile below combines the shared product fundamentals with CA-specific market and listing context.

## 2a. What is the product?

- **One-liner:** A Japanese-style spicy chili miso finishing oil with garlic, fermented miso, and sesame, sold in a 230 mL glass jar at $19.99 CAD.
- **Key features:** Vegan, gluten-free, non-GMO, preservative-free. Umami-forward (miso-based), not a Sichuan-style numbing chili crisp. Toronto-made. Premium positioning.
- **Value prop:** A Japanese alternative to the broader chili-crisp category. Clean-label umami finisher, not raw heat. Designed for ramen, rice, eggs, stir-fry, dipping.
- **Purchase motivation:** Home cooks who want a specifically Japanese flavour finisher, and clean-label condiment shoppers willing to pay a premium for transparent ingredients.

## 2b. Who is the customer?

- Foodies and home cooks 25-45, urban-Canada lean (Toronto / Vancouver / Montreal). Strong overlap with the Japanese-cooking, ramen-culture, premium-condiment buyer.
- "Authentic Japanese" + "made in Canada" are both reasons to choose Abokichi over imported Sichuan or US competitors. The brand explicitly leans into local-CA on adjacent SKUs (the Curry 2-pack title is "Handcrafted in Canada by Abokichi").
- Purchase drivers from review tone (consistent with US): versatility across many uses, umami depth, clean ingredient label, gift-ability.

## 2c. Brand profile

- Toronto-based, husband-and-wife founded. **Listed on Amazon CA since October 2018** (~8 years on platform, longer-tenured than the US listing).
- Registered brand, Brand Store built, A+ Premium content deployed on all main chili miso oil listings.
- Indie-premium positioning. Stronger CA-domestic equity than US (more search volume on branded queries in CA than US per Step 3).
- Brand vibe: founder-led Japanese-Canadian, premium-but-approachable.

## 2d. Competitive landscape (CA)

CA category is meaningfully smaller than US but Abokichi's relative position is much stronger. Amazon CA shoppers see a thinner shelf, and "Made in Canada" plus the only Japanese-miso-based chili oil makes Abokichi a near-default choice on the relevant queries.

## 2e. Listing quality (P0 + P1 + P3 singles, the chili miso oil hero line)

All three singles (B07JGJXTK8 Spicy, B07JWCDC4D Mild, B07JGJXYHP Curry) share the same listing infrastructure:

**Strengths:**
- **A+ Premium content** present on every chili miso oil listing (4,126 characters of A+ payload per ASIN per Keepa metadata).
- **Brand Store** built and linked.
- **Video** on each of the three singles (videos payload ~214 chars indicates 1+ embedded video).
- **9 images per listing** (images payload ~205 chars).
- **5 bullet points per listing.**
- **4.5-star current rating** (Keepa current_rating = 45 on a 0-50 scale).
- 8-year listing tenure (P0 listed Oct 2018) gives organic ranking pedigree.

**Opportunities:**
- **Multipack listings (B088YKQV2K Spicy 2-pack, B088YNXZ4B Mild 2-pack) have no video.** The videos payload is ~4 chars (empty) vs ~214 on the singles. Same gap exists in US. Re-using the existing single-pack video on the 2-packs is the fastest CVR fix.
- **Variety Pack (B07MCYB2VV) has no video.** Same fix applies. Given the Variety Pack does $5-7K/month with zero ad spend, even a small CVR lift from a video would translate into meaningful organic uplift.
- **Title length 197-200 characters** on all singles, almost at Amazon's 200-char limit. The titles are keyword-stuffed (e.g., the P0 title strings together "Umami-Rich Chili Crisp with Garlic & Sesame, Vegan Gluten-Free Non-GMO Preservative-Free for Ramen, Stir Fry, Dipping & Cooking"). Mobile readability suffers. A rebuild that leads with the top 3 keywords and reads naturally would help CTR on the search results page.
- **Sansho Pepper variant (B0CTKKJWFF) is under-built:** no A+ content, no brand store linkage, fewer images (~3 vs 9 on the singles), title 172 chars. Re-launched March 2026; this is the listing-side reason it's currently doing only $172/3 months. If the seller intends to keep this SKU live, the listing needs a full build-out to match the other singles.
- **A+ content style:** the existing A+ is image-rich but per CLAUDE.md guidance, image-first A+ with text-on-image is the current best practice. We won't flag the existing A+ as a primary opportunity (it's already deployed and the brand already has strong content), but a refresh to image-first could be part of a Phase 2 listing initiative.

**Strong-but-not-prioritized observations:** Coupons and promotions data aren't fully captured in Keepa CA data; we can verify these on the call. A standing 5-10% coupon is a known CTR/CVR booster at this price tier and worth checking if it's already deployed.

## 2i. P1 collapse diagnosis (B07JWCDC4D Mild Single)

This is the most important investigation item from Step 1. The pattern in CA Seller Analytics is:

| Metric | Feb 2026 | Mar 2026 | Apr 2026 |
|--------|----------|----------|----------|
| Total sales | $4,102 | $8,259 | **$193** |
| Sessions | 2,450 | 3,220 | **148** |
| Ad spend | $384 | $1,500 | **$0.06** |
| Buy box % | 91% | 93% | 69% |
| CVR | 9.6% | 13.9% | 6.8% |

A 95% drop in sessions alongside the buy box drop is unusual. A simple buy box loss would still let the listing get sessions (the page is still being indexed and clicked), it just wouldn't convert. Sessions evaporating to 148 from 3,220 means the **listing stopped appearing in search/category placements**, which is much more consistent with:
- A stockout (Amazon de-prioritizes out-of-stock ASINs in ranking, both organic and ads), or
- A listing suspension (legal/compliance/category restriction event), or
- An ad pause that removed the meaningful share of paid impressions this listing had been getting.

The Keepa "last_price_change" on B07JWCDC4D shows 2026-04-20, exactly mid-collapse, but the actual list price moves we can see (Mar 5: $18.50 → Mar 17: $18.99) are tiny and pre-date the collapse window. This is more consistent with a Keepa update timestamp than a meaningful price event.

**Confidence: leaning toward stockout, not MAP/price.** Confirm with the seller.
