## Section 2: Qualitative Product Understanding (P0)

**Product:**
- A V-shaped, ABS plastic clip that holds the flaps of a cardboard box open. Sold as a set of 4, in orange.
- Resembles a folded taco shell (hence the name).
- Solves a small, practical problem: when packing or unpacking alone, box flaps fall closed.
- Need-state purchase, not lifestyle. Buyers are home movers, e-commerce sellers, retail stockrooms, and storage organizers.

**Customer:**
- Anyone who packs or unpacks cardboard boxes at scale.
- Discovered through functional searches: "box clips," "box flap holder," "carton flap holder."

**Brand:**
- Tiger Taco Industries, LLC. Single product, registered brand on Amazon.
- Listed on Amazon since August 2014 (11+ years live).
- Mature in tenure, modest in scale (~$24K annual revenue, ~4,400 units/year).
- Brand store published, A+ Content live, video live. Infrastructure is in place.
- Brand vibe: utilitarian, problem-solving, a touch of personality from the orange color and naming.

**Competitive Landscape:**
- **Tiger Taco essentially defined this product category.** The "taco" terminology in search queries (taco clip, box taco) originated with this brand and has become semi-genericized.
- Competitors now bid on these terms. On the Tier 1 query "box clips" (2,390 annual searches), ~24 other listings show on the first page.
- Tiger Taco captures ~25% of all purchases on "box clips" with only ~2.5% of impressions, evidence of a strong product-query fit.
- Average selling price $5.50 (Q1 2026 actual), in line with category accessories. Not premium-priced, not discount-priced.

**Listing Quality:**

Strengths:
- **Buy box and operations:** ~99% buy box across the year, no MAP or pricing volatility. Stable price (zero changes in last 30 days).
- **Title:** 96 characters, includes brand and core feature. Readable.
- **Image gallery:** 9 product images.
- **Brand Store + A+ Content + Video** all published.
- **Reviews:** 4.7 stars, 135 lifetime reviews, 87% 5-star (1.4% 1-star). Healthy distribution.

Opportunities:
- **Main image (highest impact lever):** Currently shows the clips in isolation. The Keepa signal system flagged a specific recommendation: show a close-up of the clip in use on a box edge with a "Fits Single & Double Walled Boxes" badge. Use-case clarity drives CTR on the SERP.
- **A+ Content is text-heavy:** 207 word count across only 2 modules. 2026 best practice is image-led with text overlaid on images. Layout has room for 3 more modules.
- **Bullets are short:** 405 total characters across 5 bullets. Industry benchmark is 800-1,200. Keyword and benefit content is being left out.
- **Video is too short:** Both videos under 30 seconds. A 30-60 second demo of the clip in real packing/unpacking would convert harder.
- **Coupons never used:** has_coupon_ever = false. A coupon badge on the SERP is a free CTR lever.
- **Review velocity has slowed:** 0 new ratings in last 30 days, 0.05/day over 180 days vs lifetime average 0.254/day. Review momentum will matter once paid traffic starts flowing.

- **Tier 2 (Functional Description):**

**SQP Notes:**

- The brand owns conversion in its niche but loses the visibility battle. Tier 1 brand purchase share is 26% on 2.4% impression share - the brand wins the click and the cart way more than its visibility justifies.
- "Taco" terminology has been semi-genericized. Branded defense is needed before scaling non-brand campaigns.
- "box clips" search volume grew ~80% from Q2 2025 (443) to Q1 2026 (801). Whether this is real demand growth or seasonality cannot be confirmed with only one year of history.

**Product Targeting Opportunity (Non-Keyword Lever):**

Keyword targeting on broad queries like "moving boxes" (851K quarterly), "moving supplies" (466K), "shipping boxes" (352K), "cardboard boxes" (118K) does not work because shoppers want the boxes themselves, not an accessory. Industry CVR for Tiger Taco on those queries is 0%.

But the same shoppers, once on a moving-box or packing-supply PDP, are in active packing-or-moving mode where a flap clip becomes a logical add-on. **The play is product targeting on adjacent PDPs, not keyword targeting on adjacent search queries.** Sponsored Products PAT and Sponsored Display product targeting, bid on:

- Top-selling moving box ASINs (small/medium/large)
- Top-selling packing tape, bubble wrap, packing paper
- Moving labels, markers, shipping supplies
- Box cutters, shrink wrap, packing knives
- Moving supply kits/bundles
- Storage box ASINs

The addressable market upstream of Tiger Taco's niche is in the millions of monthly purchases. Even a small attach rate (1-2% of moving-box buyers also buying flap clips) materially expands the brand's revenue ceiling beyond what keyword targeting alone can produce. **This is the primary non-branded growth lever.**

## Section 5: Ad Analysis

**Tiger Taco has never run Sponsored Products advertising.** There is no campaign data to audit, no spend to optimize, no targets to evaluate. This section is intentionally short. The full ad strategy is in Section 6 (Action Plan) as a launch playbook, not a fix playbook.

What this means for the engagement:
- Every ad dollar spent is net-new visibility on a brand that already converts above industry on its core queries.
- The first 60-90 days are about establishing baseline performance and learning, not scaling.
- The brand is not coming in with bad campaigns to clean up - the slate is clean.

## Section 6: Action Plan (Ad-Launch Strategy)

The primary blocker across every tier is impression share. The action plan is straightforward: launch paid search campaigns that bid on the keywords where the brand already converts when it shows up. PPC is the deterministic lever for impression share, so PPC takes the front of the plan. Listing improvements (which lift CTR and CVR further) run in parallel and publish in weeks 4-6 to compound the ad investment.

**Budget framing:** Given the small absolute market size (~$3,400/year addressable through tagged queries), ad budget should start small (~$300-500/month) and scale only with demonstrated ROAS. Spending more than the addressable market can absorb will not generate proportionate returns.

### Weeks 1-2: Launch the Foundation

The blocker is impression share, so the first actions get ads live on the queries that already convert. In parallel, launch product targeting on adjacent PDPs since that lever has the biggest revenue ceiling.

- **Launch Auto Campaign (one campaign, $5-7/day budget):** Tiger Taco has no historical search-term data on Amazon ads. An auto campaign for 2-3 weeks will surface the queries Amazon's algorithm thinks the listing is relevant for, including queries we haven't tagged in SQP. This is the search-term harvest mechanism.
- **Launch Manual Exact Campaign - Tier 1 ($3-4/day budget):** Bid on the 4 Tier 1 keywords (box clips, cardboard box clips, box clip, cardboard box clip) on exact match. Start at suggested bid + 20% to win impression share quickly. Tier 1 is where impression share is most below cap and the funnel is proven.
- **Launch Manual Exact Campaign - Tier 2 ($3-4/day budget):** Bid on top 6 Tier 2 keywords by volume: box flap holder, box flap holders, cardboard box flap holder, box flap clips, box clips for packing, carton clips. Start at suggested bid + 30% (Tier 2 has 10x industry CTR, suggesting the listing wins these placements aggressively).
- **Launch Sponsored Products Product Targeting (PAT) Campaign on adjacent PDPs ($5-7/day budget):** Bid on the top 20-30 ASINs across moving boxes (small/medium/large), packing tape, bubble wrap, packing paper, moving labels, shrink wrap, and box cutters. This is the primary non-branded growth lever. Shoppers on these PDPs are in active packing/moving mode where a flap clip is a complementary purchase. Start at suggested bid + 20% to test placement performance.
- **Launch Sponsored Display Product Targeting Campaign ($3-5/day budget):** Same target ASIN list as PAT, but on the Sponsored Display surface (carousels and "people also bought" placements). SD product targeting often delivers better attach-rate economics than PAT because the placement is closer to the cart decision.
- **Launch Branded Defense Campaign ($1-2/day budget, ~3-5% of total ad spend):** Sponsored Products exact match on "tiger taco," "tiger tacos," "tiger taco box clips," and the top 3 "taco clip / box taco" derivatives. This prevents competitors from poaching brand-name searches. Per the workflow, branded defense is protective, not growth-oriented.
- **Set placement modifiers on keyword campaigns:** +50% on Top of Search across all manual keyword campaigns. Top of Search converts higher and is where impression share gains have the biggest funnel impact.

### Weeks 2-4: Scale What Works, Harvest from Auto

By week 2-3, the auto campaign and product targeting campaigns should have meaningful data. The ad copy is the listing, so this is the moment to start iterating on listing content in parallel.

- **Promote converting search terms from Auto -> Manual:** Any search term in Auto that converts at or above the Tier 2 CVR benchmark (>15%) gets pulled into a Manual Exact campaign at suggested bid. Add as negative phrase to Auto to prevent overlap.
- **Negative-target irrelevant queries** flagged in Auto: anything related to "moving boxes," "shipping boxes," "moving supplies" where SQP shows zero brand conversion via keyword targeting. These will be wasted spend on keyword campaigns if left unblocked. (Note: these terms still work as product targets on the corresponding PDPs - that's a different surface.)
- **Promote converting ASIN targets from PAT -> dedicated PAT campaigns:** Any ASIN target in the seed PAT campaign that converts above 10% CVR gets pulled into a dedicated PAT campaign with higher bids. Negative-ASIN-target the original campaign to avoid overlap.
- **Add new PAT targets weekly:** Audit the "Frequently Bought Together" and "Customers also bought" sections on top moving box and packing supply PDPs. Add the cross-listed ASINs to the PAT campaign, those are the closest behavioral matches.
- **Begin listing content drafts (no publishing yet):**
  - New main image with use-case shot + "Fits Single & Double Walled Boxes" badge
  - 3 additional A+ Content modules (image-led, minimal standalone text)
  - Expanded bullets to 800-1,000 characters with keyword integration
  - 30-45 second product demo video
- **Activate a coupon (5% off):** Free CTR lift on the SERP, the brand has never used this lever.

### Weeks 4-6: Publish Listing Improvements, Add Sponsored Brands

Listing changes go live, ad mix expands.

- **Publish new main image, A+ modules, expanded bullets, and demo video.** Monitor CVR for 1-2 weeks. The expectation is CVR moves from 12.8% (current Tier 1) toward 18-20% as listing presentation improves.
- **Launch Sponsored Brands campaign on branded queries ($1/day budget):** Headline ad on "tiger taco" / "tiger tacos" SERPs to own the top of the page. Drive to brand store to encourage browsing the (single) product properly.
- **Launch Sponsored Display - Product Targeting on own ASIN ($0.50-1/day):** Defensive ad on the Tiger Taco product detail page, prevents competitors from poaching browsers from the PDP.
- **Begin a review-velocity push:** Use Vine (if eligible) and the Request a Review button on every order. The slowing review velocity (0.05/day) will become a ranking risk as paid traffic increases organic ranking pressure.

### Weeks 6-8: Evaluate, Scale, and Plan What's Next

- **Evaluate Tier 1 and Tier 2 impression share movement:** Target 5-7% impression share by week 8 (up from 2.4% / 2.8%). If achieved, expand the Tier 2 keyword list to include longer-tail queries that came in below the original cutoff.
- **Evaluate PAT and Sponsored Display performance:** This is the lever with the biggest ceiling. By week 8, identify the top 10-15 ASIN targets driving conversion and reallocate budget from the broader PAT campaign into dedicated, higher-bid campaigns on those targets. The goal is to lock in the moving-box and packing-supply attach rate as a repeatable revenue stream.
- **Evaluate ROAS:** With a $5.50 product and ~10-15% target ACoS, profitable CPC is roughly $0.20-0.40. If observed CPCs are higher than this on Tier 1, lean harder into Tier 2 (where brand CVR is 28%, supporting higher CPC). PAT placements often run at lower CPCs than keyword search, so they should be the most profitable surface.
- **Plan the catalog expansion conversation with the seller:** The single-product nature of the catalog is the structural ceiling. Ad spend can compound once, but real growth past the PAT-driven ceiling requires new SKUs. This is a separate conversation, surfaced in Section 7 below.

## Section 7: Insights & Questions for the Seller

**Insights:**

- **P0 (Box Clips) keyword search has impression share as the blocker but a small absolute ceiling.** The funnel below impressions is healthy on every tier (CTR 9-10x industry, CVR 1.3-2x industry). The brand wins the click and the cart when it shows up. But the entire tagged keyword market is ~10,500 annual searches, so even at full impression share capture, incremental keyword-driven revenue is only ~$2-3K/year.
- **P0 (Box Clips) primary growth lever is product targeting on adjacent PDPs, not keyword targeting.** Shoppers searching "moving boxes," "moving supplies," "packing tape" want the box / tape itself, not a flap clip - so keyword targeting fails. But the same shoppers, once on those PDPs, are in active packing/moving mode where a flap clip is a logical add-on. PAT and Sponsored Display product targeting on top moving-box, packing-tape, bubble-wrap, and shipping-supply ASINs unlocks a market an order of magnitude larger than the keyword market.
- **P0 (Box Clips) listing has full infrastructure but isn't fully optimized.** A+ exists, video exists, brand store exists. None of them are doing maximum work. The main image, in particular, doesn't show the use case, which is a high-leverage CTR fix flagged by the Keepa signal system.
- **P0 (Box Clips) sees no defense on branded terms.** Brand impression share on "tiger taco" itself is only 2.9%, with competitors flooding the brand-name SERP. A small branded-defense campaign (3-5% of ad budget) closes this leak before non-branded ads start scaling.
- **P0 (Box Clips) "taco" terminology has been colonized.** Queries originated by Tiger Taco (taco clip, box taco) now show competitors. The brand has tolerated this for years because it has no defense ads running. Treating these as branded for ad purposes recaptures the share.
- **The structural ceiling is still the single-product catalog.** Product targeting expands the addressable market significantly, but each customer ultimately buys one SKU. Real scale past the PAT ceiling requires new SKUs (different pack sizes, heavy-duty version, complementary packing accessories).

**Questions for the Seller:**

- **The brand has never advertised. Is that a deliberate choice (margin protection, low priority product) or simply because nobody has tried?** The action plan assumes this is a "never tried" situation. If there's a margin reason, budget will need to be conservative and the focus shifts to high-CVR Tier 2 keywords.
- **Have you considered launching variations** (8-pack, 12-pack, heavy-duty version, different colors, complementary packing supplies)? With one ASIN, the catalog ceiling is fixed at roughly $50-70K annual even after we capture the SQP opportunity. SKU expansion is the only path to materially more revenue.
- **The product has been live since 2014. Has the listing been refreshed substantively over the years (image redesigns, A+ rebuilds), or is the current listing largely the original setup?** This sets the baseline for what "an update" should accomplish and informs how much CVR upside is realistic from listing improvements.
- **Search volume on "box clips" is up ~80% year-over-year (Q2 2025 to Q1 2026). Have you noticed any external trend driving this** (e.g., a TikTok / social moment, a viral mention, a new use case emerging)? Understanding the demand driver shapes whether to expect growth to continue or revert.
