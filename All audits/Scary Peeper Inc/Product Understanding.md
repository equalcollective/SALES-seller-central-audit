## Product

**What it is:** A static 2D-printed plastic face mask of a creepy man in a red hat, hands framing his face, peering through glass. Attaches to any window with an included suction cup, indoor or outdoor.

**Key features:**
- Lifelike printed man's face designed to look like someone is peering into the home
- Heavy-duty suction cup for reliable window mounting
- Rated indoor/outdoor safe
- Reusable across windows (move it anywhere)
- No electronics, no motion, no sound. Pure static visual

**Value prop:** Jump-scare prank. Low-effort setup (press suction cup on glass), long-lasting usability, works year-round. Does one thing exceptionally well: sitting still in a window and making someone's heart skip when they glance up.

**Purchase motivation:** Emotional. Buyers want the joy of watching a friend, family member, or trick-or-treater be genuinely startled. The laugh after the scare is the real product.

## Customer

- **Primary:** Halloween decorators, adults aged 25-55 who enjoy practical jokes. Seasonal peak Sep-Oct.
- **Secondary:** Year-round prank gift buyers. Birthdays, April Fools, sleepovers, white elephant exchanges. The April 2026 sales data shows $200-300/month in off-season, confirming real year-round demand.
- **Purchase driver:** The "scare reaction" is the product. Buyers are not buying a decoration. They are buying the moment of another person's fear.

## Brand

**Scary Peeper Fright At First Sight** (the seller's actual brand, though P0 is mis-registered under "Forum Novelties" on Amazon, see Opportunities below).

- **Identity:** Registered brand with genuine DTC presence at [scarypeeper.com](https://scarypeeper.com/) (Shopify). Holds US Patent 8,651,969 on the product concept.
- **Maturity:** Established brand, not new. Amazon listings date back to 2013-2017 with multi-year rating history. DTC website is well-developed with full product catalog.
- **Social presence:** 7.1K Facebook followers, active YouTube and TikTok channels (@ScaryPeeper). Small but real.
- **Retail:** Primarily DTC + Amazon. No meaningful brick-and-mortar retail distribution.
- **Brand vibe:** Campy, fun, proudly novelty. Tagline "So Effective, You'll Scare Yourself!" The brand leans into the absurdity of the product and doesn't take itself seriously. Strong product-market fit for its niche.

### Opportunities

- **Brand registry error (critical):** P0's listing brand is registered as "Forum Novelties" on Amazon. Every other Scary Peeper SKU in the catalog (B012U6OSZO, B01KSDO0BS, B01LXND0HR, B073X583YZ, B075757SFG, B07HS2CHKX) is correctly registered under "Scary Peeper Fright At First Sight." This is the signature brand product sitting under a competitor's brand name. It means:
  - Brand Store traffic doesn't route here
  - Brand Registry protections (A+ premium, Sponsored Brands eligibility, etc.) don't apply cleanly
  - Shoppers clicking the brand link from the product page go to Forum Novelties, not Scary Peeper
  - Any brand analytics are fragmented
- **Bullet quality:** Five bullets are present, but they are far below the quality of sister SKUs.
  - **Bullet 5 is in Spanish** ("Mascara de Miedo para Ventana") — a wasted slot for the US marketplace
  - Bullets are short (~70-100 characters each) vs. ~200+ on the Level 3 "Giggle" version. Dense keyword stuffing rather than benefit-led copy
  - No benefit framing ("JUMP SCARE IN SECONDS" style leaders that sister SKUs use)
  - No mention of gift occasions (birthdays, April Fools, sleepovers) that year-round buyers actually search
  - Suggested rewrite structure (modeled on the better-performing B012U6OSZO):
    - Bullet 1: "SCARE ANYTIME - Perfect for Halloween, April Fools, birthdays, or year-round pranks. Press the suction cup onto any window and watch friends jump the moment they glance up."
    - Bullet 2: "HEAVY-DUTY SUCTION CUP - Stays put indoors or outdoors through wind and weather. Move it between windows to scare the same person twice."

    - Bullet 5: "PERFECT GAG GIFT - For the friend who loves a good scare. Also great for haunted houses, party props, and holiday decorations year-round."
- **A+ content alt text misalignment:** The A+ modules reference other products in their alt text ("Tapping Peeper", "Scary Peeper Giggle", "Scary Intruder") rather than staying focused on this SKU. Either the A+ was repurposed from another listing or alt text was copy-pasted sloppily. Either way, it dilutes the product's own narrative. The A+ also leans heavily on text blocks, which is below the 2026 best practice (images with text designed into them).
- **Main product video is 8 seconds:** Too short to demonstrate the product in use. The selling point is the scare reaction, but an 8-second video barely shows the product, let alone someone being startled by it. A 30-45 second reaction video would convert significantly better. Works for search indexing but hurts shopper trust. A cleaner title with the patent mention and the brand name upfront would position it as the original, not a knockoff.
