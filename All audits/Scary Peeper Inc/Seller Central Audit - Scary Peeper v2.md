## Section 2: Qualitative Product Understanding (P0)

**Product:** Static printed plastic face mask (red hat, hands framed around face, peering through glass). Vac-formed PVC, printed on, suction cup mount, no electronics. Entry tier of a 3-tier product family.

**3-tier product family (seller-confirmed):**
- **Level 1 (~$11-14):** Vac-formed thin PVC, printed (blister-packaging style). P0 sits here.
- **Level 2 (~$20-25):** Thicker vac-formed, painted, fabric hoodie. **Seller's stated margin sweet spot.** Does not appear to exist as a live Amazon SKU — flagged as a seller question.
- **Level 3 (~$30-40):** Soft vinyl/latex, feels like real skin, hand painted, fabric hoodie, inner support. B012U6OSZO (Giggle), B01LXND0HR (Lenticular Clown), B073X583YZ (Hitchhiker) sit here.

**Customer:** Primarily Halloween decorators (Sep-Oct peak). Secondary: year-round prank-product buyers (April Fools, birthdays, sleepovers). Seller's own view: year-round demand is weak outside the Halloween window, which matches the 9.6x peak-to-trough ratio in the sales data.

**Brand:** Scary Peeper Fright At First Sight. Established DTC brand at [scarypeeper.com](https://scarypeeper.com/). US Patent 8,651,969 on the concept. 7.1K Facebook followers. Mature but small, no retail distribution.

**Listing Quality (P0):**

**Strengths:** Rating 4.3 stable since 2023. A+ content live. Main and customer video live.

**Opportunities:**
- **Brand registry error:** P0 is registered under "Forum Novelties" on Amazon. Every other Scary Peeper SKU is correctly registered as "Scary Peeper Fright At First Sight." This is zero-cost to fix and blocks Brand Store routing + Brand Registry protections on the signature product.
- **Spanish bullet 5** ("Mascara de Miedo para Ventana") on a US listing — wasted slot.
- **Main video is 8 seconds** — too short to demonstrate the scare reaction (the actual product). Sister SKU B073X583YZ has a 45-sec merchant video that sets the benchmark.
- **A+ alt text references other products** (Tapping Peeper, Giggle, Scary Intruder). Stale repurposed modules rather than P0-specific narrative.
- **Bullets are keyword-stuffed and short** (70-100 chars) vs. 200+ on the Level 3 Giggle listing.

## Section 6: Action Plan (8 weeks)

Every recommendation below traces back to data above. Numbers are what the data implies, not promises; weeks 1-4 validate before weeks 4-8 scale.

The primary sequence: listing fixes first (so incremental traffic converts at brand norms), then ad buildout. Halloween 2026 preseason is the real revenue event — August is the latest acceptable launch window for ad infrastructure.

### Weeks 1-2: Zero-cost fixes

- **Fix P0 brand registry** (Forum Novelties → Scary Peeper Fright At First Sight). No ad spend, no content work. Unlocks Brand Store routing and recovers some of the 71% branded impression leak identified in SQP.
- **Rewrite P0 bullets.** Remove the Spanish bullet, mirror the benefit-led structure used on the Level 3 Giggle listing (B012U6OSZO).
- **Launch a small branded defense EXACT campaign** ($5-10/day). Covers scary peeper + brand variants. Protects the branded traffic the listing fixes will surface.
- **Pause on razor blades:** nothing to do here until the account health situation resolves. Do not reactivate those campaigns even if the 180-day wait succeeds — that business line is closed.

### Weeks 2-4: Listing build + ad campaign design

- **Produce a new 30-45 second P0 video.** Install → scare reaction. Sister SKU B073X583YZ proves the format works.
- **Rework P0 A+ content** (fix cross-product alt text, move toward image-driven modules per 2026 best practice).
- **Design (don't launch) Tier 1 EXACT campaign.** 6 ad groups, one keyword each. Bid targets TBD based on preseason competitive environment.
- **Clean-up check on B0FDQXP44B (Kidde adapter) and any other generic-resale listings** that could trigger additional trademark complaints. Prevent further account health damage.

### Weeks 4-6: Launch and measure

- **Publish listing updates.** Monitor P0 CVR for 10-14 days vs the current 7.76% Q1 baseline.
- **Launch Tier 1 EXACT campaign** at $15-25/day. Data implies this should hit 5x+ ROAS given 2.1x industry CTR and above-industry CVR on Tier 1 — but we validate before scaling. If it hits 3x-4x, we hold at current spend and diagnose. If it hits 5x+, we scale.
- **Launch Tier 2 PHRASE campaign** at $10-20/day. This is off-season, so volume will be small. The purpose is to generate early CVR data on Tier 2 queries before Halloween preseason so we know which terms to scale.

### Weeks 6-8: Preseason prep

- **Scale Tier 1 spend based on Weeks 4-6 data.** Target moving impression share from 19.7% toward the ~24-27% cap.
- **Build P1/P2 campaign infrastructure** (Hitchhiker + Lenticular Clown). These SKUs come alive in Sep-Oct — infrastructure must exist by mid-July.
- **Investigate B07HS2CHKX (Electronic Tapping) dormancy.** $8.3K in Sep-Oct 2025, then zero. Inventory or listing issue. Restoring it matters for Halloween 2026.
- **Confirm target Halloween 2026 ad budget** with the seller. The razor blade playbook (10.8 ROAS) shows the team knows how to run ads profitably when they commit. Decide the commitment level for Halloween before inventory planning.

### What we are not doing

- **Not consolidating the 3-tier product family into one parent ASIN.** Discussed in past, seller cold on it. Included as a hygiene upside to revisit once core fixes land, not as a headline lever.
- **Not chasing year-round prank-crew growth.** Seller is honest that the product is primarily Halloween. Year-round becomes a test in Q2 2027 if Halloween 2026 performs, not a Week 1 priority.
- **Not scaling Tier 3.** Market is too diluted to justify spend.

## Section 7: Insights & Questions for the Seller

### Insights

- **The brand registry error on P0 is the single biggest zero-cost fix available.** B00F4HRW50 is tagged as Forum Novelties on Amazon while every other Scary Peeper SKU is correctly branded. Fixing it recovers branded traffic and unlocks Brand Registry protections without any ad spend.
- **The team has proven PPC competence on razor blades at 10.8 ROAS (3.6x break-even at your margin).** The same playbook has not been applied to the Halloween portfolio — that's an allocation choice, not a capability gap.
- **Tier 2 is the only tier that can meaningfully move current run rate.** Tier 1 is mostly captured (19.7% of ~24-27% cap) and the market is small (~$1.6K/mo). Tier 2 is 60x bigger ($98K/mo) and the brand has 0.16% share. A modest preseason lift on Tier 2 is what produces the peak-season revenue the current run rate can't.
- **Current live ad keywords clear break-even but are in the "thin profit" band** (5.19-5.50 ROAS = 1.7-1.8x break-even at 33% net margin). Scaling the razor blade ad discipline (ROAS 10+) to Halloween is what makes the effort worthwhile financially.

### Questions for the Seller

- **Is there a live Level 2 SKU on Amazon, or is that tier currently unfilled?** You described Level 2 as your margin sweet spot (thicker vac-formed, fabric hoodie). We cannot identify a Level 2 SKU at $20-25 in the live catalog. If Level 2 exists off-Amazon or as an inactive SKU, listing it should be a priority — that's the highest-margin tier in the family by your own description.
- **Why is B00F4HRW50 registered as "Forum Novelties"?** Legacy distributor relationship, uncorrected field, or something else? Determines whether the fix is a simple brand update or more involved.
- **Is B07HS2CHKX (Electronic Tapping Peeper) intentionally off between Nov and Mar?** Did $8.3K in Sep-Oct 2025 and zero since. Inventory, listing suppression, or seasonal off-cycle — each has a different fix.
- **Did the Scary Peeper Halloween portfolio run meaningful ads during Sep-Oct 2025?** Our 90-day ad window does not cover Halloween 2025. Shapes whether 2026 is a greenfield ad buildout (needs 2-3 months) or an optimization of existing structure.
- **Is B0FDQXP44B (Kidde smoke detector adapter) another legacy generic-resale listing similar to the razor blades?** 47,855 sessions, 0% buy box, 0 sales. If it's Kidde-branded inventory sold as generic, it could be the next trademark complaint. Worth removing proactively.
- **On Krampus queries:** brand is converting on "krampus decorations" (108K quarterly volume, 27 cart adds) and 5 other Krampus terms. No obvious product fit. Is there a Krampus SKU we haven't seen, or is Amazon fuzzy-matching your catalog into that query cluster?

### Things we're separately investigating

- **Account health / trademark complaint removal options.** Whether the deletion + 180-day approach is the best available path, or whether there are appeal routes. Reporting back with findings before the call.
