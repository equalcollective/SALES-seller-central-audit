# ASIN Selection - Scary Peeper Inc.

## Context

Scary Peeper Inc. is a Halloween prank decoration brand. Their signature product concept is the "Peeping Tom Face Mask" — a suction-cup window mask sold in three quality tiers (entry, mid, premium) that differ primarily by material and build quality.

**Data window:** Business report data covers 2024-07-01 to 2026-04-12. Ad data covers last 90 days only (2026-01-22 to 2026-04-20).

**Audit focus:** This audit centers on the Scary Peeper Halloween brand and the Peeping Tom Face Mask family. A separate razor-blade business line and a suppressed Kidde smoke-detector adapter listing are flagged as seller questions.

## The Two Business Lines

The account has two distinct product lines that need to be discussed separately:

**Line A: Scary Peeper Halloween brand (the core business)**
- Halloween window prank decorations: face masks, peepers, clown masks, hitchhiker props, motion-activated tapping windows, Christmas elf peepers
- Peaks Sep-Oct (Halloween preseason through Halloween)
- Year-round baseline on entry-level Peeping Tom and Creeper Clown SKUs
- Q1 2026 off-season revenue: ~$1.2K across all Halloween SKUs

**Line B: Harry's-compatible razor blade refills (recent diversification)**
- 4 ASINs launched Oct 2025 through Feb 2026
- Q1 2026 revenue: $25.2K at 17.3% CVR and 8.93 ROAS (very strong unit economics)
- Completely off-brand for a Halloween prank company
- Several active listings

The audit deep-dive focuses on Line A. Line B is covered in "Questions for the Seller."

## Priority Selection

P0 is the entry-level Peeping Tom Face Mask (B00F4HRW50), the signature product that:
- Carries the Scary Peeper brand identity (it is the original Peeping Tom)
- Has year-round sales data (even in Q1 off-season, 66 units and $743 at 7.76% CVR)
- Has active ad spend to analyze
- Anchors a 3-tier product family where consolidation opportunities likely exist

P1-P3 cover the other meaningful Halloween SKUs.

### Why P0 is B00F4HRW50 and not the highest-annual-revenue product

The Hitchhiker Prop (B073X583YZ) has higher annual revenue ($13.2K vs $8.7K), but almost all of it comes from Sep-Oct. Picking it as P0 would mean analyzing a product that is effectively dormant 10 months of the year, with very little current ad activity to critique.

B00F4HRW50 is the better hero because:
- It is the signature brand product (the original Scary Peeper Peeping Tom)
- Year-round sales give us current data to analyze
- Active Q1 ad spend means we can actually audit ad performance today
- Strong Sep-Oct lift (~$2K peak vs $200-300 off-season) gives us the seasonal narrative for the 8-week plan
- Serves as the anchor for a 3-tier product family where consolidation is a major lever

### Not prioritized

- **Razor blade line (B0FT1ZY2RT, B0GHGSWRG7, B0GHKNM5C7, B0FMV43JMV):** Biggest current revenue but off-brand. Covered as seller questions, not deep-dived.
- **B0FDQXP44B (Kidde smoke detector adapter):** 47,855 Q1 sessions with 0% buy box and 0 sales. Flagged as critical issue.

## Things to Investigate (for later steps)

- **Peeping Tom Face Mask tier mapping:** B00F4HRW50 is clearly Level 1 ($11 AOV). B012U6OSZO ($40 AOV, "Scary Peeper Giggle") looks like Level 3. Level 2 is not identifiable from the catalog. Needs seller confirmation in Step 2.
- **Consolidation opportunity:** If the 3 tiers are truly the same product at different quality levels, they are strong candidates for merging into a single parent ASIN with variations. This would combine reviews, concentrate traffic, and let shoppers choose their price point on one detail page. Validate in Step 2 (Product Understanding).
- **B07HS2CHKX dormancy:** Zero sales Nov 2025 - Mar 2026 after $5.8K in October. Either an intentional seasonal out-of-stock, an inventory issue, or listing suppression. Check for stock and buy box status.

## Questions for the Seller

**On the 3-tier Peeping Tom Face Mask family:**
- Which ASINs correspond to Level 1, Level 2, and Level 3? B00F4HRW50 looks like Level 1 and B012U6OSZO looks like Level 3, but Level 2 is unclear. Is there a live Level 2 SKU, or is that tier currently unfilled?
- Have you considered consolidating all three tiers under one parent ASIN with variations? The primary differences being material and quality make this a clean variation play, and it would pool review velocity, concentrate traffic on one detail page, and let shoppers compare tiers without leaving the listing.

**On the razor blade business (Line B):**
- The 4 Harry's-compatible razor blade ASINs did $25K in Q1 2026 at 8.93 ROAS and 17% CVR, which is significantly bigger than the entire Scary Peeper Halloween line right now. What is the strategy here? Is this a permanent vertical expansion, a test, or something else?
- Are these your own manufactured blades or reseller/wholesale inventory? This changes the margin picture and growth ceiling significantly.
- Do you want the Halloween and razor-blade businesses run under the same seller account long term, or is a split being considered?

**On the Electronic Tapping Peeper (P3, B07HS2CHKX):**
- This product did $8.3K in Sep-Oct 2025 and then zero sales from November onward. Is this an intentional "Halloween-only" listing, an inventory issue, or something else (buy box loss, listing suppression)? It is your flagship premium product and a meaningful Halloween revenue line being dormant matters.

**On the Kidde smoke detector adapter (B0FDQXP44B):**
- This listing is under your seller account and is getting 47,855 sessions per quarter but has 0% buy box and 0 sales. It generated $32 in Jan 2026 and $48 in Dec 2025, then nothing. This is a massive amount of wasted detail-page traffic. What is this listing, and why is it on your account? Is it yours, a shared listing, or something you need removed?
