## Section 2: Qualitative Product Understanding (P0)

**Product:**
- A static printed plastic face mask of a creepy man in a red hat, peering through a window with hands framed around his eyes
- Attaches to any window via heavy-duty suction cup, rated indoor/outdoor, reusable across windows
- No electronics, sound, or motion. Pure static visual at the $11 price point
- Value prop: an effortless jump-scare prank that works year-round. One suction cup press, instant startle reaction
- Purchase motivation: emotional. Buyers are buying the laugh, not the decoration

**Customer:**
- Primary: Halloween decorators aged 25-55 who enjoy practical jokes. Seasonal peak Sep-Oct
- Secondary: year-round prank gift buyers (April Fools, birthdays, sleepovers). Proven by consistent $200-300/month off-season sales
- Purchase driver: the "scare reaction" itself, not the physical decoration

**Brand:**
- Scary Peeper Fright At First Sight is a registered brand with a real DTC presence at [scarypeeper.com](https://scarypeeper.com/)
- Established brand (not new). Amazon listings date back to 2013-2017 with multi-year rating history
- Holds US Patent 8,651,969 on the product concept, tagline: "So Effective, You'll Scare Yourself!"
- 7.1K Facebook followers, active YouTube and TikTok (@ScaryPeeper). Small but real social presence
- Primarily DTC + Amazon, no meaningful brick-and-mortar retail
- Brand vibe: campy, fun, proudly novelty. Does not take itself seriously. Strong product-market fit in its niche

**Competitive Landscape:**

Price positioning: Avg window Halloween decoration (clings, masks, small props): $8-18 | P0: $11 | Mid-range within broader Halloween decor, entry tier within the Peeping Tom Face Mask family

There are no direct competitors at the "peeping tom face mask" product level. Scary Peeper owns the specific niche. Substitutes compete on broader Halloween decor intent.

| Substitute | Typical Price | How it competes |
|-----------|---------------|-----------------|
| Window cling decals (ghosts, spiders) | $3-10 | Cheaper, flat stickers, less realistic |
| Inflatable window figures | $15-30 | Bigger, indoor only, bulkier |
| Halloween animatronics | $30-100 | Motion and sound premium segment (Scary Peeper's own B07HS2CHKX Electronic Tapping Peeper plays here) |
| Generic costume masks (wearable) | $5-25 | Different use case, but cross-shopped |

**Listing Quality (P0):**

**Strengths:**
- Rating 4.3 stable since 2023. Recovered from 3.4-3.5 low in late 2019, no recent drops. Strong social proof for a patented niche product
- A+ content present (6 modules)
- Video live (main + customer video)

**Opportunities:**
- **Brand registry error (critical):** P0 is registered under "Forum Novelties" on Amazon, while every other Scary Peeper SKU in the catalog (B012U6OSZO, B01KSDO0BS, B01LXND0HR, B073X583YZ, B075757SFG, B07HS2CHKX) is correctly registered under "Scary Peeper Fright At First Sight." The signature brand product sits under a competitor's brand name. This blocks Brand Store routing, fragments brand analytics, and limits brand registry protections. Also means branded SQP impressions on "scary peeper" queries routed to this listing count as NON-Scary-Peeper (contributing to the 71% branded impression leak flagged in Section 4)
- **Bullets significantly weaker than sister SKUs:** 5 bullets present, but they are short (70-100 chars each vs 200+ on B012U6OSZO), keyword-stuffed rather than benefit-led, and **Bullet 5 is in Spanish** ("Mascara de Miedo para Ventana"), a wasted slot in the US marketplace. No gift-occasion hooks (April Fools, birthdays) that year-round buyers search
- **Main product video is 8 seconds:** Too short to demonstrate the scare reaction (the product's actual selling point). Sister SKU B073X583YZ has a 45-sec merchant video that works. An 8-second clip undersells the hero use case
- **A+ content alt text references other products:** Modules have alt text "Tapping Peeper", "Scary Peeper Giggle", "Scary Intruder" — repurposed from other listings rather than tailored to this SKU. Text-heavy A+ layout is below 2026 best practice (images with designed-in text)
- **Title is pure keyword stuffing:** No readability, no brand-of-origin story. Does not position P0 as "the original" despite the brand owning a patent

## Section 6: Action Plan (8 weeks)

Every action traces back to findings above. The primary blocker is visibility on Tier 2 (0.16% impression share on a $100K/mo addressable market). Because this is a Halloween-seasonal brand, the 8-week plan sequences listing fixes first (so traffic converts when it arrives) and ad infrastructure second (so the seller is fully loaded when Halloween preseason begins in late July). PPC scaling happens once the listing is ready.

### Weeks 1-2: Immediate Fixes

## Section 7: Insights & Questions for the Seller

### Insights

- **P0 (Peeping Tom Face Mask, Level 1) has a brand registry error that blocks the largest free revenue lift available.** The listing sits under "Forum Novelties" instead of "Scary Peeper Fright At First Sight" on Amazon. Fixing it recovers branded traffic, enables Brand Registry protections on the signature product, and unifies brand analytics — no ad spend required.
- **Tier 2 is the growth story, not Tier 1.** Tier 1 market is $1.6K/mo and the brand already has 20% impression share of a ~24-27% cap. Tier 2 is 60x bigger ($98K/mo) with 0.16% impression share. The 8-week plan correctly allocates listing fixes before PPC scaling so incremental Tier 2 traffic converts.
- **The brand has proven PPC competence (razor blades at 5.6-10.8 ROAS) but has not applied it to the Halloween portfolio.** Only $26 of ads across 90 days on 5 Scary Peeper SKUs. This is an allocation problem, not a capability problem, which is the best kind of problem to have.
- **P0 (Peeping Tom Face Mask) anchors a 3-tier product family (Level 1 $11, Level 3 $40) where consolidating into a single parent with variations pools reviews (4.3 + 4.7 combined) and concentrates traffic on one detail page.** Requires the brand registry fix first.
- **The seller has built a small but real market position organically.** Tier 1 impression share of 19.7% was achieved with almost zero Halloween advertising. That is the hardest half of the work already done.
- **Halloween 2025 generated $33K across the Scary Peeper Halloween portfolio in Sep-Oct alone.** At current ad discipline ($26 spend on Halloween SKUs in 90 days), there was essentially no ad contribution to that revenue. The upside of adding a disciplined ad stack for Halloween 2026 is material.
