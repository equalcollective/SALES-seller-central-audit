# Product Understanding: SortJoy

## Product Profile: P0 - Sculpted Storage Bins + Lids (Set of 3)

**ASIN:** B0DGQTYJG6 (parent and default child)
**Child variants:** B0DGQSXPR5 (Carbon, Dark Grey), B0DGQTZV68 (Stone, Oatmeal)
**Metabase ID:** 142051

### Product

- Set of 3 sculpted felt storage bins with matching lids, measuring 12.5" x 12.5" x 11.63". The lids double as trays.
- Made from felt derived from post-consumer recycled plastic (70-80% post-consumer recycled PET, 20-30% non-recycled PET). Machine washable, soft, won't scratch surfaces.
- Solves the problem of cluttered closets, pantries, and shelving while maintaining a cohesive, modern aesthetic. Most storage bins look utilitarian; these are designed to be visible and decorative.
- Purchase motivation is driven by the intersection of organization and home decor. Buyers want to organize their space without introducing ugly plastic bins. The sculpted shape, neutral felt material, and lid functionality make these bins something you display, not hide.

### Customer

- **Primary buyer:** Women 25-45, homeowners or apartment renters, invested in home aesthetics. Likely follows home organization or interior design content. Willing to pay a premium for products that look good and align with sustainability values.
- **Purchase driver:** Moving, reorganizing a space (closet cleanout, pantry overhaul, nursery setup), or upgrading from cheap plastic bins to something that matches their home's look. Seasonal trigger: January (New Year organization), spring cleaning.

### Brand

- **Registered brand with real identity.** SortJoy was founded in 2021 by Stefani Herr (professional organizer) and Alexa Cohn (interior designer). Women-founded, sustainability-focused.
- **DTC-first brand expanding into retail.** Primary channel is sortjoy.com with a strong retail presence: Anthropologie, Crate & Barrel (via Havenly), Kassatex. Also sells on Amazon.
- **Notable signals:** Professional website, clear brand story, retail partnerships with premium home retailers. Partners with CleanHub for ocean-bound plastic recovery. Brand has credibility in the home organization space.
- **Brand vibe:** Minimalist, modern, intentional. Earthy neutral tones (Stone/Oatmeal, Carbon/Dark Grey). Clean photography with white backgrounds. The aesthetic is "elevated everyday," positioned between utilitarian storage and high-end home furnishing.

## Insights

- **P0 (Sculpted Bins + Lids) has zero reviews, which is likely the primary driver behind the CVR collapse.** A $160 product with no social proof faces an enormous conversion barrier. Even with good traffic (249 sessions in Feb), shoppers will not commit at this price without reviews. This may explain the progression: early sales likely came from DTC customers or external traffic who already knew the brand, but as that source dried up, Amazon-native shoppers (who rely on reviews) would not convert.
- **The listing content does not justify the premium price.** No A+ content, no video, duplicate bullets, and keyword-stuffed title. At $160, this listing needs to work much harder to communicate value. Competitors like Folden Lane are at similar price points but likely have more polished Amazon listings.
