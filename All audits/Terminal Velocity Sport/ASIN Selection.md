## Priority Selection

### P0: B0F2VQDVHM - Motorcycle Cell Phone Mount with Dual USB-C
The clear hero. 53% of trailing 3-month revenue, healthy buy box (99.7%), highest ROAS at meaningful scale, and clearest product story (mount + charger combo). Has 4 active color variants (Black, Blue, Orange, Yellow). This is where Step 2, 3, and 4 deep dives will focus.

### P1: B0DY5DPK2G - Bike Phone Holder/Motorcycle Mount (Skull variants)
Second-largest by revenue ($1,177 / 3 mo). Same product family as P0 but a different parent listing with skull-themed color variants. Worth flagging because the seller has three near-duplicate parent listings for what looks like the same mount product (this parent plus B0DJCM5S7Y and B0DY5J2MRR), which fragments traffic and reviews. Investigate further.

### P2: B0F1K6WR2Q - American Eagle Projection LED Motorcycle Lights
Third-largest by revenue ($958 / 3 mo) and totally different product category (projection LED underglow that projects an eagle wing onto the road). Profitability is poor: ROAS 0.32 to 0.95 over the last three months, TACoS over 100% (April TACoS 107%, March 133%). The product is bleeding money on ads. CVR is also very low (1.6% in April vs ~5-6% category benchmark). Flag for either listing/pricing fix or pause.

### P3: B0FY4DC95Z - Motorcycle Phone Mount (newer SKU family)
Smaller revenue but explosive growth and best efficiency. $27 in Feb → $190 in Apr (7x). April ROAS 3.86 with 9.68% CVR (best in catalog), TACoS 16.7%. This is what a healthy growth product looks like. Worth scaling.

### Not prioritized
- **B0CVMTTPL9 (USB charger only):** $268 / 3 mo, declining ROAS, accessory-only product without a mount, lower repeat motivation.
- **B0DJCM5S7Y and B0DY5J2MRR:** Listings that appear to share product identity with P1 (B0DY5DPK2G). Fragmenting traffic across multiple parent listings is a structural issue, not a product issue. See Things to Investigate.
- **B0FYSX3STH (SNOODLE dog toy):** Off-brand product (pet toy in a motorcycle accessories catalog) with $0 sales and only $2.63 of ad spend over 3 months. Looks orphaned. Confirm with seller whether to keep or remove.
- **Bicycle stem-piece SKUs (B0F699XX3Y, B0F7T7NMD4, B0F7TRRPP9, B0F7TZZ6NP, B0F7V2YMYG, B0F7V4M2P6, B0F7VBVCXP, B0F7YT1371):** All with 0 to 12 sessions/month and $0 sales. Looks like a series of bundle/component listings that never launched commercially.

## Insights

- P0 (Motorcycle Cell Phone Mount with Dual USB-C) is the hero by a wide margin (53% of trailing 3-mo revenue). The product has a clear differentiator (built-in dual USB-C charger), buy box is essentially full, and current ad efficiency is workable (Apr ROAS 1.36, TACoS 34%).
- P3 (newer phone-mount SKU B0FY4DC95Z) is quietly the best-performing product on every efficiency metric in April: 9.68% CVR, ROAS 3.86, 16.7% TACoS. It needs more ad budget allocated.
- The brand is **down 15% YoY in April** despite turning ads on. Organic sales are lower than the same period last year, which suggests ads may be partially cannibalizing organic rather than adding incremental volume. Worth dissecting in Step 4.
- P2 (LED Wings) is structurally unprofitable on ads (Apr ROAS 0.95, Mar ROAS 0.32). Either CVR is broken on the listing or the keywords being targeted are too broad. Needs a hard look before more spend.

## Things to Investigate

- **Duplicate parent listings for the same phone mount product.** B0DY5DPK2G (Skull variants), B0DJCM5S7Y (base), and B0DY5J2MRR (Flame variants) all carry the same title format "Bike Phone Holder/Motorcycle Mount from US TV Sport..." and look like the same physical product split across multiple parent listings. This fragments reviews, search ranking, and ad attribution. Combined trailing-3-mo revenue across the three is $1,524, which would consolidate to a respectable second-largest product if merged. Need to compare listings side-by-side in Step 2 to confirm.
- **YoY -15% in April despite running ads.** Ad spend in April 2026 was $1,201 but total sales were $474 less than April 2025 (which had $0 ad spend). This is consistent with ads cannibalizing organic clicks the brand was already winning. Dig into search-term level data in Step 4.
- **P2 (LED Wings) negative-ROAS pattern.** Three months of ROAS below 1.0 on $1,220 of spend ($1,160 burned vs. organic baseline). Investigate whether the CVR problem is a listing/price issue or targeting (e.g., the product is in a niche where it should be only on intent keywords like "motorcycle underglow projection wings", not broader "motorcycle lights").
- **Bicycle stem-piece SKUs** (B0F699XX3Y, B0F7T7*, B0F7V*, B0F7Y*) all have near-zero sessions and zero sales. Determine if these are intentional listings (separate accessory components for upselling) or stale dead listings that should be removed to clean up the catalog.
- **Buy box dipped to 79.5% in October 2025.** All other months are at 90%+ . Single-month dip with no obvious explanation. Could be a pricing change, suppression, or competitor on-listing event. Check whether it correlates with a known incident.

## Questions for the Seller

- The SNOODLE dog toy (B0FYSX3STH) sits in your catalog with $0 sales but is still receiving $0.87 to $0.90/month in attributed ad spend. Is this an intentional test SKU or a leftover from another business that should be removed?
- You have three parent listings that look like the same motorcycle phone mount product split by color theme (skull, base, flame). Was this an intentional brand strategy (one listing per design family) or did they accumulate over time? Consolidation could materially help organic ranking and review velocity.
- You started running ads in February 2026 (or at least that's when the data window begins). Were you running ads before that, and if so, what was the prior strategy? This will help us understand whether the Apr 2026 YoY decline is a continuation of a prior trend or new.
- Are you OK with us pausing or significantly cutting back the American Eagle LED Wings campaign? It has been losing money on ads for at least three months and the data suggests a structural CVR issue, not a campaign-tuning issue.
