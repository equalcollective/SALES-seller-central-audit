## Step 2a: What Is the Product

A motorcycle handlebar phone mount that includes a wired hardwired 12V to dual-USB charging port. Combines two products into one: a quick-release phone cradle and a USB charger sourced from the bike's 12V system (SAE pigtail).

**Key features and components:**
- One-handed quick-release cradle (1s lock and release, "press to lock" design)
- Dual USB output (USB-A + 30W USB-C)
- Hardwired to bike via SAE connector with 51" (130cm) cable
- Includes handlebar AND mirror-mount adapters
- Phone compatibility 3.25" to 7" displays
- Waterproof phone enclosure with rain cover included
- Anti-slip pads, Apple/Android adapters in box
- Smart auto-off (cuts power when bike off)

**Value prop:** "Mount and charge in one." Eliminates the need to buy a separate phone mount and a separate USB charger. Saves install time and wiring complexity.

**Purchase motivation:** Riders who use their phone for GPS/navigation on longer rides drain battery fast. Phones running maps in direct sunlight overheat and shut down. Combined mount + charger keeps phone alive on day rides. Secondary use case: ATV and snowmobile riders who run their phone for trail apps.

## Step 2b: Customer

**Target buyer:** Motorcycle owners (especially Harley/cruiser segment based on the bullet copy and brand-store positioning), ATV/UTV riders, and adventure cyclists who already use their phone as navigation. Skews male, 30-60, mid-tier discretionary spend.

**Purchase driver:** Combining mount + charger in a single 1-second-mount unit. Most competitor motorcycle phone mounts do NOT include a charger; charging is sold as a separate accessory. The integrated charge is the wedge.

### Opportunities

- **Title typo: "iPhone 17/15/1614/13/12/11"** (should read 16/14). Small, but for a $22 product where every signal matters, a typo in a title that scans for compatibility hurts trust.
- **Bullet #6 is a "NOTE" full of caveats**: "isn't compatible with flip phones... fits up to 15mm thick with case... OtterBox cases need two hands... not compatible with 90V e-bike batteries." Listing the limitations as a top-of-fold bullet is unusual and likely introduces doubt at the cart-add stage. Better placed inside a comparison module in A+ content or moved to the end. The brand-CVR gap vs industry on Tier 2 keywords (Step 3) is consistent with a CVR-suppressing element on the PDP.
- **Brand name is used inconsistently across the catalog.** Different listings vary between "Terminal Velocity Sport", "TV Sport", and "US TV Sport". Picking one canonical brand voice across all listings would reduce ambiguity and improve brand recall (which matters for repeat purchase and branded search later).
- **No clear competitive positioning copy on the PDP.** The listing tells you what the product is and does, but doesn't anchor the "this replaces TWO products" pitch that is the actual unique selling proposition. A clear comparison module ("mount only vs. mount + charger" with $ math) would materially improve CVR.
- **Reviews data is not visible** in the Keepa snapshot (rating count is NULL across variants). This may be a data gap, but if the actual review count is low (e.g., under 200) the listing is starved of social proof relative to category leaders that have 1K+ reviews.

## Insights

- P0 is a **mount + charger combo** product. The integrated charger is the wedge: most direct motorcycle phone-mount competitors do not include charging, so P0's natural battlefield is "motorcycle phone mount with charger" / "motorcycle phone charger" / "motorcycle usb charger" keywords, not the broad "motorcycle phone mount" head term where it competes on price against mount-only products.
- The listing is technically complete (title, bullets, A+ content, video, brand store, multi-color variants) but lacks **comparative positioning copy**. A "this replaces two products" comparison module would directly address the CVR gap on broader keywords.
- Rating, buy box, and sales-rank trajectory are all stable to improving. Nothing structurally broken on the product side.
- **AOV is sticky around $22-25** with occasional promo dips. Within the mount+charger combo segment this is competitive; vs. plain mounts it is premium. Pricing is a CVR lever only if the comparison value (mount + charger as a bundle) is communicated clearly.
