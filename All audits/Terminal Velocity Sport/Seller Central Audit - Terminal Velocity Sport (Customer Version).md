# Seller Central Audit - Terminal Velocity Sport

**Hero product (P0):** Motorcycle Cell Phone Mount with Dual USB-C Charger (B0F2VQDVHM family).
**Focus of this engagement:** Concentrate the next 8 weeks on the motorcycle phone mount lineup. Weeks 1-4 are PPC restructuring. Weeks 5-8 are listing optimization. End of Week 8 we evaluate whether to scale P0 further or move into the next product.

## Section 1: Listing Quality (P0 - Motorcycle Cell Phone Mount with Dual USB-C)

### Strengths

- **Title is keyword-rich and clear.** Covers the core SEO terms (motorcycle phone mount, USB charger, waterproof, ATV, snowmobile, 12V dual USB-C, mirror mount, rain cover) and the size range (3.25-7") that buyers filter on. Brand prefix is present.
- **A+ content is present** on the listing.
- **Brand video is present.**
- **Brand Store is set up** with a dedicated landing page.
- **Buy box at 99-100%** across all four colorways (private label, no competing sellers).
- **Rating trajectory is stable.** All four variants settled at 4.4 stars after a normal post-launch decline from 5.0. Healthy curve.

### Opportunities

- **Bullets are too long.** The five bullets average ~280 characters each, with bullet 1 over 330. In a category where buyers skim search results and PDPs, dense bullets compete with themselves for attention. The strongest selling angle ("mount + charger in one, replaces two products") should be the first bullet in clear, short framing, not buried mid-bullet. Targeted rewrite to ~180-200 characters per bullet, all-caps lead-ins, single-benefit framing per bullet.
- **Bullet #6 is a "NOTE" full of caveats.** Lists limitations (no flip phones, max 5/8" case thickness, OtterBox case caveats, no 90V e-bike compatibility) at the top of the fold. Surfacing caveats this early in the PDP introduces doubt right at the cart-add decision. Move into A+ content comparison or to the end of the bullets.
- **Title typo: "iPhone 17/15/1614/13/12/11".** Should read "iPhone 17/16/15/14/13/12/11". Small but a credibility hit on a compatibility-driven product.
- **No comparison module on the PDP.** The product's actual unique selling proposition is "this replaces two products" (mount + USB charger). That argument isn't anchored anywhere on the listing. A "Mount Only vs. Mount + Charger" comparison module in A+ content, with $ math showing the bundled value, would directly address the conversion gap on broader keywords.
- **Main image is studio-style.** Category buyers respond well to handlebar-mounted hero shots that show the product in use. A real-bike lifestyle main image would lift click-through from the search results page.
- **Video doesn't address buyer questions.** The current video doesn't cover install, water resistance, or actual charging speed. A 30-45 second video showing install + a brief water/rain test + a charge-rate demo would convert better than the current cut.
- **Brand voice is inconsistent across listings.** Different listings use "Terminal Velocity Sport", "TV Sport", and "US TV Sport". Picking one canonical brand voice and applying it across the catalog improves brand recall and search consistency.

## Section 3: Ad Analysis

**Account-level (Feb 7 to May 6, 2026):**
- Total ad spend: $3,564 over 89 days
- Total ad sales: $3,553
- **Account ROAS: 1.00** (break-even at gross revenue, below break-even after COGS and fees)
- 37 active campaigns, only ~10 with meaningful spend

### Campaign Structure

> **Finding: Campaign structure is over-fragmented**
>
> **Problem:** 37 campaigns active, but only ~10 spending more than $25 over 89 days. Most product families have a full set of 5-6 campaigns (Auto/Close, Auto/Compliments, Auto/Product, Manual Broad, Manual Phrase, Manual Exact). Daily budgets spread so thin that successful campaigns get starved while broad-match campaigns with worse ROAS soak up most of the budget.
>
> **Solution:** Consolidate auto sub-types into one auto per product. Pause campaigns for inactive SKUs. Reallocate to high-ROAS starved campaigns (Diamond Mount Phrase at 2.65 ROAS, FAFO Manual Broad at 1.67, FAFO Manual Phrase at 1.80).
>
> **Impact:** Phrase-match P0 campaign currently $28 over 89 days at 2.65 ROAS = $74 sales. At 10x budget ($280), assuming ROAS halves to a still-healthy ~1.5 due to bidding into broader queries, that's $420 in sales. **+$340 over 89 days from a single starved campaign.**

### Auto vs Manual Split

| Targeting Type | Clicks | Spend | Sales | ROAS | AOV | CPC | CVR |
|----------------|-------:|------:|------:|-----:|----:|----:|----:|
| Automatic | 2,190 | $1,224 | $1,512 | 1.24 | $20.72 | $0.56 | 3.33% |
| Manual | 3,427 | $2,299 | $1,973 | 0.86 | $17.62 | $0.67 | 3.27% |

> **Finding: Auto outperforms Manual. The harvest-and-scale loop is broken.**
>
> **Problem:** Auto returns 1.24 ROAS, Manual returns 0.86. Healthy pattern is the opposite. Auto AOV is also higher ($20.72 vs $17.62), meaning the algorithm is finding better-fit buyers than the manual keyword list is.
>
> **Solution:** Mine top-converting search terms from Auto campaigns. Launch tight Manual/Exact campaigns for them with dedicated budgets. Negate those harvested terms from Auto so spend isn't duplicated.
>
> **Impact:** Bring Manual to Auto's 1.24 ROAS → current $1,973 Manual sales become $2,851. **+$878 over 89 days at same Manual budget.**

### Placement Analysis

| Placement | Impressions | Clicks | CTR | Spend | Sales | ROAS | CVR | AOV |
|-----------|------------:|-------:|----:|------:|------:|-----:|----:|----:|
| Top of Search | 7,898 | 504 | **6.38%** | $353 | $438 | **1.24** | 3.77% | **$23.04** |

| Off Amazon | 512 | 1 | 0.20% | $0.18 | $0 | 0 | 0% | $0 |

> **Finding: Top of Search is the only profitable placement and it's underfunded**
>
> **Problem:** Top of Search returns 1.24 ROAS, 6.38% CTR (more than 3x Rest of Search and 10x Product Pages), and the highest AOV ($23.04 vs $18.18 elsewhere). It's the placement where the listing wins. But it only receives 10% of total spend ($353 of $3,564). Product Pages, at 0.88 ROAS, gets the largest single share of spend ($1,243).
>
> **Solution:** Increase Top of Search bid modifiers (typically 30-50% uplift to start, tuned weekly based on ROAS) to shift more spend toward the placement that converts best. Trim Product Pages bid modifiers where ROAS is sub-1.0.
>
> **Impact:** If 20% of current Product Pages spend ($249) shifts to Top of Search at its current 1.24 ROAS, that's **+$309 in sales over 89 days** at the same total budget. If the Top of Search win-rate increases as more impressions become available on Tier 1 keywords, the ceiling is higher.

## Section 4: Action Plan

### Weeks 1-4: PPC Restructuring on the Mount Lineup

The first four weeks are entirely on the PPC side. Goal: stop the bleeding on unprofitable spend, concentrate budget on the placements and tiers that already work, and unlock budget for the unadvertised P0 color variants.

- **Pause Wing LED (P2) Manual Broad, Phrase, and Exact campaigns.** $869 in spend at ROAS 0.45. Keep only one low-budget Auto/Close for learning.
- **Pause USB 12V Auto/Close and Manual/Broad** ($153 spent, $75 sales). Keep Manual/Exact only.
- **Pause SNOODLE Chew, Bike Stem Mount, and other zombie campaigns** with $0-25 of spend over 89 days.
- **Pause Diamond Mount with USB/Manual Keyword/Broad** ($501 at 0.97 ROAS). Will rebuild in Week 3 with harvested keywords.
- **Increase P0 Phrase and Exact budgets by 5-10x** ($28 → $250+; $18 → $150+).
- **Mine the Diamond Mount With USB/Auto/close campaign** for top-converting search terms. Launch a new Manual/Exact campaign targeting Tier 1 keywords (motorcycle phone mount with charger, motorcycle phone charger, motorcycle usb charger). Negate Tier 1 terms from Auto/close.
- **Activate ads for P0 color variants** Black (B0DJCR293K), Blue (B0DPWRFNCH), Yellow (B0DJNS3QS7) at moderate budgets ($150 each over 90 days).
- **Increase Top of Search bid modifiers across P0 campaigns** (start at +30-50%, tune weekly based on ROAS).
- **Reallocate freed budget to the newer mount (FAFO, B0FY4DC95Z)** which is running at 1.7-1.8 ROAS but only 3% of total spend.

### Weeks 5-8: Listing Optimization

With PPC stabilized and showing improved efficiency, attention moves to the listing. Goal: lift CVR on P0 by addressing the listing issues, so the PPC scale-up from Weeks 1-4 lands on an improved PDP.

- **Rewrite bullets** for tighter readability (~180-200 characters each). Lead bullet 1 with the "mount + charger replaces 2 products" angle in clear, short framing. Single-benefit framing per bullet, all-caps lead-ins for scannability.
- **Relocate Bullet 6** (limitations / NOTE). Move into A+ content comparison module or to the end of the bullet stack.
- **Fix title typo** ("iPhone 17/15/1614/13/12/11" → "iPhone 17/16/15/14/13/12/11").
- **Add a comparison module to A+ content:** "Mount Only vs. Mount + Charger" with $ math showing the bundled value over buying two separate products.
- **Reshoot main image** as a handlebar-mounted lifestyle hero (with phone in cradle, optional charging LED visible) instead of the current studio render.
- **Reshoot video** to 30-45 seconds covering install + water/rain test + a charge-rate demo. Replaces the current cut.
- **Standardize brand voice** across all listings. Pick one canonical name (recommend "Terminal Velocity Sport") and apply consistently.
- **Continue PPC tuning** from Weeks 1-4 in parallel; do not start new campaigns during this window.

### Week 8 and Beyond: Evaluate and Decide

End of Week 8 we look at the data and pick one of two paths:

- **Path A: Scale P0 further.** If the Weeks 5-8 listing changes have lifted brand CVR on Tier 1 (target moving from 2.1% toward industry 5-8%), push P0 PPC harder. With improved CVR the impression-share play can absorb 2-3x more budget without sacrificing efficiency. Continue concentrating on P0 through riding season.
- **Path B: Move into the next product.** If P0 has hit a ceiling on CVR despite listing fixes, redirect attention to the next product in the lineup. The newer mount (FAFO, B0FY4DC95Z) is the most efficient product in the catalog at 1.7-1.8 ROAS and 9.68% CVR but barely receives any ad budget. It is the natural next candidate.

Either path is supported by the data at Week 8. The decision is made on what the CVR data tells us at that point.
