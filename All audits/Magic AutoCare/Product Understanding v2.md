## Product Profile: P0 - Graphene Ceramic Spray Coating

**Product:**
- A spray-on graphene ceramic coating for cars that provides up to one year of surface protection. Comes in a 16oz spray bottle with a microfiber application cloth.
- Combines graphene and SiO2 ceramic technology in a spray format, eliminating the need for multi-step professional application. Claims to coat up to five cars per bottle.
- Protects car surfaces from UV damage, scratches, oxidation, and water spots while delivering a high-gloss, hydrophobic finish.
- People buy it for the convenience: professional-looking results without professional tools, experience, or cost. Spray, wipe, buff, done.

**Customer:**
- Car enthusiasts and everyday car owners who want their vehicle to look freshly detailed without the $200+ cost of a professional ceramic coating. Skews toward DIY-oriented male consumers, 25-55.
- The core purchase driver is the combination of easy application and long-lasting results. These buyers want protection and shine but won't spend hours doing a multi-step detailing process.

**Brand:**
- **Magic Shield** is a registered brand, not a white-label product. Consistent branding across packaging, product line, and web presence.
- **Amazon-native, transitioning to DTC.** Based in Calgary, Canada. Founded around 2019 (per bottle marking "EST. 2019"). ~10 SKU product line built out over 2-3 years. Amazon is the primary revenue channel.
- **Notable signals:** Three web properties (magicautocare.shop, magic-shield.ca, magicautocare.store) with clean, professional design. Social media presence on TikTok (@magicautocare), Instagram, and Facebook, though following appears modest. Listed on Walmart.com marketplace. No brick-and-mortar retail distribution.
- **Brand vibe:** "Accessible pro-grade car care." Black and gold color scheme projects premium feel. Messaging emphasizes that anyone can get professional results at home. Sits between mass-market brands (Turtle Wax, Meguiar's) and enthusiast-grade products (Chemical Guys, CarPro) in positioning.

**Competitive Landscape:**
- **Price positioning:** Average 16oz graphene ceramic spray: ~$25-35 mid-tier. Magic Shield 16oz: ~$30-34. Sits squarely in mid-tier, competing directly with Shine Armor and Adam's Polishes.
