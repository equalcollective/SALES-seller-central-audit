**Share & Blocker Window:** Dec 2025 - Feb 2026 (3 months, monthly)

## Tagging Rationale

- **Tier 1 (Hero):** "graphene ceramic coating", "graphene coating for cars", "graphene coating". Queries where the customer is searching for exactly the product type P0 is. These are graphene-specific coating queries where the product is the direct answer to the search.

- **Tier 2 (Core market):** "ceramic coating for cars", "ceramic coating". The broader ceramic coating market. The product competes here but against a wider field (traditional ceramic coatings, liquid coatings, professional kits). P0 is one of many options, not the direct answer.

- **Tier 3 (Broad/adjacent):** "car wax", "car wax spray". General car protection queries. The product can appear but the searcher's primary intent is traditional wax, not a graphene ceramic coating. Very different competitive set (Turtle Wax, Meguiar's dominate).

## Blockers & Growth Path

**Tier 1 CVR deep dive:** The ATC rate (15.2% brand vs 20.3% industry, 25% gap) and cart-to-purchase rate (17.9% vs 28.5%, 37% gap) both contribute. The cart-to-purchase gap is the more unusual finding. It suggests customers add the product to cart but abandon before purchase at a higher rate than the industry average. Possible causes: price comparison (customers see competitor options in cart), or the product page doesn't close the deal strongly enough (missing A+ text content, no comparison modules).

**Tier 2 impression share context:** At 0.7% impression share on a $387K/mo market, the brand is barely showing up. Even small improvements in visibility here translate to meaningful revenue. If impression share moved from 0.7% to 2% while maintaining current CVR, that alone could add ~$2,500/mo in revenue. If CVR also improved toward industry, the upside is significantly larger.

## Insights

- **The total addressable market for P0 is ~$717K/mo, but the brand captures a tiny fraction.** Tier 1 purchase share is only 1.6%, and Tier 2 purchase share is 0.2%. There is massive room to grow before hitting any market cap.
- **CVR is the consistent blocker across both capturable tiers.** Brand CVR is 55-65% below industry on Tier 1 and Tier 2. This is despite P0 having a healthy on-page CVR of 5.6% (from Seller Analytics). The gap suggests the issue is at the search results page level (customers see the brand but choose competitors) rather than the product detail page level.
- **Tier 1 share is declining** (impression share from 4.8% to 3.7% over 3 months, purchase share from 2.3% to 1.2%). If this trend continues, the brand risks losing its position even on its hero keywords.

## Things to Investigate Further

- **Why is SQP CVR (2.6%) so much lower than PDP CVR (5.6%)?** SQP is brand-level, so other Magic Shield products (P2, P3 with lower CVR) may be diluting the number. In Ad Analysis, check whether P0-specific keyword targeting is converting better than brand-level averages.
- **Cart-to-purchase rate gap on Tier 1** (17.9% brand vs 28.5% industry). Check in Ad Analysis whether specific campaigns or targeting types have better or worse cart-to-purchase rates. This could indicate a pricing or competitive positioning issue.
- **Tier 2 PPC coverage.** Is the seller bidding on "ceramic coating for cars" and "ceramic coating"? If not, this is the single biggest PPC scaling opportunity. If yes, check bid levels and placement data.
