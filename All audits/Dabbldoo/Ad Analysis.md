## Implications for P0 (Kids Food Picks)

The absence of PPC is the single biggest lever available to this brand. From the SQP analysis:

- **Tier 1 impression share is 0.4%** (cap: ~8-9%). The product is essentially invisible on Amazon search.
- **Total addressable market across all tiers: ~$157K/mo in cart add value.**
- The product has proven it can convert at 6-7% CVR when it gets visibility (Nov 2025 demonstrated this).
- There are no competing sellers on the listing (buy box is 100%), so all PPC traffic converts to the brand's benefit.

The entire growth path is impression share recovery through PPC. There is no existing ad structure to optimize, no waste to cut, no campaigns to restructure. This is a greenfield PPC launch.

**Starting bid guidance:** $0.75-1.00 CPC for Tier 1 exact match (niche keywords, low competition likely). Adjust based on impression share data after 1-2 weeks.

**Key principle:** The product is priced at a premium ($24 for 2-pack vs $7 for 150 bulk picks). Generic "food picks" searches will show the product next to dramatically cheaper alternatives. PPC success depends on:
1. Bidding on specific "picky eater" and "feeding therapy" keywords where the premium positioning is justified
2. Letting the strong listing (A+ content, founder story, customer video) do the conversion work
3. Avoiding broad "toddler toys" or "kitchen" terms where the product is irrelevant

## Insights

- This is one of the clearest greenfield PPC opportunities in the audit portfolio. A well-positioned product with strong listing quality, 5.0 star rating, zero competing sellers on the listing, and literally no advertising. The only constraint is visibility, and PPC directly solves that.
- The low review count (8 reviews) is a risk factor for PPC efficiency. Shoppers clicking from search results may bounce when they see only 8 reviews compared to competitors with hundreds. Review building should happen in parallel with PPC launch.

## Questions for the Seller

- Has PPC been intentionally avoided (e.g., margin concerns, preference for organic growth), or has it simply not been explored yet? Understanding the reason helps calibrate the launch approach and budget expectations.
