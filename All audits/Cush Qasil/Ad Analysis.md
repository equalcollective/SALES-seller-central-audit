# Ad Analysis: Cush Qasil

## Overview

Cush Qasil has **zero ad spend**. No advertising data exists for this seller. There are no campaigns to analyze, no search term reports to review, and no placement data to assess.

This section outlines what a PPC strategy should look like based on the SQP findings, rather than analyzing existing campaigns.

## The Opportunity

The SQP analysis identified impression share as the primary blocker across all tiers. The brand captures only 3.3% of available impressions on Tier 1 queries (cap ~8-9%). With zero PPC, all visibility is organic, and organic rankings have been declining for 12+ months.

**What PPC solves directly:** Every additional impression from PPC is incremental. On Tier 1 queries, the brand converts at roughly the industry rate (CTR 2.12% vs 2.37%, CVR 4.79% vs 5.11%). PPC impressions will translate into clicks and sales at a predictable rate.

**Expected economics:**
- Tier 1 "qasil powder" industry CPC context: Niche category with low competition. Expected CPCs in the $0.50-1.50 range.
- At $19.99 price point and ~5% CVR from SQP data, every 20 clicks should yield ~1 sale.
- At $1.00 CPC, cost per acquisition = ~$20, which is break-even on a single sale. However, the goal is organic rank recovery: PPC-driven sales velocity improves organic ranking, which reduces dependency on paid traffic over time.
- Subscribe & Save is enabled, meaning each acquired customer has repeat purchase potential at zero acquisition cost.

## Recommended Campaign Structure

### Campaign 1: Tier 1 Exact Match (Priority)

**Goal:** Capture high-intent qasil shoppers who are currently seeing competitor products.

| Keyword | Match Type | Estimated Monthly Volume |
|---------|-----------|------------------------|
| qasil powder | Exact | ~2,000 |
| qasil powder organic | Exact | ~1,200 |
| qasil | Exact | ~430 |
| organic qasil powder | Exact | ~80 |

**Budget:** $5-10/day initially. This is a low-volume niche, so budget will likely not be fully spent.

**Bid strategy:** Start aggressive ($1.50-2.00) to win Top of Search placement. In a niche category with few advertisers, Top of Search bids may be cheaper than expected.

### Campaign 2: Tier 2 Exact Match

**Goal:** Capture use-case-specific searches.

| Keyword | Match Type | Estimated Monthly Volume |
|---------|-----------|------------------------|
| qasil powder for face | Exact | ~180 |
| qasil powder for hair | Exact | ~350 |
| qasil powder organic for face | Exact | ~350 |
| qasil powder organic for hair | Exact | ~200 |
| qasil face mask | Exact | ~90 |

**Budget:** $3-5/day. Lower volume than Tier 1.

### Campaign 3: Auto Campaign (Discovery)

**Goal:** Discover queries the brand doesn't know about. Amazon's auto targeting may surface long-tail qasil variations and related natural skincare queries where the product could be relevant.

**Budget:** $3-5/day. Review search term report weekly, graduate converting terms to manual campaigns.

### Campaign 4: Branded Defense (Low Priority)

**Goal:** Protect against competitor bidding on "cush qasil" or "cush" queries.

**Budget:** $1-2/day. Small allocation, purely defensive.

**Total recommended starting budget:** $12-22/day (~$360-660/month)

### Campaign 5: Adjacent Product Page Targeting (Growth Lever)

**Goal:** Drive product discovery by placing Cush Qasil ads on the product pages of adjacent, non-competing products. Shoppers browsing powder cleansers, exfoliating powders, and organic face masks have never heard of qasil, but the product solves the same problem they are already shopping for.

**Target ASIN categories:**
- Powder face washes (enzyme powder face wash, powder cleanser): ~2,700/mo search volume
- Exfoliating powders: ~1,000/mo
- Organic face masks: ~2,700/mo
- Powder shampoos: ~1,250/mo

**How it works:** Sponsored Product (Product Targeting) and Sponsored Display ads placed on the detail pages of top sellers in these categories. When a shopper is viewing a Tatcha enzyme powder wash or a Dermalogica exfoliating powder, they see Cush Qasil in the "Sponsored" carousel. The brand's premium packaging, lab testing story, and natural/single-ingredient positioning differentiate it from chemical-based alternatives.

**Budget:** $5-10/day. CPCs on product page placements are typically lower than Top of Search.

**Total recommended starting budget (all campaigns):** $17-32/day (~$500-960/month)

## Areas to Monitor

- **Per-ounce pricing gap.** If the ~19% ATC rate gap is driven by price comparison to larger competitor containers, conversion may remain below potential until the listing or offer addresses this.
- **Review count.** 96 reviews after 3+ years is low. PPC-driven sales volume will help accumulate reviews faster, but a review generation strategy (Vine, Request a Review) should run in parallel.

## Insights

- Zero PPC on a product with healthy conversion metrics and declining organic visibility is the single biggest missed opportunity. The brand has been in a "death spiral" where declining organic rank leads to fewer sales, which leads to further rank decline. PPC breaks this cycle.
- The niche nature of the market is actually an advantage for PPC: low competition means lower CPCs and less bid pressure. The brand can likely dominate Tier 1 search results with a modest budget.

## Questions for the Seller

- What is the product margin on the Qasil Powder? Understanding the margin is critical for setting a sustainable ACoS target. At $19.99 with a 50% margin, the brand can afford a 50% ACoS ($10 per sale in ad spend) and still break even before accounting for the organic halo effect.
