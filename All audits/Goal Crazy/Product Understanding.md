# Product Understanding: Goal Crazy

## Product Profile: P0 - Undated Planner 2025/2026

**Child ASIN used:** B07QNSB373 (Black variant, 1,063 reviews). 6 color variants (Black, Brown, Forest Green, Navy, Rose Gold + one unnamed) under parent B085TN9RTW. All are true color variations of the same product.

### Product

- **What it is:** A 90-day undated guided planner and productivity journal in A5 format (5.875" x 8.25") with PU leather hardcover
- **Key features:** Goal discovery course built into the front, monthly goal-setting and reflection pages, weekly planning spreads, 5-minute daily pages with morning prep and evening review, 3 detachable habit card bookmarks, 3 ribbon bookmarks, elastic strap, rear folder, 100gsm almond pages
- **Value prop:** Structured system that breaks big goals into 90-day cycles with daily accountability. Combines goal setting, habit tracking, and daily planning in one place. Undated format means buyers can start any time.
- **Purchase motivation:** People buy this when they want to get more organized, set and achieve goals, and build daily habits. The 90-day format appeals to people who find annual planners overwhelming. The "life coach in a planner" positioning attracts people who want guidance, not just blank pages.

### Customer

- **Target buyer:** Ambitious professionals, entrepreneurs, students, and self-improvement-oriented adults (25-45). Both men and women. People who have tried generic planners and want something more structured.
- **Purchase driver:** Desire for accountability and progress. Often triggered by a new quarter, new year resolution, career transition, or feeling overwhelmed. The undated format removes the "I already missed January" barrier.

### Brand

- **Registered, founder-led brand.** Created by Jason VanDevere in 2019 after interviewing successful individuals about their goal-setting habits.
- **Maturity:** Established (5+ years), DTC + Amazon hybrid. Not Amazon-native; the brand has a real identity beyond the marketplace.
- **Online presence:**
  - Professional website (goalcrazy.com) with direct sales
  - Goal Crazy Podcast on Apple Podcasts and Spotify
  - Instagram (@goal.crazy), Facebook (Goal Crazy Planners, ~600 likes)
  - Multi-product ecosystem: planner + goal discovery course + coaching
- **Brand vibe:** Professional, motivational, clean. Navy/gold color scheme. Positions as a coaching tool, not just stationery. More substance-driven than aesthetically trendy. Compared to competitors like Passion Planner (Instagram-aesthetic) or Erin Condren (colorful/customizable), Goal Crazy leans into the "system for achievers" angle.

### Listing Quality and Conversion Drivers

**Rating:** 4.4 from 1,063 reviews. Distribution: 72% five-star, 12% four-star, 8% three-star, 5% two-star, 3% one-star. Healthy distribution with strong five-star skew. 1,063 reviews is solid social proof for this category.

**Strengths:**

- **Bullets:** 5/5 bullets, well-structured (246-267 chars each), allcaps first words for scannability. Each bullet addresses a distinct selling point: goal system, daily/weekly planning, premium materials, balanced life organization, and bonus habit cards. Good keyword coverage across bullets.
- **A+ Content:** Premium A+ with 7 image-only modules. Covers key angles (goal discovery course, monthly/weekly layout, daily pages, habit tracker). Image-only format is correct for 2026.
- **Videos:** 6 videos including seller-created content, influencer review, and customer walkthroughs. The customer videos (5+ minutes) provide in-depth product tours that address purchase hesitation. Strong video presence for this category.
- **Brand Store:** Present.
- **Image gallery:** 7 images covering product exterior, feature callouts, internal page layouts (monthly, weekly, daily), and one lifestyle shot. Each image clearly demonstrates a different feature of the planner.

**Opportunities:**

- **Title does not include brand name.** The Black variant title starts with "Undated Planner" instead of "Goal Crazy Undated Planner." For a brand with real identity and a founder story, the brand name should lead the title. This also impacts branded search discoverability.
- **Main image shows the planner closed.** The core value proposition is the internal structure (goal discovery, habit tracker, daily pages), but the main image only shows the exterior cover. Competitors like Clever Fox show the planner open with internal pages visible. Showing the planner open with the habit tracker or goal-setting pages would better communicate what makes this product different from a generic planner, improving CTR on the search results page.
- **Text-heavy images throughout the listing.** The A+ content modules, gallery images, and B+ (brand story) images all follow the same pattern: dense text overlaid on images. While the information is useful (page layouts, feature callouts), the text-to-image ratio is too high, making the listing feel like a manual rather than a product page. Shoppers scroll quickly and absorb visuals, not paragraphs baked into images. The brand owner has already acknowledged this. Reworking these images to lead with visuals (product beauty shots, lifestyle usage, close-ups of premium materials) with minimal text callouts would significantly improve readability and conversion.
- **Single lifestyle image.** Image 7 is the only lifestyle shot, and it features one person (likely the founder). Modern planner brands use diverse lifestyle imagery showing different demographics and use cases (desk at work, morning routine at home, student in library). Adding 1-2 more lifestyle images showing the planner in use across different contexts would strengthen emotional connection.

## Insights

- **P0 (Undated Planner) has a strong listing foundation.** 5 well-written bullets, premium A+ content (image-only), 6 videos, and 1,063 reviews at 4.4 stars. For a $35 planner, this is above average. The opportunities are incremental (main image, brand in title, lifestyle images), not fundamental.
- **P0 (Undated Planner) competes on substance, not aesthetics.** The goal discovery course, coaching ecosystem, and structured 90-day system are genuine differentiators. The listing communicates this through detailed page-layout images and video walkthroughs, which is the right approach for a product where the internal content matters more than the exterior design.

## Things to Investigate Further

- **Brand name missing from title on the Black variant.** Check whether other color variants also omit the brand name, or if this is specific to B07QNSB373. If all variants are missing it, this is a quick fix with potential branded search upside.
- **Main image CTR impact.** The SQP analysis (Step 3) will reveal whether CTR is a blocker. If it is, the closed-planner main image becomes a higher-priority fix.

## Questions for the Seller

- **Rating declining from 4.7 to 4.4 over the product's lifetime.** Are there any known product quality changes, manufacturing switches, or recurring complaint themes in recent reviews? The decline is gradual, so it may simply be regression to the mean as review volume grows, but worth confirming.
