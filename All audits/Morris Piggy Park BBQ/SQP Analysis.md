# SQP Analysis: Maurice Piggy Park BBQ - P0 (Original Southern Gold)

## Tagging Rationale

- **Tier 1 (Hero):** Queries where the customer is searching for a mustard-based or gold BBQ sauce. The product is the exact answer to the search. Includes "carolina gold bbq sauce," "mustard bbq sauce," "gold bbq sauce," "carolina mustard bbq sauce," "south carolina bbq sauce," and variations. (9 queries)
- **Tier 2 (Core market):** Broader Carolina BBQ or BBQ sauce queries where the product is one option among several styles (vinegar-based, tomato-based, etc.), plus the health-positioned "low sugar bbq sauce" query. Includes "carolina bbq sauce," "barbecue sauce," "carolina gold," "carolina barbecue sauce," "carolina gold sauce," "carolina bbq," "low sugar bbq sauce." (7 queries)
- **Tier 3 (Broad/Adjacent):** Very broad generic queries where the product competes against the entire BBQ sauce category. Includes "bbq sauce" (~115K vol) and "sugar free bbq sauce" (~11K vol). These are massive markets where a niche mustard sauce is unlikely to capture meaningful share. (2 queries)
- **Branded:** Maurice's and Bessinger's branded queries. Strong conversion (263 purchases over 15 months on "maurices bbq sauce" alone). Reported separately, not included in growth analysis.

Note: "carolina bbq sauce" (Tier 2) includes customers looking for vinegar-style Carolina sauce, not just mustard-based. The product can show up but is not a match for all intent behind this query.

## Market Share and Potential

3-month average (Dec 2025 - Feb 2026):

| Tier | Impression Share | Click Share | Cart Share | Purchase Share | Trend |
|------|-----------------|-------------|------------|---------------|-------|
| Tier 1 | 8.63% | 10.37% | 9.17% | 7.97% | Growing (from ~3.5% imp share pre-Oct to 10%+ in Feb) |
| Tier 2 | 0.41% | 0.20% | 0.09% | 0.08% | Flat/minimal |
| Tier 3 | 0.12% | 0.03% | 0.02% | 0.02% | Flat/invisible |

Key observations:
- **Tier 1 impression share is near the theoretical max (~8-9%).** Maurice's is already showing up on nearly every search for mustard/gold BBQ sauce. This is a strong position. Click share (10.4%) actually exceeds impression share, meaning the listing gets more than its proportional share of clicks when it does appear.
- **Purchase share (8.0%) lags click share (10.4%) and cart share (9.2%).** The brand attracts attention and gets added to cart, but something is causing drop-off at the purchase stage. This points to buy box issues (customers see the add-to-cart but the actual purchase goes to another seller) or pricing friction.
- **Tier 2 is essentially uncaptured.** 0.08% purchase share on a $137K/mo market. Even modest capture here (say 2-3% purchase share) would add $2,750-4,100/mo in revenue.
- **Tier 1 share has grown dramatically since Oct 2025.** Impression share went from ~3.5% (pre-Oct) to 10.2% (Feb 2026), coinciding with the ad spend increase. The ads are working to drive visibility on hero queries.

## Blockers & Growth Path

| Tier | Impression Share | CTR (Brand vs Industry) | CVR (Brand vs Industry) | Primary Blocker | Growth Path |
|------|-----------------|------------------------|------------------------|-----------------|-------------|
| Tier 1 | 8.63% (near max) | 2.47% vs 2.18% (Healthy) | 12.71% vs 16.49% (23% gap) | CVR | Listing fix + buy box: CVR gap likely driven by absent A+ content and 84% buy box. Fix listing first, then maintain PPC. |
| Tier 2 | 0.41% (very low) | 1.18% vs 2.32% (49% gap)* | 11.68% vs 32.00% (64% gap)* | Impression Share | PPC scaling opportunity, but caution: CTR and CVR data is based on very low volumes (~50 clicks/3mo), so rates are unreliable. Start with PPC to get visible, monitor rates with real volume. |
| Tier 3 | 0.12% (invisible) | 0.75% vs 2.30% | 7.66% vs 32.11% | Not capturable | Intent mismatch. "bbq sauce" searchers overwhelmingly want tomato-based mainstream sauces. Not worth pursuing for this ASIN. |

*Tier 2 rate metrics have low statistical significance (50 total brand clicks over 3 months). Treat directionally, not as precise measurements.

**Tier 1 blocker detail:** The CVR gap is the primary constraint. The brand's CTR is actually above industry (people click on the listing), but once they land on the product page, 23% fewer convert compared to industry. Two likely causes:
1. **Buy box at 84%:** ~16% of visitors see a suppressed or competitor offer. This directly reduces purchase conversion.
2. **No A+ content:** The product page lacks the heritage story, lifestyle imagery, and comparison modules that justify the $12.89 premium. Competitors at $8-10 don't need to justify their price. Maurice's does.

If CVR can be brought from 12.71% to industry's 16.49%, that's a 30% increase in purchases from the same traffic. On Tier 1 alone, that means ~5 additional purchases/month at current traffic levels. With grilling season approaching (May-Jul), that impact would be larger.

**Tier 2 growth path:** The immediate opportunity is PPC scaling on "carolina bbq sauce" (2,311 vol) and "barbecue sauce" (43K vol). The brand has 0.4% impression share, meaning it's barely visible. Starting with targeted PPC on "carolina bbq sauce" is the safer play because the query is closer to the product. Broader "barbecue sauce" is riskier because the CVR gap is large and the audience is less aligned.

## Insights

- **P0 (Original) has saturated Tier 1 visibility but is leaving purchases on the table.** At 8.63% impression share and above-industry CTR, the problem is not visibility or appeal. It's what happens after the click. The buy box + listing quality combination is the bottleneck.
- **Tier 2 is a $137K/mo market where Maurice's captures 0.08%.** Even capturing 2% purchase share on Tier 2 would roughly double the brand's non-branded search revenue. The path is PPC scaling on "carolina bbq sauce" first, then broader queries.
- **Branded queries convert extremely well.** "maurices bbq sauce" generated 263 purchases over 15 months, showing strong brand loyalty from customers who know the product. The challenge is reaching customers who don't know the brand yet (non-branded).

- "Tier 2 impression share is 0.4%. Check in Ad Analysis whether any ad campaigns are targeting 'carolina bbq sauce' or 'barbecue sauce' keywords. If not, this is untapped PPC opportunity."
