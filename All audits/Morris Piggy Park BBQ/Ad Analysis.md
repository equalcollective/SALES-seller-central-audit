# Ad Analysis: Maurice Piggy Park BBQ

**Date Range:** December 1, 2025 - February 28, 2026 (90 days)
**Total Account Ad Spend:** $3,737 | **Total Ad Sales:** $12,602 | **Account ROAS:** 3.37

---

## Account-Level Analysis

### Campaign Structure

The campaign structure is clean. 6 campaigns total:

| Campaign | Type | Targets | Spend | Sales | ROAS |
|----------|------|---------|-------|-------|------|
| Catch-all Auto Discovery | Auto | 4 (standard) | $2,401 | $7,539 | 3.14 |
| Southern Gold 1-PACK Exact | Manual | 4 keywords | $454 | $1,592 | 3.51 |
| Southern Gold 2-PACK Exact | Manual | 4 keywords | $325 | $1,595 | 4.90 |
| Southern Gold 3-PACK Exact | Manual | 4 keywords | $248 | $442 | 1.78 |
| PAT Category BBQ Sauces | Manual | 2 targets | $240 | $1,273 | 5.31 |
| Southern Gold Classic Exact Hero | Manual | 4 keywords | $69 | $162 | 2.34 |

All manual campaigns have 3-4 targets each. No overstuffing. The auto campaign has the standard 4 targeting groups. Structure is well-organized by pack size, each getting independent budget and bid control.

**One issue:** The "Southern Gold Classic Exact Hero" campaign appears to target the same 4 keywords as the 1-PACK campaign. With only $69 in spend, it's either redundant or a test. Should be consolidated or paused.

### Auto vs Manual Split

| Targeting Type | Clicks | Spend | Sales | ROAS | AOV | CPC | CVR |
|----------------|--------|-------|-------|------|-----|-----|-----|
| Automatic | 3,191 | $2,401 | $7,539 | 3.14 | $17.13 | $0.75 | 13.8% |
| Manual | 1,458 | $1,336 | $5,064 | 3.79 | $17.28 | $0.92 | 20.1% |

**Finding: Auto campaigns are overweight at 64% of total spend.**

**Problem:**
- Auto drives 64% of spend but only delivers 3.14 ROAS vs Manual's 3.79 ROAS
- Manual CVR is 46% higher (20.1% vs 13.8%), meaning manual campaigns attract more qualified traffic
- Auto's "complements" targeting burns $87 at 0.88 ROAS (6 orders from 113 clicks)

**Solution:**
- Negate the "complements" targeting in auto (or reduce bids to near-zero)
- Harvest top-converting search terms from auto and scale them in manual exact campaigns
- Shift budget allocation toward manual (target 50/50 as a first step, then optimize)

**Impact:**
- $87 saved on complements negation
- Shifting $500 from auto to manual, at manual's ROAS of 3.79, would generate ~$1,895 in sales vs ~$1,570 at auto's ROAS. Net gain: ~$325 in additional sales from the same spend.

### Campaign Profitability

All campaigns are above the 1.5x ROAS threshold. However, the 3-PACK campaign is borderline:

| Campaign | Spend | Sales | ROAS | Clicks | Orders |
|----------|-------|-------|------|--------|--------|
| Southern Gold 3-PACK Exact | $248 | $442 | 1.78 | 214 | 23 |

Within this campaign:
- "carolina gold bbq sauce" EXACT: $97 spend, 1.46 ROAS (borderline)
- "carolina bbq sauce" EXACT: $73 spend, 4.11 ROAS (strong)
- "mustard bbq sauce" EXACT: $72 spend, **0 orders** (56 clicks, zero conversions)
- "south carolina bbq sauce" EXACT: $6 spend, 0 orders

**Problem:** The 3-PACK ($34.93) is a high-commitment purchase. On "mustard bbq sauce," 56 clicks generated 0 orders. This same keyword converts at 14.8% for the 1-PACK and 45.3% for the 2-PACK.

**Solution:** Pause "mustard bbq sauce" and "south carolina bbq sauce" targeting on the 3-PACK campaign. Let "carolina bbq sauce" (4.11 ROAS) carry this campaign. Or pause the 3-PACK campaign entirely and redirect its budget to the 2-PACK (4.90 ROAS).

**Impact:** $78 in wasted 3-PACK spend redirected to the 2-PACK campaign at 4.90 ROAS = ~$382 in additional sales.

### Targeting Strategy

**Keyword vs Product Targeting:**

| Targeting Strategy | Clicks | Spend | Sales | ROAS | AOV | CPC | CVR |
|-------------------|--------|-------|-------|------|-----|-----|-----|
| Keyword Targeting | 3,734 | $3,168 | $10,645 | 3.36 | $16.84 | $0.85 | 16.9% |
| Product Targeting | 899 | $555 | $1,957 | 3.53 | $19.38 | $0.62 | 11.2% |

Product targeting has higher ROAS (3.53 vs 3.36), lower CPC ($0.62 vs $0.85), and higher AOV ($19.38 vs $16.84). The PAT Category campaign is the account's best performer at 5.31 ROAS. Product targeting is underfunded at only 15% of total spend. There's room to scale it.

**Match Type Breakdown:**

| Match Type | Clicks | Spend | Sales | ROAS | AOV | CPC | CVR |
|------------|--------|-------|-------|------|-----|-----|-----|
| EXACT | 1,021 | $1,082 | $3,791 | 3.50 | $16.41 | $1.06 | 22.6% |

Only EXACT match is used across all manual keyword campaigns. This is clean but limits discovery. There are no BROAD or PHRASE campaigns harvesting new search terms, which means the auto campaign is the only discovery channel. Adding a BROAD campaign on core keywords would expand the search term universe.

**What this means for the growth path:** The PPC side is already performing well on Tier 1. Spending more on Tier 1 keywords will yield diminishing returns because impression share is near max. ### Placement Analysis

| Placement | Spend | Sales | ROAS | CTR | CVR |
|-----------|-------|-------|------|-----|-----|
| Top of Search | $1,341 | $5,115 | 3.81 | 3.39% | 22.0% |
| Rest of Search | $1,402 | $5,027 | 3.59 | 0.22% | 14.8% |
| Product Pages | $936 | $2,250 | 2.40 | 0.18% | 11.2% |

**Finding: Top of Search converts at 22.0% CVR vs 14.8% Rest of Search and 11.2% Product Pages.**

**Problem:**
- Top of Search has the best ROAS (3.81) and nearly 2x the CVR of Product Pages, but gets less spend ($1,341) than Rest of Search ($1,402)
- Product Pages are the weakest placement at 2.40 ROAS, eating $936 in spend

**Solution:**
- Increase Top of Search bid modifier on the manual Exact campaigns (currently likely at default). A 25-50% modifier would shift more impressions to Top of Search.
- Product Pages spend is mostly from auto (which can't be placement-targeted). Reducing auto budget and shifting to manual naturally reduces Product Pages exposure.

**Impact:**
- If $300 shifts from Product Pages (2.40 ROAS) to Top of Search (3.81 ROAS), that's $720 in sales at Product Pages rates vs $1,143 at Top of Search rates. Net gain: $423 in additional sales.

## Insights

- **The account is well-structured but auto-heavy.** Campaign structure is clean (3-4 keywords per manual campaign, separate by pack size). But auto gets 64% of spend while manual outperforms it on ROAS and CVR. The harvest-and-scale loop is partially working (hero keywords are in manual campaigns) but auto still dominates budget.
- **The 2-PACK is the most efficient product at 4.90 ROAS.** It converts better than the 1-PACK on almost every keyword. The 3-PACK struggles, especially on "mustard bbq sauce" (0 orders from 56 clicks). Budget should shift toward 2-PACK.
- **Top of Search is underleveraged.** At 22.0% CVR and 3.81 ROAS, it significantly outperforms other placements. Increasing the Top of Search bid modifier is the simplest PPC lever to pull immediately.
- **The PPC campaigns are performing well on Tier 1 keywords.** The CVR gap identified in SQP is not a PPC problem. It's a listing/buy box problem. Fixing A+ content and buy box will lift conversion across both organic and paid traffic.
