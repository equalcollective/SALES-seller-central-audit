- **Sales recovered via ad spend, not organic growth.** The Jun-Nov period saw sessions drop 35% (1,295 to 729) as buy box fell from 94% to 73-77%. Dec-Feb sales recovered to $5K+ levels, but this was driven by a 4x increase in ad spend ($569 to $2,109/mo), not organic momentum. CVR dropped from ~24% to ~16% as the higher-traffic ad audience converts at lower rates.
- **Buy box is the central issue.** Buy box dropped from 93-94% (Apr-May) to 70-77% (Jun-Nov). It partially recovered to 83-85% (Dec-Feb) but remains well below the 95%+ threshold for healthy sales. Every percentage point below 100% means lost conversions, as some visitors see another seller's offer or a suppressed buy box.
- **The account is heavily reliant on "Original" for revenue.** P0 represents 82% of total account sales ($15,289 of $18,700). The next largest product (Grill Variety Set) is declining from $1,259 to $288/mo. This is essentially a single-product brand on Amazon.

## Insights

- **Buy box instability on P0 is suppressing organic sales.** The 84% buy box means ~16% of page views don't see Maurice's offer as the default purchase option. With 1,570 sessions in Feb, that's roughly 250 sessions where customers saw a competitor or suppressed buy box. At 19.4% CVR, that's ~50 lost orders/month (~$900/mo in lost revenue).
- **Ad spend is compensating for organic ranking loss, not driving incremental growth.** TACoS tripled from 11% to 39% between Dec and Feb while total sales stayed flat (~$5K). The seller is spending more to maintain, not grow. This is unsustainable.
- **B0FX4123MV ("Original v2") has a severe buy box problem at 61%.** This appears to be a newer listing variant of the same Original product. At 61% buy box, nearly 4 in 10 visitors see another seller's offer. Combined with low sessions (83/mo), it's barely generating revenue.
