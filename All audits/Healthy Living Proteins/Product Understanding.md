# Product Understanding: P0 - Collagen Peptides Powder (B07VMJ4PQS)

## Product Profile

**Product:**
- Multi-source hydrolyzed collagen peptides powder (Types I, II, III, V, X) in unflavored 1-pound resealable pouch, 41 servings at 22g collagen per serving
- Sourced from three protein bases: grass-fed bovine (Argentina), wild-caught marine fish, and free-range chicken. This multi-source approach covers all five major collagen types, which most single-source products cannot.
- Solves the collagen depletion problem that accelerates with age, supporting skin elasticity, joint mobility, hair/nail strength, gut health, and bone density
- Purchase motivation: customers want a comprehensive, easy-to-use collagen supplement that covers all types without taking multiple products. The unflavored format appeals to people who want to add it to coffee, smoothies, or water without changing the taste.

**Customer:**
- Health-conscious adults aged 30-55, skewing female, focused on anti-aging and joint health. Keto/Paleo dieters are a secondary audience (product is keto/paleo friendly).
- Primary purchase drivers: skin/hair/nail improvement and joint pain relief. The "aging support" angle is central to messaging.

## Brand

- **Registered brand** with a real identity. "HLP Healthy Living Proteins LLC" is brand-registered on Amazon with a Brand Store.
- **Amazon-native with DTC presence.** The brand sells primarily through Amazon but has a direct website (healthylivingproteins.com) built on Wix with free shipping. Website is professional but basic.
- **Notable signals:** Florida-based. No visible social media links. Contact email is a Gmail address (healthyproteins@gmail.com), which is below brand standards for a health supplement company. The DTC site carries the full collagen line plus "clean home products."
- **Brand vibe:** Warm and approachable, using gold and rose/terracotta color palette. Targets the wellness-conscious consumer who wants premium quality without clinical branding. Packaging is modern and clean.

## Listing Quality and Conversion Drivers

**Strengths:**
- **Rating:** 4.5 stars from 121 reviews with 76% five-star distribution (92 of 121). Very healthy for a smaller brand. No concerning negative trends.
- **Videos:** 4 videos including 2 seller-produced and 1 customer testimonial, all 30+ seconds. This is above average for the category and demonstrates the product well (mixing, dissolving, benefits).
- **Images:** 7 images covering front/back of pack, lifestyle usage, benefits infographic, and supplement facts. Main image is clean with strong shelf presence, the gold/rose packaging pops against white backgrounds.
- **SNS eligible:** Subscribe & Save is active, which is important for a consumable supplement.
- **Brand Store:** Present, giving the brand a dedicated storefront for cross-selling.

**Opportunities:**
- **A+ Content is minimal.** Only 2 modules with 2 images. The content covers benefits and ease of use, but competitors like Micro Ingredients and Ancient Nutrition have 5-7 modules covering ingredient sourcing stories, comparison charts (multi vs single source), FAQ sections, and extensive lifestyle imagery. With only 2 modules, HLP is missing the chance to build trust and address purchase hesitations (sourcing transparency, absorption, taste concerns). Specific additions: a sourcing module showing the three protein sources (bovine from Argentina, marine, chicken), a comparison module showing multi-collagen vs single-source collagen, and a FAQ/trust module addressing common questions (dissolves easily, no taste, third-party tested).
- **Bullets are walls of text.** Average 291 characters per bullet with a readability score of 32.1 (very low). Each bullet is a dense paragraph. Bullets do not lead with an allcaps benefit keyword, which is standard formatting in supplements. Mobile shoppers (40-55% of traffic for this account) will skip these entirely. Restructure: lead each bullet with a capitalized benefit phrase, follow with 1-2 short sentences. Example: "PREMIUM MULTI-SOURCE COLLAGEN - 5 collagen types (I, II, III, V, X) from grass-fed bovine, wild-caught marine, and free-range chicken. Most competitors use only one source."
- **Review count is a competitive disadvantage.** 121 reviews vs 10,000+ for Micro Ingredients and 80,000+ for Vital Proteins. This cannot be fixed through listing optimization, but it makes every other listing element more important. The listing needs to work harder to convert because the trust signal of review volume is not there.

## Sales Rank Trajectory

**Gradually declining.** In the Collagen subcategory (cat 3774321), average monthly rank has drifted from ~900-950 (May-Aug 2025) to ~1,050-1,170 (Oct 2025-Apr 2026). This tracks directly with the buy box degradation. August 2025 (best rank, avg 901) was also the month with the highest buy box percentage (93%). The rank decline is not a demand problem, it's a buy box problem.

## Insights

- **P0 (Collagen Peptides Powder) has a genuine multi-source differentiator that the listing underutilizes.** Five collagen types from three distinct sources is a meaningful advantage over single-source competitors (Vital Proteins, Sports Research). But neither the title, bullets, nor A+ content clearly communicates why multi-source matters. A comparison module in A+ content showing "HLP Multi-Source vs Single-Source Collagen" would address this directly.
