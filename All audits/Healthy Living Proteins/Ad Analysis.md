### Blocker Analysis: Impression Share

Section 3 (SQP) identified **impression share as the overwhelming primary blocker** across all tiers. HLP's impression share is 0.04% on Tier 1 against an adjusted cap of ~24%. The brand is invisible in Amazon search.

The standard approach to solving an impression share blocker is PPC: bid on the keywords where the brand needs visibility. However, for HLP, there is a critical prerequisite that must be addressed first.

**Finding: PPC cannot be launched on the hero ASIN until the buy box is recaptured**

**Problem:**
- B07VMJ4PQS (Unflavored, 1 Pound) receives 60-70% of P0's traffic and generates $640/month in sales
- A distributor holds the buy box on this ASIN at 1-3% buy box for HLP
- If PPC is launched, clicks would send customers to a product page where the distributor has the buy box
- The customer would likely purchase from the distributor, meaning HLP pays for the click but the distributor gets the sale
- This makes every PPC dollar spent on the hero ASIN a direct loss

**Solution:**
- Phase 1: Drive out the distributor (already in progress per seller conversation) to recapture buy box on B07VMJ4PQS
- Phase 2: Once buy box is at 90%+, launch PPC on Tier 1 keywords ("multi collagen peptides," "multi collagen peptides powder") to gain initial visibility
- Phase 3: Expand PPC to Tier 2 keywords ("collagen peptides," "collagen powder") as Tier 1 campaigns stabilize

**Impact:**
- The Tier 1 market for "multi collagen peptides" has ~8,100 add-to-carts per month and ~2,500 purchases per month
- Even capturing 1% purchase share on Tier 1 (25 additional purchases/month at $35 avg price) = ~$875/month in additional revenue
- At a conservative 3x ROAS, this would require ~$292/month in ad spend
- Net incremental revenue: ~$583/month from Tier 1 alone

**Finding: Smaller variants may be viable for immediate PPC testing**

**Problem:**
- While the hero ASIN cannot support PPC (buy box issue), the account has zero visibility on Amazon search
- Waiting for the distributor resolution means continued invisibility

**Solution:**
- Three P0 variants have acceptable buy box (75-95%): Unflavored 10 oz (B0CDMJNBNK), Chocolate 8 oz (B08NTZ333X), Vanilla 16 oz (B0CLQMD8Y5)
- A small-budget auto campaign ($5-10/day) advertising these variants could begin generating data on which keywords convert while the hero ASIN buy box is being resolved
- This serves as a discovery campaign, not a scaling play

**Impact:**
- At $5/day ($150/month), even at conservative 2x ROAS, this would generate $300/month in ad-attributed sales
- More importantly, the search term data from this campaign would inform the full PPC launch once the hero ASIN buy box is recaptured

## Insights

- **The complete absence of PPC is the single largest growth constraint after the buy box issue.** HLP competes in a $8.7M/month market with zero advertising investment. Every competitor (Micro Ingredients, Vital Proteins, Ancient Nutrition, Sports Research) runs substantial PPC. The brand's reliance on organic ranking alone, combined with only 121 reviews, means it will never gain meaningful search visibility without paid traffic.
- **The buy box and PPC problems are sequentially linked.** Fix the buy box first (weeks 1-2), then launch PPC (weeks 3+). Doing them in reverse order wastes money.

## Questions for the Seller

- **Have you ever run Amazon PPC in the past?** The ad data only goes back 9 days. If there was historical advertising that was paused, understanding why it was stopped (unprofitable, budget constraints, didn't know how) helps frame the PPC recommendation.
- **What is your monthly budget capacity for advertising?** Understanding how much the seller can invest in PPC determines whether we start with a focused Tier 1 campaign or a broader multi-tier approach.
