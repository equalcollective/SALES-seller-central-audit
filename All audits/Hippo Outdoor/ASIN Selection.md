## Catalog Overview

Hippo Outdoor sells 12 parent ASINs across 3 distinct product categories:
- **Tarp accessories** (B0CM72RSS8, 6 children): grommet tape, repair tape, bungee cords, protection tape
- **Fly fishing accessories** (B0F5QHC9BW 6 children, B0CGHS9B6W, B09SQB8WFV, B0FRMTF1MZ): clip-on glasses, magnifying glasses, wader bags
- **Golf accessories** (B0C24CJ5YT, B0C24DXC49, B0CNXNRTXG, B0CRZ7PDTC, B0G4XCF364): brushes, carabiner, tee gauge
- **Other:** boat safety stickers (B09Z9KYK31), reading glasses (B0CM9M7ZWW)

Revenue is concentrated in the top 3 parents (tarp tape + two fly fishing products). The golf line and several other ASINs are near-zero revenue with no ad spend.

- Sales peaked May-Jul 2025 (~$2,900/mo) and have been on a steady decline since. Tarp products are likely seasonal (outdoor/camping/boating), but the scale of the drop (down ~60% from peak) exceeds a typical summer-to-spring fade. Seasonality needs SQP verification.
- **Buy box dropped from 98.6% to ~83% in September 2025 and has stayed there.** This is parent-level, so one of the 6 child ASINs is losing buy box consistently. Investigation needed at the child level to pinpoint which one and why (MAP violations are the usual cause on private label).
- **CVR collapsed in March** from ~15-17% range down to 8.9% while sessions stayed flat. This is a recent conversion problem, not a traffic problem. Possible driver: ad traffic quality on the scaled spend in March.

## Insights

- P2 (Fly Fishing Sport Glasses) is the cleanest growth story in the account: sales grew 4.7x in three months (Jan $306 to Mar $1,433), ROAS 2.58, TACoS 23%, CVR climbed from 6% to 13%. This is a ramping launch worth protecting and accelerating. Buy box at 98.8% means the scaling constraint is not supply side.
- P1 (Fly Fishing Magnifying Glasses) is growing at reasonable ROAS but has a high TACoS (32.5%). Ad-dependent growth with only ~50% ad sales share means the base is not yet self-sustaining organically.
- The golf accessories line is effectively dead. Four separate ASINs generated <$15 combined over three months with two showing 0% buy box. Either the listings are broken or the products are abandoned. Needs a seller decision before any attention.

## Things to Investigate Further

- P0 (Tarpaulin Tape) buy box sits at ~83% at parent level since Sept 2025. Need to pull child-level buy box to identify which of the 6 children is losing it and confirm the MAP violation hypothesis.
- P0 (Tarpaulin Tape) sales decline: confirm via SQP whether the drop from $2,950 peak to $1,200 is seasonal (summer to spring fade) or a real share loss. 12-month SQP search volume will answer this.
- P0 (Tarpaulin Tape) the P0 parent groups 6 meaningfully different products (grommet tape, duct tape, bungee cords, attachment points). In Step 2, we will need to decide whether to treat one child as the hero or assess them separately.
- P0 (Tarpaulin Tape) March CVR collapse (17% to 8.9%) with scaled ad spend suggests ads are pulling in less qualified traffic. Cross-check in Step 4.

## Questions for the Seller

- P0 (Tarpaulin Tape): Buy box at the parent level dropped to ~83% in September 2025 and has stayed there. On a private-label listing like this, this typically points to MAP-related buy box suppression on one of the children. Have there been pricing changes, coupon stacks, or external seller activity around that time?
- Golf line (B0C24CJ5YT, B0C24DXC49, B0CRZ7PDTC, B0G4XCF364): near-zero sales with two ASINs at 0% buy box. Is this an intentional deprioritization, or are these listings broken and you were not aware?
- P3 (Waders Catch Bag): $0 ad spend in February, $122 in March. Was February an inventory issue, a budget decision, or something else?
