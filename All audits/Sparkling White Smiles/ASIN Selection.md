## Insights

- **Catastrophic capital misallocation on Teeth Sensitivity Kit (P3).** This product consumed $13,619 in ad spend over 3 months, which is 62% of the account's total ad spend ($22,167), yet generated only $3,362 in sales at a 0.45x ROAS. In Feb alone, $11,514 was spent on this product (ROAS 0.43). The product has a 3.9% CVR, meaning the listing or product itself does not convert. Every dollar spent here is essentially burned.
- **Account-level TACoS exploded from 13.3% (Dec) to 224% (Feb).** This is driven by two factors: the Teeth Sensitivity Kit ad spend explosion and the Sport Mouth Guards ad spend tripling in Feb ($930 to $3,572) while sessions dropped.
- **P0 (Sport Mouth Guards) Feb session drop is sharper than seasonal.** Sessions went from a typical 3,000-4,000 range to 1,853. Even the Sep 2025 trough (typically the weakest month for sports gear) had 3,087 sessions. Something beyond seasonality is happening.

## Things to Investigate Further

- Why did P0 sessions drop to 1,853 in Feb, 40% below even the previous seasonal trough? Check SQP impression share trends and ad campaign changes to determine if this is organic ranking loss, reduced ad visibility, or a broader market contraction.
- Pearldent Dental Foam (P1) ROAS collapsed from 1.51 (Dec) to 0.39 (Feb) while ad spend increased 6x. Check ad campaigns for misallocation or keyword issues.
- Appliance Cleaner (P2) has an extraordinary 61% CVR and is almost entirely organic. Sessions are declining (144 to 84 over 3 months). Is this product losing organic ranking, or is it in a naturally shrinking niche?

## Questions for the Seller

- The Teeth Sensitivity Kit (P3) consumed $13,619 in ad spend in 3 months with a 0.45x ROAS. In Dec, this product had a 9.7% buy box, suggesting it was out of stock or had a listing issue. Was the Jan-Feb ad spend an intentional product launch push after fixing the buy box, or did the campaigns run unchecked?
- Appliance Cleaner (P2) converts at 61% and is nearly 100% organic, but sessions are declining. Is this product being phased out, or is it a core product worth investing in?
- Nine of the 13 products in the catalog have zero sales. Are these discontinued, or are there plans to revive any of them?
