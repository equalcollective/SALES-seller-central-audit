# Product Understanding - Celebration Stadium

## Product Profile: P0 - Candle Holder Grandstand and Tray

**Child ASIN used for analysis:** B0C2WQ8G64 (Modular Birthday Grandstand), the primary revenue driver within the parent. This child generated ~80% of parent sales over the last 3 months ($8,094 of $10,004). The other children (White variant B07FDLYPLN and Silver-Mirror variant B09CYDMYF8) share the same product identity and listing structure, differing only in finish/color.

### Product

- A modular, flame-resistant candle holder designed to display up to 100 birthday candles in a grandstand/stadium format surrounding a birthday cake. Includes 100 gold candles.
- The product is built from 10 stackable plastic modules that assemble into a curved grandstand shape, creating a visual spectacle where every candle represents a year of life. Patented design.
- Solves a real problem: there is no good way to put 50, 60, 80, or 100 individual birthday candles on a cake. This product creates that experience while keeping candles elevated and separate from the food (no wax drip, more sanitary).
- Purchase motivation is emotional and event-driven. Customers buy this for milestone birthdays (50th, 60th, 70th, 80th, 90th, 100th) where the moment of blowing out "one candle for every year" is the centerpiece of the celebration. It is a photo-worthy, memorable experience product.

### Customer

- **Primary buyer:** Adult children, grandchildren, or event planners organizing a milestone birthday party for someone 50+ years old.
- **Purchase driver:** The desire to create a special, memorable birthday moment. The "wow factor" of 100 lit candles surrounding a cake. This is not a utilitarian purchase; it is an emotional and celebratory one.
- **Secondary use cases:** Restaurants hosting birthday events, event planners, recurring family celebrations (the product is reusable).

### Brand

- **Registered brand with genuine identity.** Founded by Knight Merritt (custom model builder in Seattle) who created the original prototype for a friend's 80th birthday. Now run by family (Carey Ide and Wil Merritt). Patented design.
- **Brand maturity:** Small, family-run, product-inventor brand. DTC-first (Shopify store at celebrationstadium.com), expanded to Amazon, Walmart, and Etsy.
- **Notable signals:** Professional website with product ecosystem (grandstand, candelabra, trays, candle refills, accessories, personalized toppers, gift books). Instagram ~1,169 followers (significantly underutilized for a product with strong visual/viral potential). Has a genuine origin story that could be leveraged for marketing.
- **Brand vibe:** Heartfelt, family-oriented, celebratory. Premium positioning with emotional appeal.

### Competitive Landscape

- **Price positioning:** This product has no direct competitors. Celebration Stadium created and owns the "milestone birthday candle holder" niche. The nearest adjacent products are small 9-candle novelty candelabras at $5-15, which serve an entirely different function. Celebration Stadium's candle holders range from $89.95 (Grandstand) to $139.95 (Silver-Mirror Gala).

### Listing Quality and Conversion Drivers

**Rating:** 4.5 stars (stable). Rating distribution not available in Keepa snapshot, but the 4.5 has fluctuated between 4.3-4.7 over the past two years with no sustained decline.

**Strengths:**

- **Images:** 9 images. Main image shows the product in use with a cake, candles lit, and clear callouts for "10 Stackable Modules" and "100 Gold Birthday Candles." Variant images show different angles and use cases.
- **Bullets:** 5 well-written bullets (1,708 chars total). Cover key selling points: milestone birthdays, elegant design, made in China with patented design, easy storage, and versatility. All caps first word for scannability.
- **A+ Content:** Premium A+ with 7 modules and 11 images. Includes feature comparison, lifestyle imagery, and embedded video. Highlights key benefits (no wax drip, sanitary, stackable storage, reusable).
- **Videos:** 7 videos including setup guides, product demos, usage examples, and even a customer video. This is excellent for a product that requires visual demonstration to understand.
- **Brand Store:** Active.

**Opportunities:**

- **Main image:** The Keepa signal flagged: "display a lit birthday cake with the candle holder in action & remove the text boxes at the bottom." The current main image has text callouts ("10 Stackable Modules," "Modular Birthday Candle Holder," "Perfect For: 40th, 50th...") that add clutter. A cleaner image showing the product in action (candles lit, birthday setting) without text overlay could improve CTR on the search results page. The text information is already in the title and bullets.
- **Title:** At 199 characters, the title is close to the Amazon limit. The current title ("Celebration Stadium Modular Birthday Candle Holder Grandstand for Milestone Birthdays - Perfect for 40th, 50th, 60th, 70th, 80th, 90th, & 100th - Includes 100 Gold Birthday Candles - Cake Decorations") is keyword-heavy but readable. The milestone age list takes up significant space. Could be tightened to emphasize the unique value prop more prominently.
- **Bullet #3** mentions "MADE IN CHINA" upfront, which is an unusual choice for a premium product. The patented design and quality messaging could lead instead.

## Insights

- **P0 (Candle Holder) has no direct competitors and a patented design.** This is both a strength (no price pressure, no market share battle) and a challenge (no established category for shoppers to search within). Growth depends on capturing demand from adjacent and broader queries rather than winning share from competitors.
- **The buy box concern from Step 1 is resolved.** All selling children have near-100% buy box. The parent-level metric was misleading due to a virtual parent ASIN with 0% buy box and negligible traffic.
- **CVR is genuinely low (2-4%) even on the primary child with 100% buy box.** This is partly inherent to the product: a $90 niche item for a specific occasion will naturally convert lower than a $10 consumable. However, there may still be room to improve. The main image optimization and "Made in China" bullet are small but real conversion friction points.

## Things to Investigate Further

- How much search volume exists for "birthday candle holder," "birthday candelabra," and milestone-specific queries? If the category is small, the growth ceiling may be low regardless of what we do (Step 3).
- Are the ads targeting the right queries, or is spend going to broad/irrelevant terms given how niche this product is? (Step 4)
- Is the ~$90 price point an issue on Amazon? The DTC site prices similarly, but Amazon shoppers may have different price sensitivity for this category. Check SQP CVR vs industry to assess (Step 3).
