# Step 3: SQP Analysis - P0 (RS400 Red Spinach Extract)

## Tagging Rationale

The brand sells two active red spinach extract products on Amazon (RS400 and VitaSpinach). SQP is brand-level, so the tags apply to the brand's full converting query set, with the framework chosen to reflect P0's relevance.

- **Tier 1 (Hero):** Queries where the customer is searching for red spinach extract / red spinach powder specifically. P0 is a direct answer. 16 queries tagged.
- **Tier 2 (Core market):** Nitric oxide booster and beet root powder queries. P0 explicitly positions as a "beet root alternative", so this is the broader category the brand is positioned to take share from. 13 queries tagged.
- **Tier 3 (Broad / adjacent):** Pre-workout, blood pressure, heart health, and oxygen-boosting queries. The two products split intent here (RS400 better for pre-workout, VitaSpinach better for blood pressure / heart health), so reach is real but conversion will be uneven. 7 queries tagged.
- **Branded:** Brand and product-name queries (rs400, vita spinach, vitaspinach, nutrigardens beet boost). Existing brand equity, not a growth lever. 7 queries tagged.

**Excluded from tagging (and why):**
- Vegetable intent: "spinach", "fresh red spinach", "frozen red spinach", "organic spinach", "broccoli crowns" - these are produce searches, not supplement searches (industry ATC rates of 80-92% confirm).
- Competitor brand searches: "oxystorm" variants and "my best heart supplement red spinach" - the brand already ranks and converts on these as conquesting plays, but they belong to a separate intent bucket from the tier framework. Called out as a defensive/offensive observation rather than a growth tier.
- Chemical / off-intent: "nitric acid", "nitrous oxide", "red", "bloom pre workout", "total war pre workout" (competitor pre-workouts).

### Tier 1 Detail

Tier 1 matches the **"Low impression share + decent CVR"** pattern. Brand converts when visible. Every funnel stage (CTR, ATC, CVR) is at or above industry. The growth is locked behind impressions. The April jump (4.7% to 9.3% impression share, click share to 27%) suggests ad spend or organic ranking has improved, and there is still 1.5-2x of headroom left to the catalog-adjusted cap.

### Tier 2 Detail

Tier 2 is the **single biggest revenue opportunity by raw market size** (~$1.4M/month), but the fastest way to lose money is to scale spend before the brand has proof that the listing converts at the brand's price point against beet/NO competitors that sell at $25-35. The growth path is paid testing on the top 2-3 keywords in this tier ("nitric oxide booster", "beet root powder", "nitric oxide supplement") at a tight ACOS target, and reading the CVR signal before committing.

### Tier 3 Detail

For RS400 specifically, only "pre workout" and "pre workout powder for men" are plausible fits, and even there the product (non-stimulant, single-ingredient) doesn't match the dominant searcher expectation (caffeinated pre-workouts). Blood pressure and heart health queries fit VitaSpinach better, so Tier 3 will need to be assessed at the product level later, not in aggregate.

## Competitor Conquesting Note

The brand is already converting on Oxystorm-related queries (Barlowe's red spinach product, the most-searched competitor brand on Amazon). In April 2026, the brand captured ~12 clicks and 4 cart adds from "oxystorm" alone, plus more from "oxystorm red spinach extract" variants. This is healthy. The action here is small (raise bids modestly on these terms, add as conquesting keywords if not already in campaigns), but it should not be confused with a Tier 2 growth play.

## Insights

- **P0 (RS400 Red Spinach) converts Tier 1 traffic above industry at every funnel stage** (CTR 3.3x, CVR +28%). The premium price is not blocking conversion when the listing is shown to the right searcher. The blocker is showing up enough times - impression share is 6% against a ~16-18% adjusted cap.
- **The market is expanding.** Tier 1 search volume scaled 4x over 12 months (753/mo to 2,976/mo), and the brand's impression share is rising in parallel. April's 9.3% impression share is the highest in 12 months. The growth track is real and being captured.
- **Tier 2 is a $1.4M/month untapped category that the brand has not yet entered meaningfully.** Even capturing 1-2% of Tier 2 share would dwarf the current Tier 1 capture in absolute terms. The "beet root alternative" positioning was built for this, but the data point we don't have is whether the brand can hold conversion rate at the $58 price point against $25-35 beet/NO competitors.

## Things to Investigate in Step 4

- **Is the brand actively bidding on Tier 1 keywords, and at what cost-per-click?** If yes, the April impression share spike was paid-driven and ROAS dictates how much further it can scale. If not, impression share growth is organic and bidding will unlock more.
- **Is the brand bidding on any Tier 2 keywords (nitric oxide, beet root powder)?** The 0% share suggests no. Confirm in Step 4 and assess whether test-and-learn budget is being allocated to category exploration.
- **Tier 1 keywords presence in title/listing.** "Beet root alternative" is in the title, which is good, but Tier 1 specific terms ("red spinach extract", "red spinach powder") should be confirmed as title or backend keywords.
