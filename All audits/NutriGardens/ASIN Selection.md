## P0 Annual Trend (RS400 Red Spinach - B0FVPCKVNY)

RS400 was launched in November 2025. This is a brand-new product, not a mature listing.

| Metric | Nov 2025 | Jan 2026 | Mar 2026 | Apr 2026 |
|--------|----------|----------|----------|----------|
| Total Sales | $879 | $2,335 | $4,910 | $5,446 |
| Sessions | 81 | 314 | 392 | 416 |
| Units | 28 | 40 | 85 | 94 |
| CVR | 34.6% | 12.7% | 21.7% | 22.6% |
| Buy Box % | 100% | 98.4% | 98.2% | 96.9% |
| Avg Price | $31.41 | $58.38 | $57.76 | $57.94 |

- **6x revenue scaling in 5 months** with a clean upward trajectory. Sessions, units, and CVR are all trending up together. ROAS has improved (6.78 in Feb to 7.85-8.06 in Mar-Apr) even as ad spend grows. This is a launch finding product-market fit.
- **Price doubled** from $31.41 in Nov to $58 by Jan, where it has stabilized. The Nov CVR of 34.6% on the lower price is misleading given the tiny base (28 units). The post-stabilization CVR of 20-23% is the real signal and is healthy.
- **Buy box has slipped slightly** from 100% (Nov-Dec) to 96.9% in April. Worth watching but not yet a constraint. No competing sellers visible.

## Insights

- **Catalog is effectively a 2-product brand in one category.** Both active products are red spinach nitric oxide boosters. RS400 has overtaken VitaSpinach in sales despite being launched 5 months ago. The two products likely cannibalize each other on the same queries.
- **RS400 has substantially better ad efficiency than VitaSpinach** (ROAS 7.60 vs 3.32 over the same 3 months, TACoS 5.1% vs 8.7%). VitaSpinach is consuming more ad budget for fewer ad sales. Capital is misallocated toward the older, lower-ROAS product.

## Things to Investigate

- **Why VitaSpinach has materially worse ROAS than RS400** despite a similar product. Hypothesis: VitaSpinach has older, less efficient ad campaigns; or its price/listing converts less well. Confirm in Step 4 (Ad Analysis).
- **RS400 vs VitaSpinach cannibalization on shared queries.** Both rank for "red spinach extract" type queries. Investigate in Step 3 (SQP) whether brand impression share is being split.

## Questions for the Seller

- B074JD3QG9 went to zero in May 2025 after running ~$1-2K/month through April 2025. Was it intentionally discontinued, did it go out of stock permanently, or was it suppressed? If discontinued, was it replaced by RS400 (Nov 2025 launch timing fits a replacement narrative).
- Berry Boost (B0B8YZC6GM) has essentially zero traffic and sales. Is this a deliberate de-prioritization, or has it lost ranking? Is it worth reviving or should it be wound down.
- RS400 price jumped from $31 to $58 between Nov and Jan. Was this a deliberate repositioning after a launch promo, and is $58 the intended steady-state price.
