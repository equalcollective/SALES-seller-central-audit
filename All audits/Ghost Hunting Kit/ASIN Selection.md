# ASIN Selection - Ghost Hunting Kit (Spirit Shack)

## Data Readiness

- **Business data:** 2024-07-01 to 2026-03-29
- **Ad data:** 2026-01-09 to 2026-04-07 (89 days)
- **Analysis window:** January - March 2026
- **Note:** Ad data is only available from January 2026 onward. This does not mean ads were not running before that date, it means the data is not available.
- **Marketplace:** UK

## Seller Context

Ghost Hunting Kit (trading as Spirit Shack / spiritshack.co.uk) sells ghost hunting equipment on Amazon UK. The catalog has 114 parent ASINs, roughly 20-25 of which are their own brand. Their own-brand products are sold under the SpiritShack and HexCom/HexBox names. The remainder are third-party products (K2 EMF meters, P-SB7 Spirit Boxes, voice recorders, etc.) that they resell.

The hero products for the brand are the HexCom (word generator/ghost detector) and the newly launched HexBox (spirit box with AM/FM sweep + word bank). These are their proprietary, differentiated products.

Account-level sales over the last 12 months show clear seasonality driven by Halloween and Christmas:
- Summer trough: ~£5,200-6,700/mo (Jun-Jul 2025)
- Halloween/Christmas peak: £16,800-22,200/mo (Oct-Dec 2025)
- Post-peak: £11,500-15,000/mo (Jan-Mar 2026)

Total 3-month sales (Jan-Mar 2026): £40,330 with £2,122 total ad spend.

## Insights

- **Seasonality is pronounced and account-wide.** Account revenue swings 3-4x between summer trough (~£5,200/mo) and Oct-Dec peak (~£20,000/mo). Both HexCom and HexBox show the same pattern. This is driven by Halloween and Christmas gift-buying for ghost hunting equipment.
- **HexBox is outperforming HexCom in organic appeal.** Despite being newer (launched Sep 2025 vs Apr 2025), HexBox achieved higher peak revenue (£4,519 vs £5,200) and has stronger post-peak CVR (5% vs 1.7%). HexBox at £70-80 price point is converting at 3x the rate of HexCom, suggesting stronger product-market fit or better listing quality.
- **Ads just started.** The seller only recently began advertising (Jan 2026). Total ad spend is modest at ~£700/mo across the account. ROAS on HexCom is strong (9.1x) but HexBox ROAS is poor (1.1x), suggesting the HexBox ad campaigns need attention.
- **Multiple HexCom listings are fragmenting traffic.** Five separate parent ASINs for HexCom variants split sessions and reviews across listings. The main listing captures 74% of HexCom revenue, but the fragmentation may be hurting organic ranking.

## Things to Investigate Further

- HexBox buy box dipping to 92-95% despite being a private label product with no competing sellers. Need to check if there are pricing changes or stock issues.
- HexBox ROAS at 1.1x. Is the ad targeting relevant keywords, or is budget being wasted on irrelevant terms? Will investigate in Ad Analysis.
- HexBox sessions dropped from 1,963 (Dec) to 538 (Feb), a 73% decline, but sales only dropped 47% because CVR compensated. Is the session drop purely seasonal, or is there a visibility/ranking issue?
- Why are there 5 separate HexCom listings? Is this an intentional SEO strategy or accidental fragmentation? Will assess listing quality in Product Understanding.

## Questions for the Seller

- The account shows strong seasonality around Halloween/Christmas. Is this expected, and do you plan product launches or promotions around these peaks? Hypothesis: ghost hunting equipment naturally spikes in Q4.

- Are the 5 separate HexCom listings intentional (SEO strategy to capture different keyword variations) or would you prefer to consolidate into fewer listings?
