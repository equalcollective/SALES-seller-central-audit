# Product Understanding - P0: HexCom Ghost Kit (B0D4FCKX2F)

## Product Profile

**Product:**
- The HexCom is a handheld ITC (Instrumental Trans-Communication) word generator designed for ghost hunting. It uses a built-in EMF (electromagnetic field) reader to trigger word selection from a 2,000+ word database, displaying them on a 3.5" color backlit screen and speaking them aloud through a built-in speaker.
- Key features: EMF-triggered word selection, conversation history (last 9 words on screen), USB-C rechargeable battery (8 hours use), pocket-sized form factor, built-in speaker for audio output.
- It solves the problem of spirit communication during paranormal investigations. Traditional methods (spirit boxes sweeping AM/FM radio frequencies) produce garbled audio that is hard to interpret. The HexCom provides clear, readable words on screen and spoken aloud, making sessions more engaging and easier to document.
- People buy it because it creates an interactive, compelling experience during ghost hunts. Whether the buyer is a serious paranormal investigator or a hobbyist looking for entertainment, the device delivers real-time "responses" that drive the experience.

**Customer:**
- Ghost hunting enthusiasts in the UK, ranging from hobbyists to semi-professional paranormal investigators. Skews toward adults who participate in ghost hunts as a hobby, attend paranormal events, or run ghost tour experiences. Some buyers are gifting for Halloween or Christmas (evidenced by the strong Q4 seasonality).
- The purchase is driven by the desire for interactive, clear spirit communication during investigations, replacing older or less precise methods (radio sweep spirit boxes, basic EMF meters).

## Brand Understanding

**Brand:**
- SpiritShack is a registered UK brand (Company No. 13122684), not a generic/white-label operation. They manufacture their own proprietary products (HexCom, HexBox, TripWire, various sensor devices) alongside reselling third-party paranormal equipment.
- **Maturity:** Established. 10+ years in the paranormal field, 20,000+ customers served. They are primarily an e-commerce brand (Amazon UK + their own website spiritshack.co.uk) rather than DTC or retail.
- **Notable signals:** Professional website with worldwide shipping, active across Facebook, Twitter, Instagram, YouTube, Pinterest, LinkedIn. Sheffield-based. The website also offers paranormal services (psychic readings, spells) and content (blog, guides), positioning them as a paranormal authority, not just an equipment retailer.
- **Brand vibe:** Dark, niche, and enthusiast-driven. The HexCom's green-on-black branding with a monospace font evokes a tech/hacker aesthetic. The overall SpiritShack brand leans into spooky and occult imagery while maintaining a professional, trustworthy feel. They clearly know their audience.

- The HexCom's main differentiators are price, USB-C rechargeable battery (competitors use AA batteries), and the color screen.

**Rating:** 3.9 stars (rating_x10 = 39). Rating distribution not available in the database. Rating has declined steadily from 5.0 (Dec 2024) to 3.9 (Mar 2026). This is a notable concern: the product lost a full star as sales volume increased.

**Strengths:**
- **Images:** 9 images providing thorough coverage of the device from multiple angles. Clean, professional product photography on neutral backgrounds. The screen is clearly visible with words displayed, immediately communicating what the device does.
- **Video:** 1 video present, which is critical for a product that needs demonstration to be understood. Most competitors in this niche lack video content on Amazon.
- **Bullets:** 5 bullets present, all starting with all-caps benefit headers (SPIRIT COMMUNICATION DEVICE, BUILT-IN EMF DETECTION, etc.), which is standard best practice for scannability.

**Opportunities:**
- **Title:** 200 characters of pure keyword stuffing. The title reads: "HexCom - Ghost Hunting Equipment UK, Paranormal Equipment, Ghost Box, Spirit Box, Ghost Investigation Equipment, Ghost Equipment, Ghost Detector, EMF Reader, Paranormal Activity, Ghost Hunters, Rempod." This is a comma-separated list of search terms, not a title. It tells the shopper nothing about what the product actually does. A shopper scrolling search results sees "HexCom" and a wall of generic keywords. The word "word generator" or "word bank" (the product's core function) does not appear in the title at all.
  - **Suggested:** "HexCom Word Generator - Ghost Hunting Equipment with Built-In EMF Reader, 2000+ Word Bank, 3.5" Colour Screen, Rechargeable Battery - Spirit Communication Device by SpiritShack"
- **Bullets:** While 5 bullets are present, they are excessively long (400-500+ characters each) and extremely repetitive. The phrases "ghost hunting equipment," "paranormal," "Rem Pod," and "ghost detector" are repeated in nearly every bullet. This keyword stuffing degrades readability. A shopper trying to understand the product's actual features has to wade through redundant marketing copy. The bullets should be condensed to focus on one distinct benefit per bullet with relevant keywords woven in naturally, not force-repeated.
- **A+ Content:** Absent. For a £130 niche electronics product where the buyer needs to understand how it works before purchasing, A+ content is a significant gap. The product page should show the device in use during an actual investigation (dark room, team of investigators, screen lit up with words), explain the word generation process visually, and compare against traditional spirit boxes. This would directly address the conversion problem visible in the data (1.7-2.0% CVR).
- **No lifestyle/in-use images:** All 9 images are studio product shots from different angles. There are no images showing the HexCom being used in a ghost hunt, no images showing the screen close-up with word generation in progress, no infographic explaining features, and no comparison images. For a product where the purchase decision hinges on "does this actually work and look cool in practice," lifestyle imagery is essential.
- **Rating decline:** From 5.0 to 3.9 over 15 months. As a newer product (launched mid-2024), this trajectory is concerning. The initial 5-star reviews were likely from the first few enthusiast buyers. As volume increased, mixed reviews brought the rating down. At 3.9, it sits below the 4.0 threshold that many shoppers use as a filter. This needs investigation: what are the negative reviews saying?
