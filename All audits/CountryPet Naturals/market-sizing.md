### Target Customer

Health-conscious pet owners willing to pay premium for pharmaceutical-grade, single-ingredient supplements. Often has a dog/cat with skin issues, joint problems, or the owner simply values clean nutrition.

### Category Context

Pet supplements (fish oil / omega-3) is highly competitive. Dominated by Zesty Paws, Nordic Naturals, Grizzly, Native Pet — all with 5,000–50,000+ reviews. Cod liver oil is a premium sub-niche within fish oil, differentiated by naturally occurring Vitamins A & D alongside EPA/DHA. Key purchase drivers: purity, omega-3 concentration, sourcing, ease of dosing, review count.

### Target Customer

Premium raw/frozen dog food buyer — health-conscious dog owner who believes raw/minimally processed food is superior. Willing to pay $15/lb (vs. $2–5/lb for kibble). Often has a dog with allergies or is a picky eater. Values sourcing transparency. Skews millennial/Gen-Z pet parents who "humanize" their pet's diet.

### Category Context

Frozen/raw dog food is growing fast in pet food overall, but is a **niche within a niche on Amazon**. Critical challenges:
- **Cold chain logistics** — frozen food requires special shipping
- **Non-returnable** — food safety, eliminates trial
- **High price point** — $10–15/lb creates steep barrier
- **Most raw buyers shop elsewhere** — Chewy, local pet stores, raw food co-ops
- **Industry CVR on Amazon is terrible** — 5–7% vs. 15–17% for supplements

### Honest Assessment

This product has **fundamental channel-fit problems on Amazon:**
1. **Channel mismatch** — frozen dog food industry CVR is 5–7%; people browse here but buy on Chewy/in-store
2. **Prohibitive price** — $89.99 with no trial option and non-returnable
3. **Weak social proof** — 33 reviews at 4.0 stars for a $90 product
4. **2.0% listing CVR** — the listing itself isn't converting
5. **"Gently pasteurized" confusion** — raw purists may see pasteurization as disqualifying
6. **Tiny addressable market** — even Tier 1 + Tier 2 combined is only ~$660K/year total industry

## Market Size Methodology

- **Market Size = Average Price x Total Add-to-Carts (quarterly) x 4**
- Average price estimated at ~$28 for pet supplements (cod liver oil / fish oil category) and ~$45 for frozen dog food based on category pricing
- SQP does not provide price data directly — estimates based on competitive landscape
- All search volume and ATC figures are from Amazon SQP (Search Query Performance) data, using the most recent quarterly period available
- "Brand ATCs" = CountryPet Naturals' share of add-to-carts on that keyword
