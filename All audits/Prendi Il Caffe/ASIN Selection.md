# ASIN Selection - Prendi Il Caffe

## Data Coverage

- **Business data:** 2024-07-01 to 2026-04-19 (latest period)
- **Ad data:** 2026-02-02 to 2026-05-02 (90 days, full 3-month window)
- **Analysis window:** Feb 2026, Mar 2026, Apr 2026 (April via weekly aggregation through Apr 19)

## Catalog Overview

25 parents / 26 children. Italian coffee brand selling:
- **Espresso machines** (Spinel Ciao, Spinel Pinocchio ESE Pod) - made in Italy
- **ESE pods** (Miscela Napoli, Miscela Roma, Decaf) - 100ct and 25ct boxes
- **Whole bean and ground coffee** (Napoli medium roast, Roma dark roast)
- **Aluminum Nespresso-compatible capsules** (Forte, Napoli, Intenso)
- **Adjacent Italian foods** (Pelino confetti candy, Orsini tarallini, hot chocolate)

The catalog is concentrated: the top 4 parents drive ~70% of 3-month revenue. The two espresso machines (Ciao, Pinocchio) account for ~45% alone, with pods and ground coffee filling out the consumables side.

## Catalog-Level Annual Trend

Since both P0 (Spinel Ciao) and P1 (Pinocchio) are the dominant SKUs and parent structure changes make per-parent annual history unreliable, the account-level annual trend is the most useful lens.

| Metric | Apr 2025 | Jul 2025 | Oct 2025 | Jan 2026 | Mar 2026 |
|--------|---------|---------|---------|---------|---------|
| Total Sales | $5,354 | $3,593 | $3,938 | $4,160 | $11,601 |
| Sessions | 1,082 | 820 | 461 | 822 | 2,085 |
| CVR | 7.3% | 5.5% | 10.6% | 14.7% | 8.9% |
| Buy Box % | 73.8% | 61.2% | 50.1% | 83.1% | 97.7% |
| Ad Spend | $0 | $0 | $0 | $0 | $1,406 |

- **Buy box collapsed from April 2025 through October 2025** (down to 50-65% for six straight months). Sales held flat-to-declining despite reasonable session counts because the brand was losing the buy box on competing offers. Worst buy box month coincides with lowest sales month (Aug 2025 = $1,935).
- **Buy box recovered Nov 2025 onward** to 80%+, and sales climbed back. By Jan 2026 buy box was 83% and sales hit $4,160 on the strongest CVR of the year (14.7%) without any ads running.
- **Feb 2026 was the first month with paid advertising.** Sales doubled month-over-month ($4,160 to $8,076), then jumped again to $11,601 in March. Ads started working from day one.

## Insights

- **P3 (Miscela Napoli 100ct Pods) is the strongest organic performer in the catalog by far.** 96.6% organic sales, 32-43% CVR, near-zero ad spend. The product is selling itself at high efficiency. Pushing modest ad spend behind it should produce strong incremental ROAS.
- **P0 and P1 (the espresso machines) are doing the heavy lifting on revenue but at much lower CVR (1-2.5%).** This is normal for a $350+ machine, but it means PPC efficiency on machines will be capped by traffic quality and listing presentation. ROAS on both is 2.6-2.7x, which is workable but not strong.
- **Account growth is being driven almost entirely by ads starting in Feb 2026.** Pre-ad baseline was ~$4K/month, now running at $11K+/month. The brand is in launch mode for paid traffic.

## Things to Investigate Further

- **P0 ad spend pattern in April:** $350 in week 1, $186 in week 2, $160 in week 3, then drops to $23 in week 4. Need to confirm in Step 4 whether this is intentional pacing, budget caps, or campaigns being paused.
- **P2 (Miscela Roma ESE) has the best ROAS at 5.45x with very little spend.** April sales accelerated. Worth checking in Step 4 whether spend can be scaled here without ROAS collapse.
- **Whole Bean Coffee (B0GQ4FBDLW) buy box dropped to 71.64% in March from 92.16% in February.** Not in P0-P3 but worth flagging - investigate in Step 4 if ads are driving traffic to a listing without buy box.

## Questions for the Seller

- **The buy box collapsed for six months (Apr-Oct 2025), dropping as low as 50%.** What happened in that window? Hypothesis: pricing/MAP issue, hijacker, or stock-out problem on the main machine SKU. This is the single biggest historical event in the account and the story would help frame what we do next.
- **February 2026 was the first month of paid ads. What triggered the decision to start advertising, and what's the current monthly ad budget target?** This shapes the action plan: if budget is constrained, we prioritize ROAS; if there is room to scale, we prioritize impression share on the machines.
