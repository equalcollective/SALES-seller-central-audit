### ⚠️ Statistical Significance Note

These projections assume that current ROAS holds at scale, but the data does NOT yet support that conclusion with confidence. "Rollator with seat" has generated only **1 order from 11 clicks** — far too small a sample to draw reliable conclusions. The true conversion rate could be significantly higher or lower.

**Before acting on these numbers, we need to:**
- Run the keyword in its own campaign for 4–6 weeks to gather statistically significant data (minimum ~30–50 clicks per keyword, ideally 100+)
- Monitor whether the conversion rate holds as spend increases (ROAS almost always compresses at higher spend levels)
- Check search term reports to see what actual search queries are triggering each keyword

**The directional insight is valid** — qualified, high-intent keywords like "rollator with seat" deserve their own campaigns so they aren't starved by generic terms. But the exact revenue projections above should be treated as directional, not guaranteed.

## Broader Structural Recommendations

1. **Split by keyword intent:** Create separate campaigns for generic terms ("rollator", "mobility walker") vs. qualified terms ("rollator with seat", "foldable rollator with seat", "mobility walker with seat")
2. **Isolate top performers:** "Foldable rollator with seat" (ROAS 6.87, 2 orders) is the most statistically reliable high performer — give it its own campaign first
3. **Cut zero-order keywords:** "Mobility walker" BROAD (£15.25 spend, 0 orders), "rollator walker with seat" PHRASE (£11.25, 0 orders), "lightweight rollator" BROAD (£9.60, 0 orders) — these have spent £36.10 with zero returns over 7 months. Pause or significantly reduce bids
4. **Deduplicate:** "Rollator" runs in both BROAD and PHRASE — combined £243.71 spend for £551.29 sales (ROAS 2.26). Consolidate to one match type or separate into distinct campaigns with different bid strategies
