- The product launched in June 2025, peaked Oct-Nov at $8-9K/mo with 16%+ CVR, and has declined every month since. March 2026 sales are 57% below the Nov peak.
- CVR has dropped from 15-17% in the peak months to 11-13% now. Sessions have fallen roughly in step with sales, so the revenue drop is driven by both less traffic AND a worse conversion rate, not one isolated lever.
- March shows a new issue on top of the trend: buy box fell from 99.8% (Feb) to 85.2% (Mar). As a private-label brand with no competing sellers on the listing, the likely cause is a MAP-related suppression tied to a recent price change.

## Insights

- **P0 (Straight Razors) decline is system-wide, not a single-lever issue.** Sessions are down ~40% from peak, CVR is down ~4pp, and buy box just broke in March. Each of these independently costs sales, and together they compound. This is the biggest lever in the account and the focus of the engagement.
- **P1 (Hair Cutting Scissors) has a CVR problem, not a traffic problem.** Sessions are reasonable (800-2,800/mo) but CVR sits at 5-8%, roughly half of what the Straight Razors product was doing in its peak months. The product page is likely the blocker.
- **P2 (Pumice Stone) is a promising new launch.** 21% CVR on 1,665 sessions in March is statistically meaningful and well above the category norm. If the seller pushes traffic here, conversion is already proven.

## Things to Investigate Further

- **P0 (Straight Razors):** Is the Oct-Nov peak followed by a steady decline a seasonal pattern (men's grooming gifting in Q4) or a true demand loss? SQP search volume on razor/barber queries over 12 months will answer this.
- **P0 (Straight Razors):** What is driving the CVR drop from 16% to 12%? Candidates: rating/review drift, price changes, new competitor listings, or changes to the listing itself. Check in Step 2 via Keepa rating and price history.
- **P1 (Hair Cutting Scissors):** Feb 2026 sales were $470 on 822 sessions, then bounced back to $1,917 on 2,424 sessions in Mar. Buy box was healthy in Feb (99.5%), so what caused the February dip? Possibly stock issue or price change.
