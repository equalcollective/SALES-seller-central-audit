# Beauticone Trends - SQP Analysis (P0: Straight Razors for Men)

## Tagging Rationale

**Tier 1 (Hero):** Queries where a straight razor is exactly what the shopper wants. The product is the direct answer.
- straight razor, straight razors for men, straight edge razor, single blade razor, single blade razors for men, navajas para barbero (Spanish hero), barber razor

**Tier 2 (Core razor market):** Broader razor queries where a straight razor is one option among several (cartridge, safety, electric, one-blade). Beauticone shows up and gets clicks here, but the intent is mixed.
- razor, razors for men, shaving razor, one blade razor, razor knife

**Tier 3 (Adjacent barber space):** Broader barber/grooming supplies queries where a straight razor is one item in a larger universe of barber products (scissors, clippers, capes, etc.).
- barber, barber accessories, barber supplies, razor blades for men

**Catalog Overlap Check:** Only P0 (Straight Razors) ranks on Tier 1 queries. P1 (Hair Cutting Scissors) and P2 (Pumice Stone) are unrelated products. **Tier 1 cap stays at ~8-9%** (no adjustment needed). For Tier 3 "barber accessories" and "barber supplies," P1 (Hair Cutting Scissors) could plausibly rank in addition to P0, so the adjusted cap for Tier 3 is ~16-18%. Tier 2 cap stays at ~8-9%.

**Tier 1 deep dive:** This is a classic PPC-scaling opportunity. When Beauticone shows up on Tier 1 queries, it converts 18% better than the industry average and gets added to cart at parity. The funnel is healthy; the only broken link is top-of-funnel visibility. The brand is at 3% of a ~9% cap, so there is room to roughly 3x impression share on Tier 1 before hitting the cap. At current conversion rates, that implies a material revenue lift on P0 driven purely by more bids on the same queries they already convert on.

**Tier 2 deep dive:** The intent mismatch is real. On queries like "razors for men" (230K volume/mo), the majority of shoppers are buying Gillette-type cartridge razors, not straight razors. Beauticone's CVR on Tier 2 is 14% vs industry 24.6% because the shopper who clicks a straight razor from a broad "razor" search often bounces back. This is not a fixable listing problem; it's a query-intent problem. Recommend excluding broad Tier 2 queries from search campaigns (or using them only as negatives) and focusing budget on Tier 1.

## Insights

- **P0 (Straight Razors) primary blocker is Tier 1 impression share.** Brand converts above industry when shown (12.3% CVR vs 10.5%), but only captures ~3% of impressions versus a ~9% ceiling. Tripling impression share on the current Tier 1 query set is the single biggest growth lever in the audit.
- **P0 (Straight Razors) sales decline is a visibility problem, not a market or product problem.** Tier 1 market purchases grew from 10.7K in Apr 2025 to 14.2K in Mar 2026. Beauticone's share fell from 6% to 2% in the same window. The product converts well, the market is growing; the brand is just showing up less.
- **P0 (Straight Razors) has a real CVR moat.** Brand CVR above industry CVR at the tier level is unusual. The 4.6 rating, 61-second video, and bilingual targeting are likely doing the work. This is what makes the impression-share fix a high-confidence play: incremental traffic will convert, not burn.
- **Tier 2 broad queries are not a growth opportunity.** Large volume (486K/mo) but intent mismatch (cartridge/electric razor shoppers) means Beauticone's CVR here is 43% below industry. Spending on broad "razor" queries will waste money.

## Things to Investigate Further

- **Why did Tier 1 impression share collapse after October?** Three candidates: (a) ad spend was cut or reallocated, (b) competitors outbid on the same keywords, (c) organic rank deteriorated. Step 4 (Ad Analysis, now that ad data is uploaded) will answer (a) and (b) directly. Sales rank data from Step 2 partially supports (c): straight razor BSR moved from #10-15 (Oct-Nov) to #20 (Mar), but the magnitude of rank change does not fully account for the impression share drop, so ads are the more likely primary cause.
- **"navajas para barbero" performance is strong in the limited months it shows up.** Only 1 of 3 months in the 3-month window had data for this query, but cart adds were 138 and it's a low-competition query (263K total impressions vs 7.5M for "straight razor"). Worth checking whether this is being actively bid on in Step 4.
