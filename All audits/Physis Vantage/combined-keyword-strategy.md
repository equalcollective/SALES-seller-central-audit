---

## HOW TO READ THIS DOCUMENT

Every keyword below has been evaluated from **two independent data sources**:

1. **SQP (Search Query Performance)** — Shows organic search behavior: how many people searched, how many saw PhysiVantage, how many clicked and bought *without* ad influence. This tells us about **relevance and organic demand**.

2. **Ad Search Term Reports** — Shows paid ad performance: what happened when PhysiVantage actually spent money on these terms. This tells us about **profitability when we pay for visibility**.

When both sources agree, we have high confidence. When they disagree, we have a diagnostic signal — something is broken (creative, bid, listing) that needs fixing.

## 3. SQP SAYS YES, ADS SAY NO: Diagnose the Disconnect

These keywords have organic relevance but paid ads aren't converting. Something is broken between the organic signal and the paid execution.

### 3a. "collagen powder" — Organic Works, Paid Doesn't
| Source | Data |
|---|---|
| **SQP** | 350K–540K/quarter, 13–23 clicks, 3–6 purchases. Consistent organic converter. |
| **Ads** | $44.17 spent → 12 clicks → 1 order → **96.1% ACoS, 1.04x ROAS** |

**Diagnosis:** SQP shows 3–6 organic purchases per quarter — people DO buy PhysiVantage from this keyword. But ads generated only 1 order at nearly break-even. This means the **organic listing** (appearing naturally in results) converts, but the **ad placement** (likely lower position or different context) doesn't. Possible causes:
- Ad is appearing on page 2 or product pages where context is different
- Competitors' ads above PhysiVantage's create comparison shopping that PhysiVantage loses (review count disadvantage)

**Action:** Reduce ad bid significantly. Let organic do the work here. Allocate the $44/period to untested opportunities instead.

---

### 3b. "collagen for men" — Neither Channel Converts Well
| Source | Data |
|---|---|
| **SQP** | 60K/quarter volume, 13 clicks across 5 quarters, 0 purchases. Recommended: test only after listing optimization. |
| **Ads** | $18.67 spent → 6 clicks → 0 orders. Combined with "hydrolyzed collagen for men" ($11.12, 0 orders) = $29.79 wasted. |

**Diagnosis:** SQP correctly flagged this as Tier 3 (test after listing optimization). Ad data confirms — $30 spent across "collagen for men" variants with zero returns. PhysiVantage's listing doesn't resonate with the "collagen for men" searcher. The product IS relevant (athletic positioning appeals to men), but the listing's current creative/imagery/copy doesn't communicate this.

**Action:** STOP all ad spend on men's collagen terms immediately. Save $30/period. Only re-test after creating male-athlete-focused A+ content and potentially a male-oriented main image variation.

## THE ONE-PAGE TAKEAWAY

**What SQP tells us that ads can't:** Where PhysiVantage has organic relevance and right-to-win — "collagen for athletes" (3.75% share), "bcaa and collagen powder" (perfect PMF), "tendon and ligament supplements" (1–2.5% share). These are proven organic signals that ads haven't captured yet.

**What ads tell us that SQP can't:** What converts when we actually pay for clicks — climbing-specific terms (100% CVR), SENDURE-X cross-product terms (15–34x ROAS), knee/tennis elbow recovery terms. These are too low-volume for SQP but prove real purchase intent.

**Where they agree most strongly:** Branded defense (both 15x+ efficient), "collagen" head term (both profitable), and the tendon/ligament cluster (organic relevance + paid conversion). These are no-brainer investments.

**Where they agree something is broken:** "collagen for men" (0 purchases in both), "collagen powder" (organic converts, ads don't), and generic pre-workout terms (wrong product targeting). Fix or kill.

**The single biggest opportunity:** The 6 SQP-confirmed keywords with near-zero ad spend represent the widest gap between signal strength and investment. Combined, they have $11.55 in historical ad spend against quarters of strong organic data. Funding these alone could add 15–25 incremental orders/month at 8–15x ROAS.
