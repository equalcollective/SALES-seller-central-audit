# Blue Sky Studios - SQP Analysis (P0 Wand Pen)

## Tagging Rationale

**Tier 1 (Hero):** Queries where the customer is searching specifically for a wand-shaped pen or pen set themed on Harry Potter. The product is the exact answer. 14 queries tagged. Examples: "harry potter wand pen", "harry potter wand pen set of 4", "wand pen", "harry potter levitating wand pen".

**Tier 2 (Core market):** "Harry Potter pen" general queries where the wand pen is one of several Harry Potter pen options the customer might pick. The notebook & pen set (P2) also competes here, so the brand has 2 products that can rank for these queries. 4 queries tagged: "harry potter pen", "harry potter pens", "harry potter notebook and pen", "harry potter quill pen".

**Tier 3 (Broad/adjacent):** "Harry Potter gifts" queries. The wand pen is one of many Harry Potter gift options, alongside the Self-Stirring Cauldron Mug (P3), Hogwarts backpacks, A5 notebook & pen sets (P2), desk mats, lunch bags, and more across the catalog. 3 queries tagged: "harry potter gifts", "harry potter gifts for women", "harry potter gifts for girls".

**Excluded - intent mismatch:** Queries like "harry potter wand", "harry potter wand that shoots fire", "elder wand", "draco malfoy wand" represent customers searching for toy wand replicas, not pens. The brand has 2-4% impression share on these but converts at industry parity at best, often much worse. The wand pen is structurally not the answer to those searches. Excluded from tier analysis. If the brand wants to capture toy wand demand, that requires a different product, not better marketing.

### Catalog Overlap Check (Phase 5)

- **Tier 1:** The wand pen parent has 4 children (single levitating wand, triple pack, quad pack), and Amazon will surface multiple variants for the same query. Effective cap adjusted to **~16-18%** (2 variants ranking).
- **Tier 2:** Wand pen + Notebook & Pen Set (P2) both rank for "harry potter pen" type queries. Effective cap **~16-18%**.
- **Tier 3:** 5+ Harry Potter products across the catalog (wand pen, mug, notebook & pen set, backpack, lunch bag, mood lights, mini lamp, etc.) can all rank for "harry potter gifts" queries. Effective cap **~24-27%**.

### Seasonality

The market is intensely Q4-seasonal across all tiers:
- **Tier 1** search volume: 621-789 (April-July) -> 3,131 (December) -> 1,130 (March). Peak is **5x off-season**.
- **Tier 3** search volume: 52K-65K (April-August) -> 355K (December) -> 49K (March). Peak is **7x off-season**.

This matches the seller's revenue pattern from Step 1 (P0 went £203 in May 2025 -> £35,891 in December 2025). The product is market-seasonal, not brand-seasonal. Q4 is the play.

**Tier 1 detail:** This is the strongest signal in the audit. Brand CTR is 67% above industry, brand CVR is 75% above industry, and brand cart-to-purchase is 14pp above industry. Whenever the wand pen shows up in a Tier 1 search, it wins. The only thing limiting growth is visibility, and visibility on Tier 1 is a deterministic PPC problem.

**Tier 2 detail:** The wand pen doesn't dominate Tier 2 the way it dominates Tier 1 because it is competing with all Harry Potter pen formats (quill pens, regular character pens, novelty pens). The brand is "fine" at industry rates here, so scaling impressions translates linearly into more cart adds. The notebook & pen set (P2) should also be in this lift since it can rank for the same queries.

**Tier 3 detail:** This tier is mostly a Q4 lever. Outside of Q4, the search volume is one-tenth of peak, and the brand is at industry parity. The growth path is to walk into Q4 2026 with a sharper gift-focused listing, gift-driven A+ content, and PPC budget ready to scale on "harry potter gifts" while seasonal demand surges. This benefits the entire HP catalog, not just the wand.

## Insights

- **P0 (Quad Wand Pen Pack) wins Tier 1 by every funnel measure, the constraint is visibility.** 33% purchase share vs 11% impression share means every additional impression converts at a 3:1 ratio above the market. PPC bidding on Tier 1 queries should be the highest-ROAS lever in the account ahead of Q4.
- **The wand pen ramped Tier 1 impression share from 4% to 13% organically in 2025 with zero ad spend.** This is uncommon. With paid spend layered in for Q4 2026, hitting the ~16-18% adjusted cap on Tier 1 should be the working assumption, not a stretch.
- **Tier 3 ("harry potter gifts") is the brand-level Q4 prize, not a P0-only opportunity.** 114K monthly searches at peak with 4% brand impression share. The growth path is a coordinated catalog play across the wand pen, mug, notebook & pen set, and other HP gifts, ramped up Sep-Dec.

## Things to Investigate Further

- **Tier 2 impression share decline (8.6% Dec -> 4.3% Mar):** Step 4 should check whether the ad data shows the brand cutting bids on "harry potter pen" queries after Christmas, or whether competitors have stepped up paid presence. Both would explain the share drop.
- **Whether the brand currently bids on Tier 1 wand-pen queries in ads at all:** Step 4 will examine spend allocation against Tier 1 vs Tier 2 vs Tier 3 to confirm where current budget is going. The hypothesis is that Tier 1 is under-bid given its strong organic share.
- **Whether competing wand pen sellers exist on Amazon UK that could close the cap:** If the cap is really 16-18% and competitors hold the rest, fine. If the rest is held by toy wand replicas that don't directly compete, the brand may have effective cap headroom higher than 16-18% on Tier 1.
