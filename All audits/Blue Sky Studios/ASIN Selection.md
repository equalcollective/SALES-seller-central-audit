### Notes on products not prioritized

- **Many tail products with sub-£1K 2-month sales.** The catalog is long-tail by design (licensee/IP play). The next ~6 products (£500-£1.4K) are mostly desk and homeware (mood lights, mug & sock gift sets, pen holders, drink bottles, mini lamps). They follow the same playbook as P1-P3 (multi-IP variants under one parent), so insights on P1-P3 will largely transfer.
- **40+ parents launched in 2025 (B0F* and B0G* ASINs) have no sales yet.** This is a pattern, not an anomaly: Blue Sky Studios appears to launch SKUs in batches and let some lag. Worth surfacing on the call but not productive to deep-dive each one.

## P0 Annual Trend (Wand)

The wand has the most dramatic story in the catalog. From Apr 2025 (launch month, £385) it grew 90x by Dec 2025 (£35,891), then collapsed to ~£3.5K/mo in Feb-Mar 2026. The brand started running ads on this ASIN only in **January 2026**, after the entire Q4 explosion was already organic.

- **Q4 holiday seasonality is the dominant driver.** Combined Nov+Dec 2025 sales were £53.7K, more than the entire account did in Feb+Mar 2026 combined (£67K). This is a gift product, peak demand is Christmas. The 2-month "current" window we are using to prioritize is the bottom of that seasonal cycle.
- **Buy box collapsed to 53.7% in November during peak demand,** then recovered to 98.9% in December. Whatever caused the November dip (stockout, MAP issue, listing suppression) likely cost a large amount of sales during the most valuable month of the year. Note the "parent-level" buy box is sessions-weighted across all 4 children, so the underlying child driving the dip would need to be identified separately.
- **Ads were never run during the Q4 ramp.** Q4 growth was 100% organic. Ads were turned on in Jan 2026 (£130 spend) and scaled to £758 in March. ROAS in March is 3.19, which is healthy. There is significant headroom to lean into ads ahead of the next Q4 cycle.

### Hero is a child, not the parent

- Parent B0FT54R9CP has 4 children: a Levitating Wand single (B0F2JQ63JB), a Quad Wand Pack (B0F9FRV1MM), a Triple Wand Pack (B0F2JQCYK8), and the parent-as-child placeholder.
- **The Quad Wand Pack drives 91% of parent sales** (£6,184 of £6,772 in Feb+Mar). Buy box at child level is 99.9%. The 70.7% parent-level buy box is being dragged down by older/legacy children, not the actual hero variant.
- For Step 2 (Product Understanding) and beyond, the focus child is **B0F9FRV1MM (Quad Wand Pack, £13.92 avg price)**.

## Insights

- **P0 (Wand) is a confirmed Q4 gift seasonal play that is currently hibernating.** The opportunity for the call is not "fix the current £3.5K/mo run rate," it is "prepare the brand for the next Q4 to capture more of the demand than they did last year." Last Christmas they did £35.9K in December with zero ads. With ads, listing optimization, and a buy box fix, that ceiling is materially higher.
- **The catalog is a licensee aggregator model.** Most multi-child parents are organized as "one product format, many IPs as variants" (e.g., the Desk Mat parent has 10 children: Harry Potter Hogwarts, Harry Potter Ollivanders, Spongebob, TMNT, Transformers, Sanrio, Pochacco, Kuromi, Snoopy, Emily in Paris). This is a scalable, low-marginal-cost expansion play, but it also means each variant is competing in a different keyword universe. The P1 desk mat audit will likely find that one variant (Harry Potter) carries most of the parent.
- **P3 (Cauldron Mug) has a critical buy box issue: 49.8% at parent level, declining 18% Feb to Mar.** This is a private-label-style listing (Blue Sky Studios IP via Harry Potter license), so a likely cause is MAP / sudden price changes triggering buy box suppression. This is a question for the seller in addition to a flag for child-level investigation.

## Things to Investigate Further

- **P0 (Wand) parent-level buy box of 70.7%.** The hero child (Quad Wand Pack) is 99.9%. Need to identify which child is dragging the average and whether it's the same root cause as the November 2025 BB collapse.
- **P1 (Desk Mat) child-level winners.** With 10 IP variants under one parent, identifying which 2-3 variants drive most sales will sharpen any listing or PPC recommendations. Will be addressed in subsequent steps if needed.

## Questions for the Seller

- **P0 (Wand) - November 2025 buy box drop to 53.7%:** What happened? Stockout, MAP price triggered by a deal, or competing FBM offers? This is the single biggest lever for next Q4 if it can be prevented from recurring.
- **P0 (Wand) - Q4 fulfillment readiness for 2026:** Last December they sold 3,069 units organic. With ads layered in for Q4 2026, the volume could 1.5-3x. Is inventory and licensing capacity in place to absorb that?
- **P3 (Cauldron Mug) - 49.8% buy box, no competing sellers expected on a licensed product:** Have there been recent price changes that could have triggered MAP-related buy box suppression?
- **Catalog launch cadence:** 40+ recently launched parents (B0F*/B0G*) have no meaningful sales yet. Is this a deliberate "throw against the wall" approach, or is there a launch playbook that has stalled?
