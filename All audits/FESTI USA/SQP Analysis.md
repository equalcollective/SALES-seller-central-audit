# SQP Analysis: P0 - Balloon Shine Spray

## Tagging Rationale

**Tier 1 (Hero):** Queries where the customer is searching for exactly a balloon shine spray. The product is the direct answer. These are the must-win keywords.
> "balloon shine spray", "balloon shine", "balloon spray shine", "spray balloon shine", "balloon shiner"

**Tier 2 (Core market):** Broader balloon spray/shine queries, including competitor-branded terms (hi shine, megashine) and longer-tail variations. Same customer need, wider competitive set.
> "balloon spray", "balloon shine spray latex balloons", "hi shine balloon spray", "balloon spray to last and shine", "megashine balloon spray", "balloon gloss spray", "party shine balloon shine spray", "high shine balloon spray", "shiny balloon spray", "hishine balloon spray"

**Tier 3 (Adjacent):** Functional queries (prevention-focused) and broader gloss terms. The product can serve these searches, but the intent is slightly different.
> "balloon spray to prevent popping", "balloon gloss", "balloon shine spray for outdoor", "balloon spray shine to prevent popping", "balloon gloss shine spray", "balloon shine spray indoor"

## Blockers & Growth Path

### Tier 1 (Hero queries, $223K/mo market)

**Primary blocker: Impression share (1.4% of ~9% max)**
The brand simply isn't showing up enough. During the high season (May-Jun), impression share reached 2.5-3.5% with corresponding higher purchase counts (163-245 brand purchases/mo). In the current off-season, it has fallen to 1.2-1.4%.

**Secondary blocker: CTR (0.78% brand vs 1.85% industry)**
When the brand does show up, it gets clicked at less than half the industry rate. This is surprising given the strong main image. Possible explanations: (1) price appears higher than smaller-format competitors at a glance, (2) competitors like Hi-Shine have stronger brand recognition in search results, (3) ad placements may be in lower-CTR positions (Rest of Search, Product Pages vs Top of Search).

**CVR is below industry but manageable:** Brand CVR averages 14.8% vs industry 20.9% (29% gap). However, absolute conversion numbers are thin in winter months, making the rate less reliable. During the high season, brand CVR tracked much closer to industry.

**Growth path:** This matches **Pattern 1 (Low impression share + decent conversion history)** from the high-season data. The brand converts when it shows up, and it converts at scale during peak months. The play is:
1. PPC scaling to increase impression share on Tier 1 keywords (balloon shine spray, balloon shine, balloon spray shine)
2. Address CTR gap through better ad placements (Top of Search bid adjustments) and potentially price positioning/coupon strategy to compete visually on the search results page
3. Time the scaling with the seasonal ramp (start scaling in March to capture the April-June peak)

### Tier 2 (Core market, $122K/mo market)

**Primary blocker: Impression share (1.4%)**
Same visibility gap as Tier 1.

**Secondary blocker: CTR (0.72% brand vs 1.79% industry)**
Same pattern, slightly worse.

**CVR is actually at parity with industry:** Brand CVR averages 18.0% vs industry 17.5%. When the brand shows up and gets clicked on Tier 2 queries, it converts at the same rate as the market. This is a strong signal that the product is relevant to these searches.

**Growth path:** Also **Pattern 1**. The CVR parity makes Tier 2 an even cleaner PPC scaling opportunity than Tier 1. Bidding on terms like "balloon spray", "hi shine balloon spray", "megashine balloon spray" should yield strong returns because the product already converts at industry rates when it gets traffic.

### Tier 3 (Adjacent, $38K/mo market)

**Data too thin for reliable conclusions.** Brand received only 7 clicks and 1 purchase across all Tier 3 queries in Feb 2026. Rates based on these numbers are not actionable. The impression share (0.9%) and CTR (0.5%) follow the same pattern as Tiers 1-2, but the smaller market and lower data volume make this a lower priority. Not worth investing in until Tier 1 and Tier 2 are scaled.

## Insights

- P0 (Balloon Shine Spray) sits in a $383K/mo total addressable market across all tiers, with $223K/mo on Tier 1 alone. At current purchase share of 0.4%, the brand is capturing only ~$900/mo from search on Tier 1. Even at a modest 2% purchase share (still well below the 9% cap), Tier 1 alone would yield ~$4,500/mo from search
- The seasonal pattern is the most important strategic factor. The market is 3x larger in May-June vs December-February. Every dollar of optimization done now will compound 3x during the peak. The brand should be scaling PPC starting March/April, not pausing it (as it did from Feb 1 onward)
- Tier 2 CVR at industry parity is the best signal in the data. These queries ("balloon spray", "hi shine balloon spray", "megashine balloon spray") represent $122K/mo of market and the brand converts at industry rates. This is low-hanging fruit for PPC

## Things to Investigate Further

- CTR gap is the biggest puzzle. The main image is strong, the product is differentiated (largest can). Need to check in ad data whether the CTR gap is driven by poor ad placements (product pages and rest of search have inherently lower CTR) rather than a listing issue
- The brand had 3.5% impression share in June at its peak. How much of that was organic vs ad-driven? If ads were not running in June, the organic ranking was driving that share. If ads were driving it, pausing ads directly caused the share collapse

## Questions for the Seller

- Search volume for balloon shine spray triples from winter to spring/summer. Are you planning to restart ad campaigns before the seasonal ramp? The current gap (ads paused since Feb 1) means the brand will miss the start of the peak season if ads don't resume by late March
