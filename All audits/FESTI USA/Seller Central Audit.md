# Seller Central Audit: FESTI USA (DECOCHAMP)

**Audit date:** March 20, 2026
**Hero product (P0):** Balloon Shine Spray (DecoShine)
**Brand:** DECOCHAMP (distributed by FESTI USA, Houston TX)

---

## Executive Summary

FESTI USA sells professional balloon decoration products under the DECOCHAMP brand. The hero product, DecoShine Balloon Shine Spray, sits in a **$383K/mo total addressable market** but currently captures less than **0.5% purchase share** on its core search terms. The brand has a strong product (largest can size in the category, 4.3-star rating, excellent listing) and a proven wholesale distribution network. The gap is almost entirely on the **visibility and traffic side**, not the product or conversion side.

The account has three major issues:
1. **Ads stopped running entirely from Feb 1**, right before the seasonal ramp begins in March
2. **81% of ad spend went to unprofitable campaigns** (0.88 overall ROAS vs 1.5x break-even)
3. **Buy box instability** on the Helium Tank (6-13%) is leaving the biggest revenue product exposed

The opportunity is significant: fix the ad structure, restart before peak season, and this account can 3-5x its current revenue trajectory.

## P0 Deep Dive: Balloon Shine Spray

### The Product

DecoShine is a 20.28 oz (600ml) aerosol balloon shine spray. It gives latex balloons a high-gloss finish and protects against oxidation. No-wipe formula, unscented. The largest single-can format on the market (competitors top out at 16.9 oz). Priced at ~$16 per unit.

The target customer is professional balloon decorators and event planners who need fast, reliable shine for arches, garlands, and large balloon installations. Secondary audience is DIY party planners for weddings, birthdays, and baby showers.

### The Listing (Strong)

The listing is one of the strongest in the category:
- 7 images with an excellent main image (before/after shine effect)
- 9 videos (1 brand + 8 customer/influencer)
- 5 well-structured bullets covering all key selling points
- A+ Content with 6 image modules
- 4.3-star rating, stable

**This is not a conversion problem. The listing does not need fixing before scaling traffic.**

**Seasonality:** The market is highly seasonal. Search volume peaks May-June at 3x the winter trough. The seller's own revenue pattern mirrors this exactly: $23K/mo in June 2025 vs $2.9K/mo in February 2026. This is market-driven, not a brand-specific problem.

The brand has ~1.4% impression share against a max of ~8-9%. There is 5-6x room to grow just by showing up more.

### What's Blocking Growth

**Primary blocker: Visibility.** The brand shows up on only 1.4% of relevant searches. During the peak season (June 2025), impression share was 3.5% and the brand was doing $23K/mo. Even that was less than half the theoretical max.

**Secondary blocker: CTR.** When the brand does show up, it gets clicked at 0.78% vs industry average of 1.85%. This is likely driven by ad placement mix (product pages and rest-of-search placements have inherently lower CTR) rather than a listing issue, since the main image and title are strong.

**CVR is not a blocker on Tier 2.** Brand CVR matches industry on Tier 2 queries (18% vs 17.5%). On Tier 1, brand CVR is slightly below industry (14.8% vs 20.9%) but this gap narrows during the high season when sample sizes are larger.

## Key Insights

1. **The biggest immediate opportunity is restarting ads before peak season.** Balloon shine spray search volume triples from March to May. Every week without ads during the ramp-up is lost market share that compounds through the peak. The brand should be scaling PPC now, not sitting dark

2. **The Helium Tank is the hidden revenue monster.** It generated $35.5K in 3 months with zero ad spend, but the buy box is at 6-13%. This product alone did $27.4K in February (Valentine's). If buy box can be recovered to even 50%, this becomes a six-figure product

3. **$1,071 in ad spend was completely wasted on Big Head Cardboard Cutout campaigns** (0.04-0.52 ROAS). An additional $336 was wasted on the Silver Glitter variant. That is $1,407 that could have gone to the one profitable DecoShine campaign at 1.62 ROAS, generating $2,279 in sales instead of $297

4. **The brand has wholesale distribution through multiple balloon supply retailers.** This is both an advantage (brand recognition in the decorator community) and a potential buy box risk (other retailers may be selling the same ASINs on Amazon, competing for the buy box)

## Plan of Action

### Weeks 1-2: Immediate Actions (PPC)

- **Restart P0 ads immediately.** Reactivate the profitable "DecoShine Manual balloon spray shine NS" campaign and scale budget 2-3x. This campaign has proven 1.62 ROAS and was under-budgeted
- **Pause all Big Head Cardboard Cutout campaigns.** $1,071 in wasted spend at 0.12 ROAS
- **Pause Silver Glitter variant (B0D4RBM6NH) campaigns.** $336 at 0.26 ROAS
- **Lower bids on generic hero keywords.** "Balloon shine spray" had $3.23 CPC, which is unsustainable for a $16 product. Target $1.50-2.00 CPC
- **Scale branded search term bids.** "Deco shine balloon spray" and "decoshine balloon spray" are converting at 18-33% CVR. These should get more budget
- **Investigate Helium Tank buy box.** Determine who else holds the buy box and whether pricing, inventory, or fulfillment changes can recover it

### Weeks 2-4: Short-Term Optimizations

- **Launch Tier 2 keyword campaigns.** "Balloon spray", "hi shine balloon spray", "megashine balloon spray" have industry-parity CVR for the brand. Create dedicated campaigns with 3-5 keywords each, proper EXACT/PHRASE separation
- **Add Top of Search bid modifiers.** CTR is the secondary blocker. Top of Search placements have inherently higher CTR. Add 25-50% Top of Search bid adjustments on the profitable campaigns to shift spend toward better placements
- **Consolidate campaign management.** The TGA campaigns (well-structured) and theppclab campaigns (overstuffed, unprofitable) appear to be running without coordination. One management approach is needed

### Weeks 4-6: Medium-Term Growth

- **Scale P0 aggressively into peak season.** By April, search volume will be 2x current levels. The campaigns should be optimized and proven by then, ready to absorb the traffic surge
- **Review Life Size Cardboard Cutout ad strategy.** At 1.07-1.31 ROAS, these campaigns are borderline. The "cardboard cutout life size" search term has 3.38 ROAS, but the "custom cardboard cutout" term is at 0.86 ROAS. Surgical optimization could make this product line profitable
- **Listing-level A+ content refresh for P0.** While the listing is strong, adding a comparison module (DecoShine vs competitors) and expanding alt text for SEO could incrementally improve organic ranking

### Weeks 6-8: Scaling and Refinement

- **Evaluate Tier 3 expansion.** After Tier 1 and Tier 2 are scaled and performing, test Tier 3 keywords ("balloon spray to prevent popping", "balloon gloss") with small budgets
- **Explore Sponsored Brand / Sponsored Display.** The current account only runs Sponsored Product campaigns. Sponsored Brand (headline search) on "balloon shine spray" would increase brand visibility and CTR
- **Assess P1 (Helium Tank) for ad viability.** If buy box is recovered, test advertising on this product. At $35K+ organic sales with 6-13% buy box, even modest ad spend with a healthy buy box could unlock massive revenue

## Growth Levers

1. **Restarting ads before peak season** (April-June): The market triples in size. Being present with optimized campaigns during the peak could take P0 from $3-4K/mo to $10-15K/mo based on historical peak performance ($23K in June 2025 with only 3.5% impression share)

2. **Reallocating wasted ad spend:** $1,407 currently going to campaigns with 0.04-0.52 ROAS. Redirected to the profitable DecoShine campaign at 1.62 ROAS, this generates ~$2,280 in sales per period

3. **Helium Tank buy box recovery:** Currently at 6-13% buy box on a product doing $35.5K in 3 months. Recovering to 50% buy box on a product this popular could unlock $50-100K+ in quarterly revenue with zero ad spend

4. **Tier 2 PPC scaling:** $122K/mo market where the brand converts at industry parity but has only 1.4% impression share. Conservative 3% impression share target on Tier 2 alone could add $3-5K/mo in attributed sales

5. **Seasonal preparation:** The single biggest growth lever is timing. Every optimization made in March-April compounds through the May-June peak. Last year the brand peaked at $23K/mo for Balloon Shine Spray alone. With better ad structure, that peak is beatable

## Questions for the Seller

1. **Why did ads stop on Feb 1?** The seasonal ramp starts in March. Every week dark is lost market share going into peak season

2. **Who manages the theppclab campaigns?** Their Big Head Cutout campaigns spent $1,071 at 0.12 ROAS. Are these being monitored?

3. **Helium Tank buy box at 6-13%:** Who else is selling this product on your listing? Is this a 3P seller issue, or is Amazon itself competing? What steps have been taken to recover the buy box?

4. **Are the Cardboard Cutout products strategic?** Combined they spent $5,127 in ad spend over 2 months with sub-1.5x ROAS. Would you consider pausing these campaigns to redirect budget to the profitable Balloon Shine Spray campaigns?

5. **Wholesale distribution and Amazon buy box:** DECOCHAMP products are distributed through multiple balloon supply retailers. Are any of those retailers also selling on Amazon and competing for your buy box? The Jul-Sep 2025 buy box collapse on Balloon Shine Spray (from 67% to 25%) may have been caused by this
