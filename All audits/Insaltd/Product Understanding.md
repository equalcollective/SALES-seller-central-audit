# Step 2: Product Understanding — P0 (Insaltd Electrolytes)

P0 hero parent: **B0GC18NZC4** ("Electrolytes")
Hero child for analysis: **B0GC1LNJ76** (Variety Pack) — drives 52% of parent revenue

## Product Profile

**Product:**
- Sugar-free, stevia-free electrolyte powder hydration packets, sold as single-flavor 28-packs and a 12-stick variety pack (3 of each flavor: Citrus, Mango Passionfruit, Grapefruit, Lemon Ginger)
- Per-serving electrolyte composition: **900mg sodium, 300mg potassium, 100mg magnesium, 18mg calcium**. This is in the same high-sodium tier as LMNT (1,000mg), well above mass-market brands like Liquid IV (~500mg)
- Differentiator: sweetened with **Glyvia™**, a proprietary blend of glycine (an amino acid) and Reb M. Glycine doubles as a hydration co-factor (supports electrolyte absorption per the bullets). The "no stevia aftertaste" promise is the brand's central positioning hook
- Zero calorie, zero sugar, gluten-free; safe for pregnant/breastfeeding per bullets
- "2026 revamped formula" referenced in the bullets, focused on improved solubility
- Subscribe & Save eligible

**Customer:**
- Active health-conscious adults (estimated 25-45) using electrolytes for keto, intermittent fasting, running, HIIT, and brain fog. Brand DTC site explicitly positions for **Hyrox athletes** (functional-fitness competitors)
- Secondary use case visible in the older listing's title: hangover prevention/relief
- Core purchase driver: people who want LMNT-tier electrolyte content without stevia. A customer-uploaded video on the older listing is literally titled **"Good frugal alternative to LMNT"**, which is the brand's natural market positioning

**Brand:**
- Registered brand with trademark, full DTC presence at **getinsaltd.com**, also distributed through specialty retailers (NutriChem, Body Energy Club)
- Influencer-driven marketing: dedicated landing pages for Cole Shoults, Eminent Holistics; Amazon listings carry videos from Aidan Chin (running) and Sarah Beers (review). This is a brand that has done its content groundwork off-Amazon
- Brand vibe: athletic/functional, slightly cheeky (the wordplay "Insaltd" = "in-salted" / "insulted"). Targets serious-but-not-clinical hydration users. Not as polished as LMNT, more approachable than Pedialyte
- Brand store on Amazon: Yes

**Competitive Landscape:**

Price positioning: **LMNT 30-stick variety ~$45 ($1.50/serving) | Insaltd 12-stick variety ~$20 ($1.67/serving) | Insaltd 28-stick single ~$40 ($1.43/serving)**. Insaltd sits at price parity with LMNT on a per-serving basis, but with a smaller starter pack to reduce trial friction.

## Insights

- **P0 (Electrolytes) competes directly with LMNT on spec, undercuts on entry-tier trial price, and is the only credible "no-stevia" alternative in the high-sodium tier.** This is the brand's wedge and should be the headline copy in any new ad creative or listing optimization.
- **P0 (Electrolytes) listing has solid bones (title, bullets, premium A+, brand store) but missing seller-uploaded video.** The asset already exists on the older parent ASIN; this is a 1-2 hour fix with non-trivial CVR impact.
- **P0 (Electrolytes) rating dropped from 4.4 to 3.7 between Apr 10 and Apr 23** while organic traffic was scaling. Rating is now the most important conversion variable. PPC scaling without first stabilizing rating will deliver lower ROAS and could amplify negative-review velocity.

## Things to Investigate

- **Cause of the April rating drop on P0 (Electrolytes).** Review the most recent 1-3 star reviews on B0GC1LNJ76 and the other children. Likely candidates: 2026 reformulation feedback, taste expectations vs. the "no stevia" promise, packet portioning, or shipping/melting in transit. Determines whether the fix is product, listing, or operations.
- **Whether the new parent (B0GC18NZC4) genuinely lacks seller videos**, or whether the Keepa snapshot is stale. If the videos are missing, re-attaching them is one of the fastest CVR levers available on this audit.
- **Variant-level CVR gap on Grapefruit (5.6% in March vs 13-19% on other flavors)** — confirm in Step 3 whether this is a search-term mismatch (people clicking through for grapefruit but expecting a different product), a price disparity, or a flavor-rating issue.
