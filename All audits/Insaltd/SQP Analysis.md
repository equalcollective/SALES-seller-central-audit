# Step 3: SQP Analysis — P0 (Insaltd Electrolytes)

P0 hero parent: **B0GC18NZC4** ("Electrolytes")

## Tagging Rationale

The product wedge from Step 2 is a **high-sodium, no-stevia, sugar-free electrolyte powder packet**. Tags reflect three concentric rings of intent.

- **Tier 1 (Hero):** Queries where someone wants exactly an electrolyte/hydration powder packet, often filtered by sugar-free or stevia-free intent. The brand is a direct answer.
  - electrolytes powder packets, electrolyte packets, sugar free electrolyte powder packets, electrolytes without stevia, zero sugar electrolytes, hydration packets, instant hydration packets
- **Tier 2 (Core market):** Broader electrolyte category. Customer wants electrolytes, not specifically packets or sugar-free. The brand is one of many viable answers.
  - electrolytes, electrolytes powder, electrolyte powder, electrolyte, electrolyte drink, hydration powder
- **Tier 3 (Adjacent / lifestyle):** Use-case-driven queries (keto, fasting, running) and ingredient queries (glycine powder) where the brand is one possible solution among many.
  - keto electrolytes, intermittent fasting, running accessories, marathon essentials, iv hydration packets, glycine powder

**Excluded queries (intentional):**
- **FSA/HSA queries** (3.2M search volume on "fsa eligible items only list" alone) — Insaltd does not appear to be FSA/HSA-eligible based on listing content. Brand impressions on these queries are accidental and the product isn't a real answer for that searcher.
- **LMNT and competitor brand queries** (~250K+ monthly volume) — these are **conquest** opportunities, separate from organic Tier 1/2/3. Treated separately below.
- **Off-category queries** ("transparent labs", "perfect amino", "true grapefruit packets", "stevia", "zero water", "flav city") — accidental impressions. Skipped.

## Catalog Overlap

P0 is the only active product in the brand's catalog (the older B0CBCJQRM1 parent is effectively dead since Feb 2026). ### Critical caveat: the 12-month data is mostly the OLD listing

Of the 145 Tier 1 brand clicks across 12 months, ~120 came from the dead B0CBCJQRM1 between Apr-Nov 2025. The new B0GC18NZC4 has produced only ~19 SQP clicks (Feb-Mar 2026). So the 12-month rates above describe the **old listing's performance, not the new one.**

This matters for two reasons:

1. **The old listing genuinely had CTR/CVR problems**, not just an impression-share problem. CTR was 41% below industry, ATC rate 70% below, CVR 86% below. Combined with the buy box collapse in Dec 2025, this is consistent with a listing that was structurally underperforming — likely part of why the seller relisted.

2. **The new listing's PDP-level CVR (16-26% per Seller Analytics) is at or above industry CVR (14.71%).** That's a significant inversion of the old listing's signal. The relist appears to have fixed real conversion problems, not just dodged a listing-health issue. The new listing is on stronger footing than the old one ever was.

### Implication

**The PPC growth thesis still holds, but the calibration changes.** PPC scaling on the old listing would have wasted spend (CVR 86% below industry would have produced terrible ROAS). PPC scaling on the new listing should perform meaningfully better, because the new listing's organic CVR is already at industry parity. The action plan can launch with reasonable confidence that traffic will convert — provided the rating slide (Section 3 of the consolidated audit) is stabilized first, since a falling rating would push the new listing back toward the old listing's CVR profile.

**Important nuance per CLAUDE.md branded keyword guidance:** Insaltd's own branded volume appears low (no significant "insaltd" queries in SQP top results), so a small (2-3%) defensive campaign on Insaltd's own name will be sufficient. The growth lever is conquest + non-branded, not own-branded.

## Insights

- **P0 (Electrolytes) impression share is essentially zero across every tier (<0.01% vs ~8% cap).** The brand is invisible to mainstream Amazon search demand. Combined with the zero ad spend from Step 1, this is the single biggest growth lever in the entire audit. The Tier 1 market alone is $2.18M/month — capturing 1% of cart-adds = $21K/month incremental, capturing 3% = $65K/month.
- **Brand growth Jan-Mar 2026 ran counter to category seasonality.** The category is in seasonal trough, yet the brand's revenue scaled 28x in the quarter. This means the listing has organic momentum independent of market tailwind. Adding PPC + entering the Q2-Q3 hydration peak compounds two upward forces simultaneously.
- **Conquest on LMNT-branded queries is a meaningful sub-lever.** ~500K+ monthly searches on LMNT and direct competitors, where Insaltd has a real defensible answer ("no stevia, same sodium"). Currently zero capture.
