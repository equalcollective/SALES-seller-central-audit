# Step 1: ASIN Selection - Olive Oil Lovers U.S.

## Context (from First Call)

- 25-year-old Italian specialty food import business (Olio2go). Wholesaler/importer model.
- Catalog: 537 child ASINs across 497 parents. 274 had any sales in the last 3 months. The rest are dormant or dead.
- Revenue collapsed from ~$20k/month historically to $4-5k/month currently. Drop attributed to new competing sellers on shared listings and outdated/poor listing content.
- **Only one exclusive brand**: Gragnano in Corsa (IGP pasta, sole US importer). All other brands are shared with other sellers, so they compete on buy box.
- **FBA constraints**: Olive oil needs climate control (Amazon FCs are not). Chocolate melts in summer. Only dry shelf-stable goods (pasta, salt, taralli, vinegar) are FBA-viable.
- **Supply constraints**: 1-month lead time from Italy, limited per-product quantities, 1-year shelf life on most items (pasta is 2 years). Seller confirmed they can serve 2-3x demand if growth happens over 4-5 months.
- **Almost no ads running** currently. No brand registry done despite being sole importer for some lines. Brand Analytics access confirmed.
- Account-level buy box ~41-48% (chronically low - typical wholesaler pattern).
- Landed COGS roughly 50% (~$16 on a $33 olive oil).

## Rationale

### Why P0: Selezione Tartufi Black Truffle Sea Salt (B001D6B0SG)

- Highest 3-month revenue in the catalog at $1,563. Currently growing at 5x month-over-month (Feb $105, Mar $803, Apr $656).
- Buy box is the clear blocker. Sessions stay in the 400-500 range when in stock, but buy box has been chronically low: monthly averages of 2.8%, 11.2%, 16.8% over the last 3 months. November 2025 hit a peak of 23.3% BB which coincided with the all-time monthly revenue peak ($756). Buy box recovery alone has a clear upside path.
- CVR has shot up in Apr to 15.82% (best in catalog at that volume), suggesting the listing is converting well when shoppers see them as the buy box winner.
- FBA-viable: shelf-stable truffle salt has no climate or shelf-life issues. Inventory commitment can be smaller per ship than other lines.
- $26 ASP, ~50% margin per seller. Healthy unit economics.

### Why P1: Agrumato Lemon Olive Oil (B0C8926MS1)

- Vally called this their hero product on the sales call. The brand currently drives the second-highest revenue and is **growing** (Feb $87, Mar $345, Apr $428).
- Sessions are the highest in the catalog and accelerating: 291 → 359 → 532. Traffic momentum is real, regardless of the buy box pressure.
- Buy box ~50% and CVR ~3% point to a different blocker than P0 - this is a listing/conversion problem, not a buy box problem (the seller already shows up reasonably often, but doesn't convert). Aryan diagnosed the same on the call (text-heavy A+ images, weak design).
- Direct competitive pressure on this listing was named on the call (competing brand running Sponsored Product ads on the Agrumato detail page).
- FBA-hostile (climate). Will need to stay FBM and solve the conversion problem first.
- The Agrumato parent has 7+ child variants (Lemon, Blood Orange, Tangerine, Citron, Bergamot, sizes, 2-packs). Halo opportunity once one variant is winning.

### Why P2: Gragnano in Corsa IGP Pasta Line

- The seller's **only true moat** on Amazon. Sole US importer; no other seller can list these ASINs, so buy box is permanently at ~100% across the 11 SKUs.
- Currently almost completely dormant: 11 SKUs combined did $134 over 3 months. Only B07YNBY8NV (Fusilli Ferrato) is moving meaningfully ($94.50 in Apr alone, 42.86% CVR on the small session base).
- Pasta is the only sub-category in the catalog with a 2-year shelf life, which makes it **the only candidate for FBA at scale**. Pairs perfectly with Jeff's stated constraint.
- Brand Registry has not been done. That alone unlocks A+ content (Premium A+ if also enrolled), Brand Story, Sponsored Brand ads, and protection against listing hijacking that Vally specifically complained about on the call.
- P2 priority (not P0) because there is no current revenue to defend. This is a greenfield growth play that depends on building traffic from zero, which is slower than fixing P0's buy box.
- Pick **B07YNBY8NV (Fusilli Ferrato)** as the spearhead since it already has signs of life. Use early wins to template the rest of the line.

### Why P3: Acetaia Cattani White Balsamic Vinegar (B00D2WK03K)

- Second-highest 3-month revenue at $1,157 - but it is generated from astonishingly low traffic.
- Apr sessions: 22. Apr CVR: 68.18%. That kind of conversion rate is pricing-power evidence; the people who land here buy, period.
- Buy box averages 46.8% (Feb 16.9%, Mar 68.4%, Apr 55.2%). Recovering buy box matters but not the primary blocker since current shoppers are already converting at unicorn rates.
- The blocker is traffic. Tiny session count is the lid. Drive impressions on this listing and revenue scales nearly linearly until CVR mean-reverts.
- Vinegar is shelf-stable and room-temperature tolerant, so FBA is workable.
- $26 ASP. Acetaia Cattani is not exclusive to them, so this is a buy-box-shared listing, but the data shows they can hold meaningful share when present.

## Insights

- **P0 (Black Truffle Sea Salt) is buy-box-constrained, not traffic-constrained.** 12 months of data show sessions in a steady 400-650 range while buy box bounces 0-23%. Revenue moves with buy box, not sessions. The Apr 2026 CVR of 15.8% says when they win the buy box, the listing converts hard.
- **P1 (Agrumato Lemon) is the inverse problem.** Sessions are growing (291 → 532) and buy box is ~50%, but CVR is stuck near 3%. The bottleneck is the detail page itself (listing copy, images, A+ content) - exactly the diagnosis Aryan gave Vally on the call.
- **P3 (Acetaia Cattani White Balsamic) shows extreme pricing power on tiny traffic.** A 68% CVR in April on 22 sessions is highly unusual. Even if conversion mean-reverts to 25-30% with scale, this listing has a much higher revenue ceiling than its current $1,157/3-mo suggests.
- **The seller's only competitive moat (Gragnano in Corsa pasta) is producing $134/3-mo across 11 SKUs.** Brand registry is not done, ad spend is zero, and listings have not been built out. The growth path here is multiplicative, not incremental.
- **Buy box at the account level is structurally low (~45%)** because they share most listings with other sellers (sometimes the original importers/wholesalers themselves, per Vally). This is a wholesaler pattern, not a fixable account issue. The only structural fixes are (a) lean harder into the exclusive brand, (b) defend the listings they do control with brand registry + listing quality + defensive ads, and (c) compete on price/availability where it makes sense.
- **Ad data is functionally zero.** Total account ad spend over 3 months is under $200. Effectively the seller is not advertising. Step 4 (Ad Analysis) will not have a normal account-level dataset to work with; the audit will pivot to an ad-launch strategy.

## Things to Investigate Further

- **P0 Jan 2026 anomaly.** $0 sales on 44 sessions with 3.51% buy box is far below the monthly baseline. Was this a stockout, a listing suppression event, or a price spike that drove away traffic? Worth cross-referencing with Keepa price/sales-rank history in Step 2.
- **P0 Apr 2026 session drop** from 396 to 158. Looks like the listing may have been demoted or stocked out partway through the month - revenue actually held up because CVR jumped, but sessions halving in a month is unusual. Confirm via Step 2 / Keepa.
- **P1 (Agrumato) Galbasa competitive attack.** Vally mentioned a competing brand running Sponsored Product ads on the Agrumato listing. Quantify this in Step 4 by pulling the placements/search-term tables, and decide whether a defensive Sponsored Display campaign is needed.
- **P2 (Gragnano) market opportunity.** SQP analysis on pasta keywords (artisan pasta, IGP pasta, Italian dry pasta, specific shape names like fusilli, paccheri, bucatini) to size the addressable market. Currently 0% market share with 100% guaranteed buy box - need to know how big the prize is.
- **Carlo Moro Pizzoccheri seasonality.** SQP 12-month search volume on "pizzoccheri" to confirm winter-only demand pattern before deprioritizing.
