# Step 3: SQP Analysis

## Important Pivot in Scope

The original P0 from Step 1 was Selezione Tartufi Black Truffle Sea Salt (B001D6B0SG). That ASIN is brand-registered to Italian Products Inc., not Olive Oil Lovers, so Olive Oil Lovers' Brand Analytics SQP feed does not contain any truffle salt queries. The same constraint applies to most of their revenue (Selezione Tartufi, Acetaia Cattani, Agrumato non-gift, Carlo Moro, etc.) - they sell those listings as a reseller but do not own the brand.

The only brand they own brand registry for is **Olio2go**, which covers ~10 SKUs: gift sets, a few specialty olive oils, and two herb SKUs (Sicilian oregano and Sicilian thyme).

The Olio2go-owned SKU with by far the best SQP signal is **B01N2Z8FQV: Naturally Dried Oregano from Sicily**. So this Step 3 analyzes the SQP for that ASIN. Findings here feed both the action plan and the broader brand strategy.

This is the "owned-brand" thread of the audit. The "wholesaler portfolio" thread is covered in Step 4 (Ad Analysis / Buy Box).

## SQP-Pivot ASIN: Naturally Dried Oregano from Sicily (B01N2Z8FQV)

**Title:** Naturally Dried Oregano from Sicily
**Brand:** Olio2go (manufacturer: Frantoi Cutrera, a well-known Sicilian olive mill)
**Category:** Listed under Produce > Fresh Vegetables (misclassified — should be Herbs & Spices)
**Rating:** 4.3 stars
**Trailing 12-mo sales (May 2025-Apr 2026):** $910 across 9 active months
**ASP:** $8-16 (variable, current $13)
**Listing state:** 2 images, no A+ content, no video, no bullets/features. Premium artisanal jar packaging (red wax seal, Italian "Origano" label).

The 12-month sales pattern shows clear buy-box and CVR responsiveness: best month Jul 2025 at $265 (100% BB, 37.78% CVR, 17 units) vs. worst at $13 (April 2026, 1 unit). The listing converts when shoppers reach it and they own the buy box.

## Tagging Rationale

The product is dried Sicilian oregano in a small artisanal jar at a premium price point. Three concentric markets:

- **Tier 1 (Hero - "Sicilian oregano" niche):** Queries where the customer is specifically looking for Sicilian or Italian-origin oregano. The product is the direct answer. 10 queries tagged. They include the main term "sicilian oregano" plus typos/variants ("sicicilan oregano", "sicilian otegano"), longer-tail ("sicilian oregano from sicily", "oregano leaves naturally dried scilian"), and the obvious branded query "cutrera ibleo sicilian oregano".

- **Tier 2 (Core market - "Dried oregano"):** Broader dried-oregano queries where the customer wants dried oregano but is not specifically looking for an Italian/Sicilian one. The product can compete but is one option among many (McCormick, Simply Organic, generic). 2 queries tagged: "oregano dried" and "organic sicilian oregano".

- **Tier 3 (Broad - "Oregano" generic):** The largest category-defining keyword "oregano" alone. Most searchers here are not premium-product hunters - they want a fresh herb plant, a bulk culinary supply, or anything else "oregano." The product can technically appear but is rarely the intent.

- **Branded:** Frantoi Cutrera (manufacturer brand) and Olio2go (seller brand) queries. 5 queries tagged. Tiny volume but useful to see brand-equity signals.

**Catalog overlap check:** B01N2Z8FQV is the only Olio2go SKU that ranks for any oregano query. The Sicilian thyme SKU (B07HKRH98J) is a different herb. Therefore the impression-share cap for Tier 1 is the standard single-product ~8-9% maximum, no adjustment.

**Tier 1 detail:**

The CVR gap is the actionable finding. Brand CTR is *above* industry (shoppers like the premium image enough to click), but only ~15% of brand clicks convert vs. ~22% for the rest of the category. The listing has zero bullets, no A+ content, no video, and just two images. For a $13 product on a search where shoppers are price-sensitive (most dried oregano is $4-8), the detail page does not justify the premium - that is the most likely CVR leak. Same listing rebuild also positions the brand to enter Tier 2.

**Tier 2 detail:**

When Olio2go did briefly appear on "oregano dried" in November 2025 (1,030 impressions in one month) they got 1 click and 1 cart and 1 purchase from it. Sample size too small to read, but it shows the listing isn't completely irrelevant on dried-oregano queries either. The bottleneck is visibility, not intent.

## Insights

- **Olio2go-as-brand has effectively one organically winning SKU on Amazon, and it is this oregano listing on a single niche query family.** Tier 1 "Sicilian oregano" is where the brand has real impression share (~2.6% on a ~9% cap). Everything else they own (gift sets, olive oils, thyme) shows up at near-zero impression share even on relevant queries.
- **The growth ceiling on the owned brand is the listing, not the market.** They are sitting at 14.86% CVR vs. a 21.58% industry CVR on the one tier where they have traction. That 31% CVR gap is the difference between $4-5k/mo in Tier 1 ceiling revenue and roughly half of that today. The listing has no bullets, no A+ content, no video, and two images - it is a 30-minute rebuild away from closing that gap.
- **The bigger absolute market lives in Tier 2 ("oregano dried", ~$67k/mo), but the brand has no presence there and the same listing has to do the work.** That means the Tier 1 listing fix doubles as Tier 2 entry. Solving Tier 1 unlocks Tier 2 cheaply because the same detail page is what users land on.
- **Tier 3 ("oregano" generic) is not a real opportunity for this product.** The intent is wrong. Most shoppers searching just "oregano" are looking for fresh herb, plants, or a McCormick jar. A $13 25g artisan jar is not what those buyers convert on. The seller would burn ad budget chasing this.
- **Branded queries ("frantoi cutrera", "olio2go") have tiny volume but 100% CVR when the brand shows up.** Loyal-customer signal is there. Branded defense PPC at 2-3% of budget is a no-brainer.

## Things to Investigate Further

- **Step 4 / Ads:** No PPC currently runs on this listing. Once the listing is rebuilt, a Sponsored Products campaign targeting "sicilian oregano" + variants should be the first test, with a small spend (~$5-10/day) since Tier 1 is small.
- **Listing-side:** Why is this listed under Produce > Fresh Vegetables instead of Herbs & Spices? Mis-categorization may also be suppressing organic eligibility for "spice" / "seasoning" queries. Worth checking category eligibility and re-classifying if possible.
- **Price reality check:** Confirm the right ASP. The 12-month data shows the listing oscillating between $7.80 and $15.61 - a 2x range. The CVR gap may be partly a price-volatility issue (Amazon shoppers re-evaluate when price moves a lot).

## Questions for the Seller

- Why are you priced 2x above generic oregano competitors? Is the Frantoi Cutrera Sicilian sourcing a verifiable differentiator (DOP, IGP, region-of-origin certification) that should be in the title and bullets? If yes, we have an immediate listing fix. If no, the premium price is exposed and we may need to discount to compete on Tier 2.
- The product is currently categorized under Produce > Fresh Vegetables, which is incorrect for a dried herb in a jar. Was this set up wrong on day one, or did Amazon force the category? Recategorizing to Herbs & Spices likely opens organic eligibility for a wider keyword set.
- The listing has no A+ content despite Olio2go being brand-registered. Is there a reason content hasn't been published, or is it just been on the to-do list?
- Is the SKU consistently stocked? July 2025 hit $265 / 17 units / 100% buy box, August dropped to $62 / 5.36% buy box. The buy-box collapse looks like an out-of-stock event. If supply is unstable, we need to factor that into the action plan before pushing for impression-share growth.
