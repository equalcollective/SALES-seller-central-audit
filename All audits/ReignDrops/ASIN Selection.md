- **Strong seasonal pattern with two annual peaks:** Spring (Apr-May, likely driven by Mother's Day and baby shower season) and Holiday (December). Troughs in July and February. This is characteristic of a baby keepsake product.
- **Buy box instability:** Buy box dropped to ~88% in Aug, Sep, Oct, and Dec 2025. This coincides with high-demand periods, suggesting a competitor or 3P seller is winning the buy box during peak traffic. The buy box recovered to 99%+ in Jan-Feb 2026 as demand normalized.
- **CVR tracks with seasonality:** CVR peaks during high-demand months (24-26%) and drops during troughs (17-19%), which is expected. The Feb 2026 CVR of 17.1% is the lowest in the dataset and worth monitoring.

## Insights

- **P0 (Ink Pad) is heavily seasonal.** The 4.7x swing between trough ($9K in Jul) and peak ($51K in May) means revenue projections and ad strategy must account for this cycle. The brand should be scaling ad spend into peaks and pulling back during troughs.
- **P1 (Inkless Ink Pad) has the best unit economics in the catalog.** 5.24 ROAS in Feb, 25% CVR, and only 21% ad sales dependency. This product is converting well organically with minimal ad support.
- **P2 (Splat Mat) is burning cash.** 0.13 ROAS in Feb, 86% TACoS, and CVR collapsed to 1.4%. The $159 in Feb ad spend generated only $21 in ad sales. This needs to be paused or significantly restructured.

## Things to Investigate Further

- Buy box dropping to ~88% during peak months (Aug-Dec 2025) on P0 is concerning. Need to check if this is a pricing issue, a 3P seller issue, or stock-related. This directly impacts revenue during the highest-traffic periods.
- P2 (Splat Mat) CVR cratered from 7.5% in Dec to 1.4% in Feb. Is this purely seasonal, or has something changed on the listing or competitive landscape?
- P0 sessions dropped 58% from Dec (21,167) to Feb (8,812) while ad spend dropped 41%. Need SQP data to confirm whether this is a market-wide seasonal drop or a brand-specific traffic loss.
