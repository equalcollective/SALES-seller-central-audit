# SQP Analysis: ReignDrops - P0 (Ink Pad)

## Tagging Rationale

**Tier 1 (Hero):** Queries where the customer is searching for exactly a baby ink pad for prints. The product is the direct answer: "ink pad for baby hand and footprints", "baby ink pad for footprint", "ink pad for baby", "baby safe ink pad", "ink pad baby footprint". 5 queries tagged.

**Tier 2 (Core market):** Queries for baby footprint/handprint kits broadly. The product is one solution among several (clay kits, frame kits, inkless pads). Broader competitive set but same customer need: "baby footprint kit", "baby hand and footprint kit", "baby handprint kit". 4-7 queries tagged depending on the month.

**Tier 3 (Broad/adjacent):** Generic ink pad queries and pet paw print queries. The product can appear but is not the primary intent for most searchers: "ink pad", "ink pads for stamps", "ink pad for dog paw prints". The generic "ink pad" query is dominated by office/craft stamp users, not baby keepsake buyers. 5 queries tagged.

Note: Tier 3 queries have a fundamental mismatch. The generic "ink pad" searcher typically wants a stamp ink pad for crafts/office use, not a baby product. The brand shows up because the product is literally an ink pad, but the conversion gap reflects that most searchers are not looking for a baby product. Similarly, "ink pad for dog paw prints" is adjacent but the product can serve this use case.

## Market Share and Potential

3-month averages (December 2025 - February 2026):

| Tier | Impression Share | Click Share | Cart Share | Purchase Share | Trend |
|------|-----------------|-------------|------------|---------------|-------|
| Tier 1 | 7.5% | 11.4% | 9.4% | 9.0% | Declining (14.1% → 3.7% purchase share, Dec → Feb) |
| Tier 2 | 0.9% | 0.6% | 0.5% | 0.4% | Declining |
| Tier 3 | 1.2% | 1.0% | 0.6% | 0.5% | Declining |

**Key observations:**
- **Tier 1 is the brand's stronghold** with 7.5% impression share (approaching the ~8-9% theoretical max) and 9% purchase share. However, share is declining sharply, dropping from 14% purchase share in December to 3.7% in February. This tracks with the seasonal drop, but the decline is steeper than the market's seasonal decline, suggesting the brand is losing ground, not just riding the cycle down.
- **Tier 2 represents the biggest untapped opportunity.** This is a $40K/mo market where the brand has only 0.4% purchase share. The brand barely shows up (0.9% impression share). There is massive room to grow simply by being visible on these queries.
- **Tier 3 is a low-priority opportunity.** The impression share is low (1.2%) but the query intent mismatch means capturing more of this market requires searchers to want a baby ink pad when they searched for a generic ink pad or dog paw print kit. Conversion will always be structurally lower here.

## Blockers & Growth Path

### Tier 1: CVR is the blocker

**Primary blocker: Conversion rate.** Brand CVR averaged 15.9% vs industry 21.2% over the last 3 months, a 25% gap. The ATC rate gap is similar (38.7% brand vs 49.4% industry). The brand gets clicked at a rate above industry (3.0% vs 2.1% CTR), so visibility and appeal on the search results page are not the issue. Customers are landing on the listing and not converting at industry rates.

**Growth path:** This is a listing optimization play matching pattern #3 from the CLAUDE.md framework. The listing gaps identified in Step 2 (no A+ content, no video, only 3/5 bullets, safety messaging buried) directly explain the CVR gap. Fix the listing first, then the existing impression share will convert better. Scaling PPC before fixing conversion would waste money, as the listing is already leaking customers.

**Feb 2026 concern:** CVR dropped to 10.3% vs 21.2% industry in February, an extreme gap. This coincides with the seasonal trough, but the brand's CVR dropped proportionally more than industry CVR, which stayed stable. Something beyond seasonality may be driving this.

### Tier 2: Impression share is the blocker

**Primary blocker: Visibility.** The brand has 0.9% impression share on a $40K/mo market. You simply cannot capture what you do not show up for.

**Secondary blocker: CVR.** Even when the brand does show up, CVR lags industry (11.6% brand vs 17.5% industry, a 34% gap). CTR is also below industry (1.5% vs 2.1%).

**Growth path:** This is a hybrid between pattern #1 (low impression share) and pattern #3 (below-industry CVR). The immediate lever is PPC: bid on "baby footprint kit", "baby hand and footprint kit", and "baby handprint kit" to increase visibility. But the listing also needs to be improved to convert this traffic. The Tier 2 customer is searching for "kits", which implies they want a complete solution (pad + cards + possibly frames), not just a standalone ink pad. The listing needs to communicate what comes with the product and position it as a kit, not just a pad.

### Tier 3: Not a priority, structural mismatch

**Primary blocker: Product-market fit.** Brand CVR is 14% vs industry 28.4%, a 51% gap. This is the largest CVR gap across all tiers and reflects a fundamental intent mismatch. The "ink pad" searcher wants a craft/office stamp pad. The "ink pad for dog paw prints" searcher wants a pet product. The baby ink pad shows up but is not what most searchers want.

**Growth path:** This tier is not capturable at scale with the current product. The brand already has some impression share here (1.2%), and the conversion gap is structural, not fixable through listing optimization. Recommendation: do not invest in scaling Tier 3. Let the organic impressions continue to generate incidental sales, but focus PPC budget on Tier 1 (fixing CVR) and Tier 2 (gaining visibility).

## Insights

- **P0 (Ink Pad) has a $40K/mo Tier 2 market it barely participates in.** The brand captures 9% of Tier 1 purchases but only 0.4% of Tier 2. Tier 2 is 3x the size of Tier 1. This is the primary growth opportunity: increase visibility on "baby footprint kit" and "baby hand and footprint kit" queries through PPC, while improving the listing to convert that traffic.
- **The CVR gap on Tier 1 is costing real revenue.** With 7.5% impression share and decent CTR, the brand is getting traffic. But converting at 15.9% instead of the industry 21.2% means the brand is leaving roughly 25% of potential Tier 1 sales on the table. At an estimated $13.9K/mo Tier 1 market with ~9% purchase share, closing the CVR gap could add ~$400-500/mo in Tier 1 alone.
- **Share is declining across all tiers.** This is partly seasonal (Q4 peak → Q1 trough), but the decline is steeper than the market's seasonal drop. The brand may be losing competitive position, not just riding the cycle. This needs monitoring into the spring peak to confirm.

## Things to Investigate Further

- Check in ad data whether the brand is bidding on Tier 2 keywords ("baby footprint kit", "baby hand and footprint kit"). If not, the 0.4% purchase share is entirely organic, and PPC is a clear unlock.
- The Feb 2026 CVR collapse on Tier 1 (10.3% vs 21.2% industry) is extreme. Check if there was a listing change, price change, or negative review spike in February that could explain this.

## Questions for the Seller

- The product shows up on "kit" queries (baby footprint kit, baby hand and footprint kit), which is Tier 2. What exactly comes with the product? If it includes cardstock/paper for prints, the listing should clearly position it as a kit, not just a pad. If it is just the pad, there may be an opportunity to create a bundle that matches kit intent.
