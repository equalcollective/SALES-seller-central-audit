# Seller Central Audit: ReignDrops

## 1. Executive Summary

ReignDrops is an Amazon-native baby products brand built around a hero product, a washable ink pad for baby hand and footprints (P0). The brand has been on Amazon since ~2018, has accumulated 8,300+ reviews, and generates ~$85K in quarterly sales from P0 alone. The brand is well-established in its niche but has not scaled beyond Amazon, with minimal social presence and no meaningful DTC channel.

The primary growth opportunity is a $40K/month Tier 2 market ("baby footprint kit", "baby handprint kit") where ReignDrops captures only 0.4% purchase share despite already having keywords that convert at 3.2x to 6.4x ROAS. The root cause is a campaign structure problem: 1,055 keywords stuffed into a single exact match campaign, starving high-performing Tier 2 keywords of budget. Extracting these into dedicated campaigns is the single highest-leverage action.

The secondary opportunity is closing a 25% CVR gap on Tier 1 queries. The listing has critical gaps (no A+ content, no video, only 3 of 5 bullets used, safety messaging buried) that are suppressing conversion. Fixing these issues while simultaneously shifting more PPC spend to Top of Search (which converts at 31.5% vs 18% for Rest of Search) creates a compounding improvement across both paid and organic traffic.

## 3. P0 Deep Dive Summary (Ink Pad)

**Product:**
- Baby-safe washable ink pad for infant hand/footprints as keepsakes
- Non-toxic, acid-free, ASTM D-4236 compliant
- 8+ color range is the strongest differentiator vs competitors who offer 1-3 colors
- ~$8 price point, positioned slightly below market average (~6% below)

**Customer:** New parents (primary), grandparents, gift-buyers for baby showers. Sentimental, one-time purchase in the first weeks after birth.

**Competitive Landscape:**
- **Pearhead:** Category leader with retail distribution (Target), clean-touch technology, strong brand recognition
- **KeaBabies:** Fast-growing Amazon-native brand, premium gift packaging, aggressive bundling, broader baby ecosystem
- **Key risk:** Market is shifting toward inkless/clean-touch technology. P0 is traditional ink. P1 (the clean-touch variant) may overtake P0 over time

**Listing Quality Issues:**
- Only 3 of 5 bullet points used. Bullet 3 is a generic Amazon international product disclaimer, wasting a slot
- Safety messaging (the #1 purchase driver for parents) is buried in the title, never expanded in bullets
- No A+ Content (major miss for an emotional/keepsake purchase)
- No video (product's biggest selling point is "easy, no-mess process" but no way to demonstrate it)
- No Brand Store (missing cross-sell opportunity with P1)
- Pet paw prints (visible secondary use case) buried mid-sentence instead of getting its own bullet

**Rating:** 4.1 stars from 8,326 reviews. Gradually declining from 4.4 (Nov 2020) to 4.1. 11% one-star rate is elevated for the category.

**Seasonality:** Two annual peaks (May for Mother's Day/baby shower season, December for holidays). 4.7x swing between trough ($9K Jul) and peak ($51K May).

## 6. Key Insights (Consolidated, Deduplicated)

**Listing Issues:**
- P0 (Ink Pad) has critical listing gaps suppressing CVR: no A+ content, no video, only 3/5 bullets used, safety messaging buried. These are all fixable and represent the biggest conversion improvement opportunity.
- P0 (Ink Pad) rating has declined from 4.4 to 4.1 over 5 years with an elevated 11% one-star rate. The long-term trend may reflect product quality issues or rising customer expectations around clean-touch technology.

**Ad Issues:**
- $1,754 is being wasted on non-performing product campaigns over 90 days (~$585/month). Mermaid, Piddle Pad, and Colic Belts campaigns should be paused immediately.
- The campaign structure (1,055 keywords in a single exact campaign) is the root cause of the Tier 2 impression share problem. High-converting Tier 2 keywords are already being targeted and convert well (3.2x to 6.4x ROAS), they just need dedicated budget allocation.
- Top of Search is underutilized at only 24% of spend despite being the best-performing placement (4.32 ROAS, 31.5% CVR).

**Market Opportunity:**
- P0 (Ink Pad) has a $40K/month Tier 2 market it barely participates in (0.4% purchase share vs 9% on Tier 1). Tier 2 is 3x the size of Tier 1. This is the primary growth lever.
- The CVR gap on Tier 1 (15.9% vs 21.2% industry) is costing roughly $400-500/month in lost Tier 1 sales alone.
- Market share is declining across all tiers, steeper than the seasonal drop. Needs monitoring into the spring peak to confirm whether this is competitive erosion or just seasonal noise.

**Strategic:**
- P0 (Ink Pad) is heavily seasonal with a 4.7x swing between trough and peak. Ad strategy must scale into peaks (Apr-May, Nov-Dec) and pull back during troughs (Jul, Feb).
- P1 (Inkless Clean Touch Ink Pad) has the best unit economics in the catalog (4.43 ROAS, 25.7% CVR, 4.8% TACoS) and aligns with the market's shift toward clean-touch technology. Over time, P1 may overtake P0.
- P2 (Splat Mat) is burning cash (0.13 ROAS in Feb, CVR collapsed to 1.4%). Needs immediate decision on whether to continue investment.
- The market is shifting toward inkless/clean-touch and bundle/gift-set formats. ReignDrop sells mostly individual pads, missing the gift-set opportunity in a gifting-driven category.

**Buy Box:**
- P0 (Ink Pad) buy box dropped to ~88% during peak months (Aug-Dec 2025), coinciding with highest-traffic periods. This directly impacts revenue during peak season. Buy box recovered to 99%+ in Jan-Feb as demand normalized.

## 7. Questions for the Seller (Consolidated, Deduplicated)

- **P0 (Ink Pad) buy box dropped to ~88% in Aug-Oct and Dec 2025.** Are there other sellers on this listing, or was there a pricing/inventory issue during those months? This directly impacts revenue during peak periods and needs to be resolved before the spring peak.

- **P0 (Ink Pad) rating has declined from 4.4 to 4.1 over the past 5 years.** Are there any known product quality changes, supplier changes, or recurring complaints that might explain this? A pattern in recent 1-star reviews would help determine whether this is fixable.

- **P0 (Ink Pad) shows up on "kit" queries (baby footprint kit, baby hand and footprint kit).** What exactly comes with the product? If it includes cardstock/paper for prints, the listing should clearly position it as a kit, not just a pad. If it is just the pad, there may be an opportunity to create a bundle that matches kit intent.

- **P2 (Splat Mat) performance is declining sharply** (CVR collapsed from 7.5% in Dec to 1.4% in Feb, ROAS at 0.13 in Feb). Are you planning to continue investing in this product, or is it being phased out?

- **Mermaid product has 143 clicks and zero orders through ads over 90 days.** Is there a reason this product is still being advertised? The ad budget would be better allocated elsewhere.

## 8. Action Plan

### Weeks 1-2: Immediate PPC Actions

- **Pause unprofitable campaigns.** Stop Mermaid, Piddle Pad, and Colic Belts campaigns immediately. Review Clear Mats exact campaign (0.66% CVR from 301 clicks) and pause if targeting cannot be fixed. Frees up ~$585/month.
  - *Expected impact:* $585/month saved in wasted spend.

- **Reallocate freed budget to P0 exact campaign.** $585/month at P0's 4.05x ROAS generates ~$2,369/month in additional sales.
  - *Expected impact:* +$2,369/month in ad-attributed sales from the same total budget.

- **Increase Top of Search bid modifier on P0 campaigns.** Shift spend toward the highest-converting placement (31.5% CVR, 4.32 ROAS).
  - *Expected impact:* Shifting $1,000 from Rest of Search to Top of Search yields a net +$1,060 in sales.

- **Extract top 5 Tier 2 keywords into a dedicated exact match campaign** with its own daily budget: "baby safe ink pad for hand and feet", "baby ink pad for baby prints", "baby safe ink pad", "baby ink pad", "stamp pad". These are currently buried in the 1,055-keyword exact campaign.
  - *Expected impact:* Doubling Tier 2 spend from ~$266/month to ~$532/month at 4.3x ROAS generates +$1,143/month.

### Weeks 2-4: PPC Optimization + Listing Prep

- **Scale the dedicated Tier 2 campaign** based on Week 1-2 performance data. If ROAS holds above 3.5x, increase daily budget incrementally.

- **Extract 2 high-ROAS keywords into solo campaigns** for maximum budget control: "baby safe ink pad for hand and feet" (5.12x ROAS) and "footprint ink for baby" (5.94x ROAS). Scale spend on these individually.
  - *Expected impact:* +$1,573/month from these two keywords alone if spend is doubled/tripled.

- **Begin listing audit and content creation for P0.** Write 5 optimized bullet points (safety as bullet #1, pet paw prints as standalone bullet, remove Amazon disclaimer). Draft A+ content brief with lifestyle imagery (finished keepsakes framed, parent with baby, gift-wrapped at baby shower). Plan product demo video (30 seconds: ink pad to finished print to wiping baby's foot clean).

- **Create P0 Brand Store** to enable cross-selling between P0 (traditional ink) and P1 (clean-touch), and to capture Sponsored Brand traffic.

### Weeks 4-6: Listing Optimization

- **Publish updated bullet points** for P0. 5 full bullets, safety-first messaging, pet paw prints standalone, clear "what's included" section to convert kit-intent searchers.

- **Publish A+ Content** for P0. Emotional lifestyle imagery, comparison module (traditional ink vs clean-touch, or ReignDrop vs messy alternatives), "what's in the box" module.

- **Upload product demo video** for P0. 30-second clip demonstrating ease of use and no-mess process.

- **Monitor CVR impact.** The listing improvements should begin closing the 25% CVR gap on Tier 1 (15.9% vs 21.2% industry). Even a partial close from 15.9% to 18% represents meaningful revenue.
  - *Expected impact:* Closing half the CVR gap on Tier 1 adds ~$200-250/month. On Tier 2, the impact compounds with increased impression share.

### Weeks 6-8: Scaling and Evaluation

- **Scale Tier 2 PPC spend** on the improved listing. With better CVR, the ROAS on Tier 2 terms should improve, allowing more aggressive spending.

- **Evaluate P2 (Splat Mat) decision.** Based on seller input and performance trends, either restructure the ad approach or sunset the product and reallocate budget.

- **Assess P1 (Inkless Clean Touch Ink Pad) for deep dive.** P1 has the best unit economics and aligns with market trends. If P0 improvements are on track, begin SQP and ad analysis for P1 to identify its growth ceiling.

- **Prepare for spring peak (April-May).** Ensure P0 campaigns are scaled up, listing is optimized, and buy box issues from 2025 are addressed before the highest-traffic period.
