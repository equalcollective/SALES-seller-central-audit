## Campaign Structure (Step 4a)

**Problem:** The campaign structure is severely overstuffed. The P0 exact match campaign ("SP - Ink Pads - exact") has **1,055 keywords** in a single campaign. Of those, 505 have zero spend, meaning Amazon's algorithm never allocated budget to them. Even among the 550 that did spend, the top 10 keywords captured only 31.5% of total campaign spend, with the remaining 1,045 keywords splitting the other 68.5%.

This is far beyond the recommended 3-5 keywords per manual campaign. Amazon is picking winners from a pool of over a thousand keywords, and the seller has virtually no control over which keywords receive budget.

- Ink Pads phrase: 510 targets

## P0 Blocker Analysis (Step 4f)

### Primary Blocker: CVR (Tier 1)

**SQP finding:** Brand CVR is 15.9% vs industry 21.2% on Tier 1 queries, a 25% gap.

**Placement Performance (Seller Level):**

| Placement | Spend | Sales | ROAS | CTR | CVR |
|-----------|-------|-------|------|-----|-----|
| Top of Search | $3,045 | $13,141 | 4.32 | 9.46% | 31.5% |
| Rest of Search | $7,460 | $24,340 | 3.26 | 1.29% | 18.0% |
| Product Pages | $1,932 | $5,394 | 2.79 | 0.38% | 12.9% |
| Off Amazon | $15 | $8 | 0.52 | 0.32% | 1.0% |

Top of Search converts at 31.5% CVR, nearly 2.5x Rest of Search (18.0%) and is the best-performing placement on both ROAS (4.32) and CTR (9.46%). However, Top of Search only captures 24.4% of total spend ($3,045 of $12,461). The majority of spend ($7,460, 59.8%) goes to Rest of Search, which converts at a lower rate.

**PPC lever for CVR:** Increase Top of Search bid modifier on P0 campaigns. Shifting more spend to Top of Search will improve the blended CVR because that placement converts at a significantly higher rate. The expected impact:

- If $1,000 is shifted from Rest of Search to Top of Search:
  - Lost: $1,000 at 3.26 ROAS = $3,260 in sales
  - Gained: $1,000 at 4.32 ROAS = $4,320 in sales
  - Net gain: $1,060 in additional sales

However, the CVR gap from SQP (15.9% vs 21.2%) is a blended brand-level metric that includes organic traffic. PPC placement shifts will help the ad-attributed conversion rate but will not fully close the gap. The listing itself needs attention (images, A+ content, bullets) to improve conversion for all traffic sources. The PPC lever buys time while listing improvements are implemented.

## Insights

- **P0 (Ink Pad) ad performance is strong despite structural problems.** 4.03x ROAS across $8,443 in spend, generating $33,954 in sales. The product converts well through ads, especially on exact match (28.5% CVR). The opportunity is not fixing what is broken, it is removing the structural ceiling that is capping growth.

- **$1,754 is being wasted on non-performing product campaigns.** Mermaid ($209 spend, $9 sales), Piddle Pad ($314 spend, $165 sales), Colic Belts ($220 spend, $150 sales), and Clear Mats ($393 spend, $365 sales) are all running below 1.5x ROAS with enough click volume to be statistically conclusive. This is roughly $585/month in wasted ad spend.

- **The campaign structure is the root cause of the Tier 2 impression share problem.** With 1,055 keywords in the exact campaign and 510 in the phrase campaign, high-converting Tier 2 keywords are being starved of budget. The seller is already targeting the right keywords and they convert well, they just need dedicated budget allocation through separate campaigns.

- **Top of Search is underutilized.** Only 24% of spend goes to Top of Search, which has the best performance on every metric (4.32 ROAS, 31.5% CVR, 9.46% CTR). Increasing the Top of Search bid modifier on P0 campaigns is the most direct PPC lever for the CVR blocker.
