## Tagging Rationale

**Tier 1 (Hero, 12 queries):** Queries where the customer is searching for exactly a paver/crack/driveway-specific weeding tool. Bi-Crackie is the direct answer.
- Tagged: crack weeder, crack weeder tool, crevice weeding tool, sidewalk crack weeder, paver weed removal tool, driveway weed removal tool, weed puller tool for pavers, weed puller tool for cracks, driveway crack weeder, crack weeder crevice weeding tool, tool for weeding between pavers, paver weeder

**Tier 2 (Core market, 8 queries):** Broader weed-puller / weeder intent. Bi-Crackie is one valid answer alongside short-handle and telescoping alternatives. Same customer need (remove weeds), broader competitive set.
- Tagged: weed puller tool, weed puller, weeder tool, weeding tool, long handle weed puller, stand up weeder, weed scraper, weed remover tool

**Tier 3 (Adjacent, 4 queries):** Generic gardening / paver queries where Bi-Crackie can technically appear but is not the primary intent.
- Tagged: gardening hand tools, garden tools, gardening tools, pavers

**Catalog overlap (Phase 5):** Bi-Crackie has 4 selling SKU variants (Original Green, Purple Paver, Set, Junior) which all rank for paver/crack queries. In practice 2-3 variants surface per query. Adjusted impression share cap: **~16-18%** for Tier 1 and Tier 2 (assuming 2 products commonly rank).

**How rates were computed:** Volume-weighted across the full 12-month window (Apr 2025 to Mar 2026) for Tier 1, because Q1 2026 alone has too few brand events (3 clicks, 0 cart adds, 0 purchases) to compute meaningful rates. The 12-month base of 11,269 brand impressions, 266 clicks, 33 cart adds, and 8 purchases is statistically borderline but directionally usable. Tier 2 / Tier 3 brand events are too thin to compute rates at all.

**Notes on the Tier 1 funnel:**
- **CTR is healthy.** When Bi-Crackie shows up, buyers click at industry rate. Listing search-results-page presentation works.
- **ATC rate is the actual breakdown point.** Brand: 33 cart adds / 266 clicks = 12.41%. Industry: 28,129 / 72,981 = 38.54%. That is a 67% gap at the click-to-cart stage. Buyers who click through are bouncing off the PDP without adding to cart.
- **Purchase CVR** of 3% (8 / 266) vs industry 15.75% inherits the ATC drop, plus a small further drop at cart-to-purchase (24% vs 41% industry).

This points to **the PDP (Product Detail Page) failing to close the deal**. Most likely drivers:
1. The PDP main image (close-up of pole tip in a crack) does not show the product. Someone arriving from a search for "crack weeder" cannot quickly see what they are buying.
2. Price ($44.99) is 80-200% above short-handle alternatives on the same SERP. The differentiation (60" stand-up, Made in USA, steel) needs to be visually obvious from the gallery and bullets the moment they land. Right now the value gap is not obvious enough fast enough.

This explains why CVR is the secondary blocker behind impression share.

## Insights

- **Impression share is the primary blocker on Tier 1, but CVR is a real secondary blocker.** P0 (BICRACKIE Weeding Tool) captures 0.32% of impressions on its hero queries against a ~16-18% adjusted cap, and even when shown, the ATC rate is 12% vs 38.5% industry. The growth path is sequential: fix the main image first to lift CVR, then turn on PPC to capture impression share. Without the listing fix, scaling ad spend will burn budget on clicks that don't convert.
- **Tier 2 (broader weed puller queries) is the larger long-term prize.** P0 (BICRACKIE Weeding Tool) is currently invisible on a 184k-monthly-search market that's 15x bigger than its hero tier. The seller's $44.99 price point makes Tier 2 more competitive (cheaper short-handle alternatives dominate volume), but the "stand up + Made in USA" differentiator is the wedge. Tier 2 is the second-phase PPC opportunity once Tier 1 is performing.
- **Seasonality confirmed market-driven, not brand-driven.** Tier 1 search volume (11.6k avg) cycles from 28k in July to 1.7k in December. The seller's own revenue follows the same shape. The flat winter is the market, not Bi-Crackie. The implication: April through July is the highest-leverage 4-month window of the year, and we are entering it now.
- **The audit is happening at the right moment.** Sales rank is currently slipping in April 2026 (#1,000 to #1,200 in Manual Weeders) precisely as competitors ramp into season. Without action, the brand will miss the peak window again, the same way Aug 2025 collapsed (sales fell 75% Jul to Aug, never recovered). The peak window is now 8 to 10 weeks long, every week without action loses material revenue.
