# Essential Oils Growth Path (Backup Plan)

Prepared as a secondary narrative in case the Reed Diffuser story doesn't land on the call. Essential oils is Ecodrop's biggest product category by revenue, and the client's original "hero" framing. The story here is different from Reed Diffuser: visibility is healthy, but conversion and catalog structure are what's holding the category back.

## Why essential oils is a credible backup story

- **Biggest category in the account.** ~£180-200k/year (£15-17k/month), ~45% of total revenue. No single product comes close to this as a category.
- **~20% of account ad spend already goes here.** £1,194 over 90 days (£398/month). Not a new spend ask, it's a reallocation story.
- **Impression share is already healthy on top scents.** Unlike Reed Diffuser (0.16% impression share, collapsed Q1), essential oils sit at 1.1-1.3% impression share overall, with many individual scents already at 3-6% (near the single-product cap). The visibility problem is solved, the conversion problem isn't.

The pattern:
- **Impression share:** Already strong on Ecodrop's clearly-positioned scents (Frankincense, Neroli, Rose, Sandalwood). Only 2-3x headroom before hitting the single-product cap.
- **CTR:** Below industry on almost every query (30-80% below). Same listing-presentation problem seen on Reed Diffuser, but not the limiting factor here.
- **CVR:** **Significantly below industry on most scents, often by 20-65%.** This is the real ceiling. The listings are getting seen, people are clicking, but they're not buying as often as they do on competitors.

Per the playbook: **below-industry CVR + listing potential = listing optimization play.** Fix the PDP first (A+ content, images, bullets, ratings), then scale traffic.

### Why CVR is below industry - working hypothesis

The split from one merged parent into 20+ individual listings likely diluted review authority. Before: one parent with ~10k reviews = strong social proof. After: each individual scent has 50-500 reviews = competitors look more authoritative. Plus the category competes against well-positioned premium brands (Tisserand, Neom, DoTerra, NOW Foods) that have specialist brand authority built in.

This is a hypothesis to confirm with the seller on the call, but the data pattern fits.

## Four Growth Levers

### Lever 1: Listing CVR Fix on Top 5 Scents (biggest lever)

**Target SKUs:** B01NCVIC53 Sandalwood 10ml, B077WJ243V Rose 10ml, B08GHCJFYH Frankincense 50ml, B07L9CJCR4 Neroli, B078591PV1 Rosemary 10ml.

**Actions:**
- Strengthen A+ content with brand authority elements (sourcing story, purity claims with test certification, therapeutic use cases per scent)
- Refresh main image with trust markers (size, purity %, organic/natural callouts)
- Add comparison tables vs premium competitors (on price, size, purity)
- Add a video per top SKU showing the product and suggested uses
- Consider Brand Story module emphasising the parent brand's depth (all 20+ scents from one maker)

**Impact math (conservative):** Top 5 scents = £61,000/year. A 20% CVR lift on these = ~£12,000/year = **£1,000/month.** This compounds over time as better CVR drives BSR, which drives more organic impressions, which compounds.

### Lever 2: Rebuild B09GNS958T as the "Discovery Set" Hero

**Context:** B09GNS958T "Ecodrop Essential Oils, 100% Pure" is the former merged parent that now has only 2 remaining children. Still doing £31k/year but declining. Almost certainly carries the legacy review volume from before the split.

**Opportunity:** The "essential oils set" query gets **87k monthly search volume** with only **0.21% impression share** from Ecodrop right now. CVR on these queries is low industry-wide (5-8%), but with a well-built set listing at a fair price, Ecodrop could capture meaningful share.

**Actions:**
- Turn B09GNS958T into a flagship "Essential Oils Discovery Set" or "Starter Kit" with 6-10 of the best scents
- Use the legacy review count as the anchor for social proof
- Dedicated Sponsored Product campaigns on "essential oils set", "essential oil set", "essential oil gift set", "essential oil starter kit"
- Higher AOV (£25-40 vs £8-15 for individual scents) improves overall account AOV

**Impact math:** "Essential oils set" market = 87k SV × ~5% CVR × £30 AOV = £130k/month market. Capturing even 2-3% = **£2,600-3,900/month in incremental revenue.** Biggest single lever in the category if executed well.

### Lever 4: Competitor Conquest via Product Targeting

**Context:** Ecodrop can't bid on competitor brand names in keyword search (Amazon blocks it), but can run **Product Targeting** on competitor ASINs (Neom, Tisserand, NOW Foods, DoTerra). This places Ecodrop's ads on competitor PDPs.

**Why this works here specifically:** Ecodrop's price point (£7-15) is significantly below premium brands (£20-50+). A price-sensitive buyer on a Neom PDP is a real conversion opportunity.

**Actions:**
- Identify top 10 competitor ASINs in each scent Ecodrop sells (Neom Lavender, Tisserand Frankincense, etc.)
- Build Sponsored Product Product Targeting campaigns per scent, targeting those ASINs
- Keep budget modest (£2-5/day per scent) and ROAS threshold tight (≥3.0)

**Impact:** Smaller than Levers 1-3 but scales with discipline. Estimated **+£200-400/month** over 2-3 months.

## What Makes This a Good Backup on the Call

1. **Emotionally aligned with the client's framing.** They came in calling essential oils the hero. If the Reed Diffuser pivot feels like a rug-pull, this is the fallback that honors their original instinct while still applying data.
2. **Less dependent on one fix.** Reed Diffuser's whole story hangs on recovering impression share. Essential oils has four independent levers, each small, all additive.
3. **Clear path for MerchantBots engagement.** The A+ refresh on 5 SKUs + the Discovery Set build is exactly the kind of full-stack work (listing + PPC + portfolio strategy) that justifies the MB engagement model.
4. **Complements, doesn't contradict, Reed Diffuser plan.** If the client wants both, the plans run in parallel. If they reject one, the other stands.

## Open Questions for the Seller (if the call pivots here)

- **Was B09GNS958T the merged parent with ~10k reviews before the split?** Confirming this unlocks the Discovery Set play. If the reviews aren't there anymore, the lever is smaller.
- **How do current ratings per individual scent compare to pre-split review volumes?** We have current star ratings but not pre-split review counts. This confirms the CVR hypothesis.
- **Is there appetite for re-merging the oils back into one parent with size/scent variations?** This is the inverse of what was done. Risky, but if executed it could be the single biggest move. Not a Month 1 recommendation, but worth flagging as a Month 3-4 option if individual-listing CVR stays suppressed.
- **Which scents are best-sellers on external channels (DTC, retail, wholesale)?** If there are scents that sell well elsewhere but have low impression share on Amazon, they should be prioritised in the PPC scaling.

## Critical Caveat

Essential oils is a **CVR-led growth path**, which means the lift is slower than Reed Diffuser's impression-share fix. PPC can't force this one; the listings have to get better. Expect the first 2-4 weeks to be investment (listing work, set launch) with revenue lift showing up in Weeks 4-8. The Reed Diffuser plan produces results faster because the fix is a bid/budget reallocation on hero keywords — instant effect.

If the client is time-sensitive to hit £60k, Reed Diffuser is the faster path. Essential oils is the deeper path.
