## Merge Feasibility Assessment

### Compatibility Check (Amazon's technical requirements)

| Requirement | Status |
|---|---|
| Same brand | ✅ Both "ecodrop essential oils" |
| Same manufacturer | ✅ Both "ecodrop essential oils" |
| Same marketplace | ✅ Both UK |
| Same Keepa product type | ✅ Both AROMA_DIFFUSER |
| Compatible variation theme (Size + Scent supported in Home Fragrance) | ✅ Yes |
| No scent conflicts | ✅ No direct scent duplication between 100ml and 250ml |
| Variation axes currently consistent | ⚠️ 100ml uses "Size + Color", 250ml uses "Set/Refill + Scent". Re-mapping needed before merge. |
| Review scores compatible | ⚠️ 250ml Orange Blossom is 2.6 stars, would drag parent average down |

**Verdict: Technically feasible.** Amazon supports SizeName-ScentName variation themes in Home Fragrance. Merge via Seller Central "Variation Wizard" or a bulk flat-file update with a unified variation theme.

### Strategic Considerations

**Arguments FOR merging:**

- The 250ml is a fresh launch (£0 all of 2025, first meaningful sales in Mar 2026). Merging under the 100ml parent would immediately give it the halo of 2.5 years of reviews and established BSR.
- Consolidated listing is easier to advertise. One ad campaign with more SKU coverage = better budget efficiency vs. running separate campaigns.
- Matches the client's preferred structure. They already had a merged essential-oil parent in the past; a merged diffuser parent is a familiar model.
- The client already built A+ content for the 100ml. A merged parent inherits that A+ for the 250ml children without re-creating everything.

**Arguments AGAINST merging:**

- **Positioning mismatch:** The 250ml parent title explicitly says "Luxury Aromatherapy Diffuser" and lists premium-leaning scents (White Jasmine, Amber Allure, English Pear & Freesia, Tobacco & Vanilla). The 100ml positions as "Home Spa Gift" with mass-market scents (Bergamot, Lavender, Wood Sage). Merging commoditises the 250ml.
- **Ratings contamination risk:** B0G362BD89 (Orange Blossom 250ml Set) at 2.6 stars would pull the parent's average down if rolled in. Recommend fixing its reviews first (or excluding it from the merge) before any merge happens.
- **Price-point confusion:** 250ml will be priced higher than 100ml. Customer lands on the merged listing and sees two price tiers. Main image selection becomes tricky (which bottle do you show by default?).
- **Recent "split" fatigue:** The client just went through an essential-oils "merged to split" transition that disrupted the business. Doing the reverse (split to merged) on diffusers is a big operational move and carries review/rank risk during the changeover.
- **Variation theme rework:** Both parents currently use inconsistent variation axes. Merging requires re-mapping all 30 children onto a unified "Size + Scent + Format (Set/Refill)" structure. A+ content and images also need adjustment.

### Recommendation

**Don't merge, at least not yet.** Reasons:
1. The 100ml parent is already the top revenue driver in the account and is declining sharply (down 56% Q4 → Q1). The priority is fixing the 100ml's own demand problem before restructuring it.
2. The 250ml's growth is accelerating (£0 → £1.8k in Mar alone). Its own merits are proving out; merging prematurely undercuts its premium positioning.
3. Review risk: at least one 250ml SKU (Orange Blossom) has a 2.6-star rating that would leak into the 100ml parent's review pool.
4. The stated goal (inheriting A+ content) can be achieved faster by building 250ml A+ content directly, which the client was going to do anyway.

**Instead, recommend:**
- Build dedicated A+ content for B0GTWLTMFH (already in the plan)
- Cross-link via Sponsored Display Product Targeting (show 250ml ads on the 100ml PDP, target 100ml buyers who want longer-lasting scent)
- Run Virtual Product Bundles combining a 100ml starter with a 250ml refill of the same scent family (where scents align)
- Use Brand Story + Brand Store to position 250ml as the premium tier
- Revisit the merge in Month 3-4 if the 250ml plateaus and the 100ml has recovered — at that point the positioning/rating risks are easier to manage.

**Final call:** Do not merge in the current plan. Defer the decision to the seller as a question in the final output. If they still want to merge, do it in Month 3-4 after A+ is built on the 250ml and the 2.6-star SKU is fixed, and plan for a 2-week rank-dip contingency.
